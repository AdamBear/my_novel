# Chapter 45: First Lesson of the Wasteland

## Section 1: Stripping the Filter

The first second Lin Yi truly stepped onto this land, he understood how "merciful" the system had been.

In the Infinite Trial's virtual instances, whether it was the extreme cold of "Eternal Night Train" or the bottom-layer sewage pipes of "Perfect City," even when the system's "sensory simulation" was set to 100%, it still had some bottom-layer protection mechanism—for example, as long as stamina didn't reach zero, you could always run; no matter how bad the air, you wouldn't actually get pneumoconiosis.

But now, all filters had been brutally torn away.

Violent wind mixed with coarse, slightly radioactive sand was like countless rusty files, mercilessly scraping Lin Yi's exposed skin. Each breath carried intense sulfur smell, heavy metal dust stench, and the rotting stench of old civilization. These deadly particles surged through his trachea into his alveoli, bringing a tearing sensation like swallowing broken glass.

"Cough, cough, cough..."

Lin Yi coughed violently, spitting out blood-tinged phlegm with black ash.

He carried the unconscious Su Liang, trudging through the sky-filling yellow sand with uneven steps. A hundred pounds might be nothing on flat ground, but in this soft sand where each step sank past the ankle, it was like carrying a mountain.

Without the stamina bar lock, carbon-based biological physiological limits were frantically torturing his nerves. Thigh muscles spasmed from overuse. Lactic acid buildup brought wave after wave of soreness. His vision began blurring. Besides wind sounds, only his own bellows-like heavy breathing filled his ears.

## Section 2: Flesh Furnace

"Put me... down..."

Su Liang on his back let out an extremely weak murmur.

The physical tube removal in reality, plus brain nerves nearly burned out from navigating for Lin Yi in Prison Zero, left her current state weaker than a candle in the wind. Jolting and cold briefly restored a trace of consciousness. She could feel Lin Yi's body trembling violently—a sign of severe stamina depletion.

"Shut up. Save your strength to breathe." Lin Yi's voice was so hoarse it was barely recognizable.

"In reality... no points to heal..." Su Liang's chin rested on Lin Yi's shoulder, voice intermittent. "Carrying me... we'll both die here..."

"You think I want to carry you?"

Lin Yi gritted his teeth, forcibly hoisting the slipping Su Liang higher. "Principal 300, compound interest 50. Before this bad debt is settled, you want to die? Dream on."

He stopped talking, because each extra word wasted precious oxygen and heat.

If it were an ordinary person, with such severe injuries and carrying weight in such extremely harsh conditions, they would have collapsed from heart failure long ago.

But Lin Yi didn't collapse.

The [Nirvana] gene in his body, after breaking free from data suppression, had completely taken root in his physical carbon-based flesh. This ancient, violent power was now using Lin Yi's body as a furnace.

It frantically extracted Lin Yi's remaining fat and energy, forcibly stimulating cell division and metabolism. Torn muscle fibers were brutally reconnected. Damaged organs forcibly operated at high temperature. This was a life-sustaining method like drinking poison to quench thirst. Though it gave him superhuman endurance, it also raised his body temperature to a terrifying level.

Su Liang, pressed against Lin Yi's back, felt like she was leaning against a scalding furnace.

## Section 3: Gaze of the Giant Beast

Lin Yi turned to look behind.

Deep in the sandstorm, Ark One's massive metal dome still stood like an insurmountable black mountain range. Though the distance had opened several kilometers, that piercing air raid siren still faintly carried on the wind.

Not only that, several thick, highly penetrating high-power searchlight beams were like massive light swords, sweeping back and forth through the dim yellow sandstorm.

Ark's guard forces would absolutely not let two awakened ones escape like this. In this wetware processing factory, every human with the [Nirvana] gene was an extremely precious processing resource, let alone that they had blown up D-zone's explosion-proof door.

"Must find cover... can't be a target in open ground..."

Lin Yi's gaze turned forward.

About a kilometer ahead, a massive shadow vaguely stood. That was the ruins of an old-era human city. Countless tilted, broken skyscrapers were like massive tombstones, interlocking to form a maze of steel and concrete.

Once inside those ruins, Ark's searchlights and thermal imaging radar would be greatly reduced in effectiveness.

"Hold on... almost there."

Lin Yi withdrew his gaze, hung the pulse rifle seized from guards on his chest, reverse-gripped the bulletproof glass shard in his left hand, extracted his body's last bit of potential, and sprinted toward that steel graveyard.

## Section 4: Tremor of the Earth

Twenty minutes later.

Lin Yi finally carried Su Liang into a commercial building ruin with only half remaining. The building's bottom layer was half-buried by yellow sand. The once-smooth marble floor was covered with dried moss and unknown creature bones.

Once inside the building, the wind force that could blow people away instantly weakened considerably.

Lin Yi leaned against a thick load-bearing pillar. His legs gave out, unable to support him anymore. He slid down the pillar to sit on the ground. He carefully placed Su Liang in a relatively clean corner, then collapsed backward, greedily gulping the slightly calmer air here.

"Made it out... really made it out..."

Lin Yi looked at the riddled ceiling overhead, listening to the howling wind outside. His lips slowly curved into an exhausted yet wild smile.

No system, no Observers, no those lofty AI.

This was humanity's cruel yet real wasteland.

However, just as he prepared to close his eyes and recover some stamina.

The black metal brand on his left palm—[Fragment of Reality]—suddenly transmitted an extremely intense, scalding pain like being burned by boiling water!

This pain was fiercer than any warning in the virtual world, even causing Lin Yi's arm to spasm.

*Thud...*

The yellow sand on the ground jumped slightly.

*Thud... thud...*

This wasn't footsteps. This was an impact sound so heavy it seemed capable of crushing the earth's crust. As the sound approached, the entire building's wreckage began shedding dust and rubble. Even the outside wind seemed cut off by this massive oppressive presence.

The exhaustion in Lin Yi's eyes was instantly dispelled, pupils contracting sharply.

He suddenly rolled up, covering Su Liang's mouth as she let out an unconscious moan, pressing his body tightly into the shadows, looking out through the building's shattered glass curtain wall.

In the sky-filling yellow sand, a steel silhouette dozens of meters tall was slowly crushing over the sand dunes they had just crossed, approaching these ruins.
