# Chapter 74: Blood-Stained Defense Line

## Section 1: Screams Behind the Door

Black Water Bar's VIP booth door was tightly closed, but that soundproof heavy wooden door still couldn't completely block the suppressed screams from inside.

Inside the booth, medic Old Ghost was using the silver sterile medical case and micron-level probes seized from Iron Blood Mercenary Corps' vault to perform an extremely dangerous nerve surgery on Su Liang. Due to lack of high-strength anesthetics, this kind of severe pain from directly cutting open the periosteum and forcibly inserting probes into brain nerves was enough to drive a person's sanity completely insane. Lin Yi strode forward, hands like iron clamps pressing firmly on Su Liang's shoulders to prevent her from struggling due to inhuman pain, using cold promises to support her not to go insane.

In the bar hall, those thirty personal guard recruits just promoted by Lin Yi gripped tightly the old-era assault rifles just distributed to them. These people had been frozen like pork in Ark's hibernation pods for a full half-century, only awakened when the system collapsed, still carrying the fragility of old-era greenhouses in their bones. Hearing that scalp-numbing sound from the booth, this group of former "civilized people" were all pale-faced, legs uncontrollably trembling slightly.

Refugee leader Old Zhao swallowed hard, feeling chills down his back. He glanced at Ranger Falcon beside him methodically wiping the heavy machine gun, eyes full of wariness toward Lin Yi's absolute violent methods.

The Ranger boss dragged his mechanical left arm over. His single eye swept over everyone in the dim light, warning them never to treat Lin Yi's just-promulgated "decimation law" as a joke. Fear—extreme fear of the internal dictator—was gradually squeezing out this group of recruits' cowardice toward the external world.

## Section 2: Mutated Fanatics

Just then, a crisp "ding" suddenly came from the outpost outside the bar—the sound of the perimeter warning device being triggered.

On the mid-level hub platform outside, over a dozen swaying black shadows had somehow emerged from the pit's dimness. They wore tattered miner's clothing, walking with extremely strange postures. Some had half their faces covered with gray-white mushroom-like fungal spores.

"Praise aberration... flesh is weak... corruption ascends..." They approached with twisted steps while mumbling incomprehensible, scalp-numbing eerie sutras.

The Ranger boss immediately recognized these monsters, face changing drastically. This was one of Black Rock City's three major local snakes—the "Corruption Cathedral," a group of cult lunatics who worshipped wasteland aberrations, treating nuclear radiation and spore viruses as divine miracles. These fanatics were obviously attracted by the bloody smell from slaughtering mercenaries in the bar earlier. They were infected with terrible mutant spores from the wasteland and had no pain sensation at all.

Falcon roared preparing to fire, but the Ranger boss's mechanical arm suddenly pressed down her gun barrel. He used his chin to point at those recruits behind trembling like quails, indicating this was an excellent touchstone Lin Yi used to screen "wolves."

## Section 3: No Retreat

Over a dozen spore-infected fanatics let out inhuman shrieks, charging like crazed zombies over the sandbag cover, directly pouncing toward Black Water Bar's broken door!

Old Zhao watched that group of hideous monsters rush up, mind going blank. Fear's instinct made him unconsciously step back. Not just him—several recruits in the front line were so scared they forgot to pull triggers, turning to run toward the bar's depths.

Bang!

A deafening gunshot. The Ranger boss's large-caliber revolver directly blasted a scorched crater in the wooden floor at Old Zhao's feet.

"Where are you running?!" The boss roared like a demon, single eye staring fixedly at them. "Forgot the rules inside?! 'Decimation law'! Anyone who dares retreat one step, tonight out of you thirty people, three will be beaten to death by remaining teammates with rocks!"

This most brutal collective punishment military law from ancient Rome, like a heavy hammer, viciously smashed into the recruits' souls. It not only locked off retreat with extreme fear, but also bound each person's life to their teammates'. Retreat meant not only your own death, but being beaten to death by companions.

"Fuck it! Fight them!"

Cornered Old Zhao's eyes turned red. He suddenly turned around, leveling that assault rifle, aiming at the fanatic charging closest, firmly pulling the trigger.

Rat-tat-tat-tat—!!!

The roar of powder weapons instantly exploded. A string of 7.62mm armor-piercing rounds screamed out, punching a row of bloody holes in that fanatic's chest. Dark green bodily fluid sprayed out. Though the fanatic had no pain sensation, the physical level's powerful kinetic energy still heavily knocked him to the ground.

## Section 4: Breaking the Cocoon Into Wolves

This first shot was like a signal gun breaking fear.

The remaining twenty-nine recruits, under the dual pressure of death and military law, finally overcame their inherent weakness. They lay prone on the broken bar counter, relying on door frames, frantically pouring out firepower. Gunshots, firelight, the crisp sound of shell casings hitting the floor wove into a cruel symphony.

The dozen-plus charging fanatics were riddled with bullets, severed limbs scattered everywhere. When the last fanatic's head was simultaneously blasted apart by several shotguns, the bar exterior finally returned to deathly silence.

Old Zhao and the recruits gasped heavily, looking at the corpses outside the door. An unprecedented wild joy mixed with killing pleasure and survival relief quietly grew in their hearts.

Creak—

Just then, that heavy wooden door of the VIP booth deep in the bar slowly opened.

Lin Yi walked out from inside. His tactical vest was already soaked with sweat. Those originally fair hands were now covered with glaring blood—blood marks left when Su Liang, in severe pain, scratched his arms with her nails while he firmly held her down.

The heart-palpitating screams from the booth had subsided. Su Liang finally forcibly endured that physical bionic modification without anesthesia.

Lin Yi walked to the bar counter, casually grabbing a rag to wipe blood off his hands. Those deep pitch-black eyes coldly swept over the fanatic corpses outside the door, finally landing on these thirty gasping, blood-soaked recruits. Just as he said, on the wasteland nature doesn't feed waste—food is seized, lives are killed for.

He saw the cowardice fading from the recruits' eyes, and the wildness growing.

"Well done." Lin Yi casually tossed the blood-stained rag on the ground, corner of his mouth curling into a satisfied cold smile. "From the moment you fired that first shot, you greenhouse pigs finally grew wolf fangs."
