# Chapter 65: Selection of Flesh and Blood

## Section 1: Beasts in the Ruins

Ark One's wreckage was burning violently.

Having lost the mainframe's physical cooling control, the bottom reactor's waste heat was transforming this once-impregnable steel fortress into a massive incinerator. Billowing smoke rose like black pillars, piercing straight into the wasteland's dark red sky.

At the ruins' entrance, the most primitive, bloodiest scene in human history was unfolding.

"Give it to me! I got this water first!"

"Get lost! This bulletproof vest is mine!"

Tens of thousands of naked "frozen meat," fighting over supplies from several Ark guard corpses on the ground, completely descended into madness. Civilization's disguise was torn to shreds. A university professor who had been refined and courteous in reality now frantically smashed a sharp rock against the head of a young man trying to snatch half a tube of nutrient paste from his hands; several seemingly frail women, like hyenas, bit down hard on a water pipe pulled from the ruins, gulping down the water even though it was mixed with industrial waste oil.

No magic, no skills, only fingernails, teeth, and the most primitive blunt instruments.

Lin Yi stood on a towering armor fragment, coldly overlooking it all.

The high temperature from breaking through [Nirvana] second stage had gradually faded from his body surface. His bones became more dense, his skin surface seemingly covered with an invisible tough membrane. The high-intensity ultraviolet rays and trace radioactive dust on the wasteland that could ulcerate ordinary people's skin fell on him like a gentle breeze, leaving no trace.

"How's your hearing recovery?" Lin Yi didn't turn his head, asking Su Liang standing behind him flatly.

"Still usable. But the wind sounds and mechanical explosion sounds here are too chaotic." Su Liang closed her eyes, brow slightly furrowed. "However... I can hear heartbeats rapidly decreasing. In just these twenty minutes, at least two thousand people died inside."

"Not enough. More will die."

Lin Yi's eyes were like a stagnant pool. He didn't need sheep—he needed wolves that could bite on the wasteland. If these millions of frozen meat didn't undergo this most brutal flesh-and-blood selection, even if brought along, they would drag him down someday in the future.

## Section 2: First Batch of Hounds

An hour later.

The screams at the ruins entrance gradually subsided. The survivors completed the first round of resource distribution—at the cost of lives.

Through the smoke, a group staggered out.

Leading them was a blood-covered man with a center-parted hairstyle. If Lin Yi remembered correctly, when this man woke from his hibernation pod, he had cried out looking for his luxury car and villa.

But now, his eyes had changed.

He wore a tattered white exoskeleton chest plate forcibly stripped from an Ark guard. In his hands he gripped tightly a pulse rifle without bullets—though bulletless, that heavy metal gun body was an extremely intimidating blunt weapon.

Behind him followed about four to five hundred people. Without exception, all were injured, holding synthetic starch, filter water bottles, or sharp metal fragments scavenged from the ruins.

As for the remaining millions, they either died in the trampling and brawls just now, or hid deep in the ruins waiting to die out of fear.

These four to five hundred were the first batch of "hounds" who survived this great purge.

The center-parted man led this group to the bottom of the armor plate where Lin Yi stood, stopping.

They looked up at Lin Yi standing high above. Though Lin Yi wore only tattered hospital clothes, his intimidating power from firing three shots in the cryogenic storage, plus the Ranger off-road vehicle with mounted heavy machine gun behind him, still made this group of newly bloodied thugs wary.

"We got the stuff."

The center-parted man raised his head, wiping the corner of his mouth with his blood-stained hand, eyes showing both the ferocity of having just killed and a probing quality. "Brother, you have guns and vehicles. We have over four hundred brothers willing to fight to the death. Take us with you, we'll make you boss."

These words sounded like surrender, but secretly applied pressure with numbers. They were testing Lin Yi's bottom line.

## Section 3: Tyrant's Prototype

Lin Yi smiled.

He didn't draw his gun, but like a lazy yet lethal panther, lightly jumped down from the three-meter-high armor plate.

Thud.

His feet landed without even bending his knees. The terrifying bone density and muscle shock absorption ability granted by [Nirvana] second stage let him ignore this level of impact.

Lin Yi walked to the center-parted man. The two were less than half a meter apart.

"You think you're qualified to negotiate with me?" Lin Yi's voice was light, yet carried a bone-chilling coldness.

"We have numbers." The center-parted man gripped his rifle barrel tightly, forcing himself not to retreat. "On this wasteland, numbers are power. No matter how good you are at fighting, can you kill all several hundred of us?"

Lin Yi didn't answer.

He suddenly extended his left hand.

No one saw his movement clearly. Only a teeth-grinding metallic twisting sound—crack!

Lin Yi's left hand bearing the black brand actually forcibly gripped the alloy barrel of the pulse rifle in the center-parted man's hands.

Under everyone's horrified gaze, that barrel forged from high-strength tungsten steel that even bullets couldn't penetrate was crushed severely dented and deformed by Lin Yi's single hand like a fragile plastic tube!

The center-parted man screamed in terror, released his grip, and fell backward onto the ground.

"Numbers are indeed power. But before absolute violence, numbers are just more fertilizer."

Lin Yi casually tossed the rifle crushed into scrap metal onto the ground with a dull thud. His cold gaze swept over these four to five hundred trembling survivors before him.

"Listen carefully. The outside world has no system panels, no health points, no customer service."

"From now on, I don't need your votes or your suggestions. My rules are the system commands on this wasteland."

"Those who obey, I guarantee you'll have water to drink and meat to eat." Lin Yi pointed at the center-parted man on the ground. "Those who dare use numbers to negotiate with me, I'll personally crush their bones into the same shape as this gun."

"Understood?"

Deathly silence.

A few seconds later, someone took the lead, and these four to five hundred thugs who had just been fierce and menacing uniformly lowered their heads, offering their most primitive submission to this tyrant who demonstrated absolute dominance in the new world.

## Section 4: Toward the Wild Sands

The Ranger boss sitting in the off-road vehicle driver's seat watched the entire process through the rearview mirror.

On his face covered with burn scars, an extremely complex expression appeared. Shock, wariness, but more so a kind of fervor.

"Falcon." The boss lowered his voice, mechanical arm rubbing the steering wheel. "We may have really picked up a monster. This kid isn't just a top-tier killer—he was born to rule the wasteland."

Falcon bit an unlit cigarette, nodding. "He doesn't talk about morality, only survival. This kind of person might have been executed in the old era, but today... he might really be able to lead humanity to survive in this hell."

Lin Yi finished handling these survivors and turned back to the off-road vehicle.

"Our Ash Camp has been bombed, where do we go now?" Lin Yi jumped onto the truck bed, looking at the boss.

"To 'Black Rock City.'"

The boss started the engine, the dull roar sounding again. "That's the Rangers' largest main city in this sector, also the nearest super-large survivor settlement. Though it's a mixed bag with warlords carving up territory, as long as you have enough supplies and weapons, you can establish yourself there."

"Warlords carving up territory?" The corner of Lin Yi's mouth curved into a playful arc. "Sounds like a good place to seize resources."

"Let's go."

Lin Yi patted the vehicle roof.

The convoy roared to life, treads and tires kicking up wild sands, driving toward the boundless wasteland depths.

Behind the convoy, those four to five hundred members of the "First Legion" recruited by Lin Yi, wearing tattered armor, followed closely in the convoy's dust cloud. They were like a pack of tamed wolves—though exhausted, their eyes had ignited the flame of survival.

Lin Yi stood in the truck bed, facing the gale mixed with radioactive sand.

He looked back at the completely burned Ark One dome. That greenhouse that had imprisoned them for fifty years finally turned to historical embers on this desolate land.

The virtual trials had ended.

But Lin Yi's wasteland rampage had only just begun.
