# Chapter 44: Escape from Ark One

## Section 1: Physical Laws

Heavy, orderly metal footsteps echoed through the wetware processing factory's circular corridor. Six fully armed Ark guards carrying electromagnetic pulse rifles approached D-14 zone in tactical formation. They were semi-mechanical cyborgs—no pain sensation, no emotions, their retinas flickering with thermal imaging scan spots.

"Targets located. Two awakened bodies. Execute elimination."

The lead guard issued a cold command through the communication channel. The dark gun muzzles instantly locked onto Lin Yi standing in the broken glass pile. No warning, no warning shot—the trigger was pulled directly.

*Whoosh—!*

A pulse round with high-voltage arcs grazed Lin Yi's cheek, shattering another cultivation chamber behind him. Pink nutrient solution poured down like a waterfall.

If this were in the virtual trials, Lin Yi would now hear the system's frantic alarms or see a red MISS float above his head. But now, there was only death brushing past, real enough to suffocate. That scorching air even singed his hair.

No system panel, but the [Nirvana] gene's awakening in his physical body was far more violent than a pile of cold data. Lin Yi felt the entire world's operation slow down in his eyes. That was bullet time brought by surging adrenaline and neural overload.

He suddenly crouched low, like a cheetah flying close to the ground. Using the pouring nutrient solution and broken glass as cover, he didn't retreat but advanced, charging straight at that six-man squad!

"Fire!"

Dense pulse barrages wove into a death net. Lin Yi made extremely biomechanically defying evasive movements in the bullet rain. His muscle fibers were as tough as steel cables. Each push off the ground left a dent in the metal floor. Thirty meters distance—he covered it in less than three seconds.

When he rushed before the lead guard, the opponent's gun muzzle hadn't even had time to lower.

"Didn't the system teach you not to fight assassins in close combat?"

A bloodthirsty crimson flashed in Lin Yi's eyes. His left hand swept aside the opponent's rifle. His right hand gripped that thick, sharp bulletproof glass shard and thrust upward from below, along the gap between the guard's helmet and neck armor!

*Squelch!*

With a teeth-grinding friction sound, the glass shard directly severed the guard's carotid artery and mechanical nerve bundle. Real blood mixed with black insulating oil gushed out, splashing Lin Yi's face.

## Section 2: Seizing Weapons and Breaking Through

Lin Yi didn't pause. He kicked away the convulsing guard's corpse and seized that heavy electromagnetic pulse rifle. The gun was extremely heavy in hand, with no system auto-calibration or sight assistance—completely relying on physical feel.

But his genetically enhanced muscles instantly adapted to this weight.

*Bang! Bang! Bang!*

Lin Yi held the rifle one-handed, stock braced under his ribs, firing three shots in succession. Blue electromagnetic pulses exploded in the narrow corridor, precisely piercing the chest power cores of three guards behind. Sparks flew as three semi-mechanical bodies fell smoking.

The remaining two guards tried to find cover, but Lin Yi was faster. The black brand on his left palm suddenly transmitted a scalding throb. An extremely keen intuition guided his movements. He blind-fired one shot behind the load-bearing pillar to his left with eyes closed. A scream immediately followed. The last guard had just tried to call for support when Lin Yi was already before him, reverse-gripping the barrel and viciously smashing the opponent's gas mask with the heavy metal stock.

In less than twenty seconds, an elite six-man patrol squad was completely wiped out.

"Huff..." Lin Yi spat out bloody saliva, quickly scavenging several spare magazines from the corpses and hanging them at his waist. He turned and ran back to the broken glass pile, lifting the still deeply comatose Su Liang.

No storage space, no backpack. Lin Yi tore tactical straps from a guard's body and tied Su Liang tightly to his back. A hundred pounds of weight pressed on him, yet for the current Lin Yi it was like wearing a light bulletproof vest.

"Su Liang, hold on tight. Though you can't hear, the road ahead might be a bit bumpy."

Lin Yi hefted the pulse rifle in his hand, looking up at the massive gate flashing red alarm lights at the corridor's end. That was the wetware processing factory's exit, and the only path to Ark's surface layer.

Piercing alarm sounds had already echoed throughout the underground base. More metal footsteps were converging from all directions.

"Come on." Lin Yi's lips curved into a fervent cold smile. "Let me see if the real world's monsters can take a beating."

## Section 3: Steel and Blood

This was a breakout path paved with flesh and engine oil.

Lin Yi, carrying Su Liang on his back, transformed into a cold killing machine in the massive processing factory. No flashy skills, no cooldown times—only the purest violent aesthetics.

He shuttled between dense cultivation chambers, using those massive glass columns as cover. A guard group of twelve fully mechanical hounds flanked from the left wing. These hounds were extremely similar to those in the fourth instance "Eternal Night Train," but faster.

Lin Yi didn't dodge. The black brand on his left hand pulsated frantically. The instant the hounds pounced, he precisely predicted their trajectories. He shot out the nutrient solution delivery pipe overhead. High-pressure spraying viscous liquid instantly blinded the mechanical hounds' visual sensors. Then, like a ghost, he cut into the pack. The gun barrel spat blue tongues of fire, blasting those mechanical heads one by one.

"Warning! D-zone defense line breached! Target moving toward central cargo passage!"

The electronic voice in the broadcast changed from cold to frantic.

Lin Yi was covered in blood—enemies' and his own from grazing wounds. Real physical combat consumed massive stamina. His lungs pulled like bellows. Each breath carried intense bloody smell. But the [Nirvana] gene in his body greedily absorbed this fatigue and pain, converting them into endless strength.

He fought his way to the massive cargo passage.

Blocking their path was a half-meter-thick explosion-proof alloy door, and two heavy defense mechs guarding the door. They carried micro-missile launchers on their shoulders. The dark Gatling gun barrels had already begun rotating and warming up.

This level of firepower—flesh and blood couldn't withstand it.

"No way out..." Su Liang on Lin Yi's back moved slightly, seemingly regaining a trace of consciousness from the vibrations. She weakly murmured by Lin Yi's ear.

"In this world, roads are blasted open."

Lin Yi's gaze locked onto the two thick hydraulic cooling pipes marked with danger symbols on both sides of the explosion-proof door. Those were the physical power source supporting the door's opening and closing.

He didn't shoot at the mechs but suddenly slid forward. The instant the Gatling guns spat fire, he hurled his pulse rifle with only two rounds remaining viciously at the left cooling pipe!

Simultaneously, the black brand on his left palm erupted with blinding red light. He punched the ground, using the reaction force to roll backward frantically while holding Su Liang.

## Section 4: Wind of the Wasteland

*Boom—!!!*

The pulse rifle detonated on impact with the cooling pipe. High-pressure coolant met pulse arcs, triggering an extremely terrifying chain explosion.

A massive fireball instantly swallowed the two heavy mechs. The half-meter-thick explosion-proof alloy door twisted and deformed in the violent explosion, finally letting out an overburdened wail and crashing outward.

The violent blast wave violently threw Lin Yi and Su Liang out.

Lin Yi forcibly twisted his body in midair, landing on his back, protecting Su Liang tightly in his arms. He slid over ten meters on the cold metal floor, crashing heavily into an abandoned load-bearing pillar.

"Cough, cough..." Lin Yi spat blood, feeling his internal organs had shifted. But he didn't stop, struggling up from the ground and looking at that massive breach blown open.

Outside there were no fluorescent lights, no nutrient tanks, no cold steel corridors.

Beyond the breach was a dim yellow, boundless world.

Violent wind howled into the processing factory—real natural wind, carrying coarse sand, pungent radiation smell, and the decaying breath of old civilization, slapping Lin Yi's face hard.

Lin Yi, carrying Su Liang, walked step by step out of that twisted gate.

When he finally stepped onto that dry, cracked earth outside, he raised his head.

Overhead was no virtual blue sky, only a dark red firmament obscured by thick volcanic ash. At the horizon's end were countless tilted, collapsed skyscraper ruins, like massive tombstones silently narrating humanity's twilight.

And in that sky-filling yellow sand, one could vaguely see several mountain-sized mechanical beasts slowly moving, emitting earth-shaking roars.

"This is... reality."

Lin Yi looked at this devastated wasteland, breathing deeply this dirty, radiation-laden air. His lips slowly spread into a relieved yet defiant wild laugh.

He had finally crawled out of that virtual hell where he was treated as a battery and spare part. Even if the real hell was before his eyes, it was humanity's hell.

"I... made it out."

He carried Su Liang, stepping forward into the depths of that sky-filling yellow sand wasteland.
