# Chapter 52: ICU Rainy Night

## Section 1: Overlapping Shadows

The air in Ash Camp's medical area was foul and oppressive.

Lin Yi slumped in front of that bed cobbled together from ammunition boxes, gaze locked onto that old scar below Su Liang's right collarbone. That pale pink, irregularly edged knife wound was like a key, brutally unlocking the memory gates in his mind that had been frozen for half a century.

Outside the bomb shelter, the wasteland's violent wind was howling, making sounds like a beast's whimper.

But this wind sound, in Lin Yi's ears, gradually became moist and dense, finally transforming into a torrential downpour.

The dim yellow incandescent light began flickering. The surrounding rusty tin walls and blood-stained gauze began distorting and fading like water ripples.

When his vision focused again, Lin Yi found he was no longer in that cruel underground camp.

He had returned to fifty years ago.

Back to that late night in the summer of 202X.

The nauseating wasteland radiation smell in the air disappeared, replaced by the hospital corridor's strong, pungent smell of lysol and disinfectant.

Nineteen-year-old Lin Yi, wearing a cheap T-shirt half-soaked by rain, leaned weakly against the cold tile wall outside the ICU (Intensive Care Unit). His hair was messily plastered to his forehead, water droplets sliding down his pale cheeks.

In his hands, he clutched a thick stack of payment demand notices, somewhat wrinkled from sweat and rain.

"Lin Yi's family member, this is today's expense list. Please go to the first-floor lobby as soon as possible to settle the outstanding fees. If not settled, tomorrow's targeted medication can't be prescribed."

A nurse wearing a mask, eyes showing fatigue and bureaucratic detachment, handed him the latest notice.

Lin Yi lowered his head, looking at the string of numbers at the bottom of that notice.

It was an astronomical figure sufficient to completely crush a young person who had lost both parents and was only a college freshman.

## Section 2: Weight of Despair

"Nurse..."

Nineteen-year-old Lin Yi's voice still lacked the coldness and ruthlessness later forged in Infinite Trial. At this moment, he was just a teenager driven to desperation by life, spine nearly broken by the weight.

He almost pleadingly grabbed the nurse's sleeve. "Can you give me three more days? I just listed our old family house with an agent. As soon as it sells, I'll have money! Please, please don't stop my sister's medication. She can't be without that medicine..."

The nurse sighed, gently pulling her hand free.

"Lin Yi, I sympathize with your sister's situation too. But the hospital has its rules. ICU daily expenses are over ten thousand. Your account is already overdrawn by more than fifty thousand. If the director hadn't signed a guarantee, the machines should have been stopped the day before yesterday."

A trace of reluctance flashed in the nurse's eyes, but more was helplessness. "Go borrow some. Relatives, friends, whatever method. Before midnight tonight, there must be money in the account."

The nurse left.

Only Lin Yi remained in the corridor, along with the overhead fluorescent light emitting a "buzz" of electric current.

Lin Yi slumped dejectedly onto the cold floor.

Borrow?

Relatives who could lend had already blocked him. So-called "friends" avoided him like the plague when they heard the three letters ICU. He had even gone to underground gambling dens to find loan sharks, but they saw he was a poor student with no background and wouldn't even give him a high-interest loan.

He looked through the ICU's thick glass window at the inside.

On the bed, that girl as thin as a fallen leaf was connected to tubes all over her body. The respirator mask covered most of her face. She was his only family in this world.

"Is there... really no way out?"

Lin Yi buried his face deeply between his knees, hands desperately clutching his hair.

It was at this moment that the later "monster Lin Yi" who could be ruthlessly cold in instances to survive, who had "profit maximization" carved into his bones, was truly born.

Because he understood more clearly than anyone: in this world, without money, even breathing is a sin.

## Section 3: Life-Selling Contract

Tap, tap, tap.

An extremely steady sound of leather shoes striking echoed in the empty corridor. Not the soft-soled shoes of doctors or nurses—the sound carried a kind of superior composure.

The footsteps stopped before Lin Yi.

Lin Yi raised his bloodshot eyes.

Standing before him was a man wearing an extremely well-tailored black suit. The man wore a dark red tie, holding a still-dripping black umbrella. On the man's chest was pinned an extremely exquisite metal badge—the "Ark Corporation" logo.

"Student Lin Yi, correct?"

The suited man looked down at him, voice gentle yet carrying a devil's temptation. "Jiangcheng University Computer Science Department freshman, IQ test 145, possessing extremely strong neural reaction speed and stress resistance. Of course, currently also a debt-ridden... pitiful person whose sister is about to have her tubes pulled."

"Who are you?" Lin Yi stared at him warily, like a young wolf guarding its food.

"Who I am doesn't matter. What matters is, I can save your sister's life."

The suited man pulled a thick document from his briefcase and handed it to Lin Yi.

"Ark Corporation is about to launch an epoch-making cross-dimensional VR device, codename 'Infinite Trial.' We need a batch of young people with excellent neural reactions for the first internal deep closed testing."

"As long as you sign this liability waiver and come with us tonight, two million yuan in 'settlement fees' will be transferred to this hospital's public account within one minute. This money is enough for your sister to use the best medicine until complete recovery."

Lin Yi stared intently at that document, heart pounding wildly.

"Two million? Just to test a game?" Though desperate, Lin Yi wasn't stupid. "What's the cost?"

"Cost?"

The suited man smiled, his smile carrying a trace of pity and fanaticism. "This game's pain synchronization rate is one hundred percent. During testing, you need to enter deep hibernation. Since the technology is still in testing phase, it may cause irreversible damage to your brain, even... brain death."

"Simply put, this is a life-selling contract." The suited man pointed to the skull warning symbol on the document's last page. "You trade your life for your sister's life. This is an absolutely fair transaction. Sign, or not?"

## Section 4: Rainy Night Accomplices

No hesitation.

Before the suited man could even use his persuasion techniques to incite him, Lin Yi had already snatched that pure black pen.

"As long as the money arrives."

Lin Yi's voice was hoarse. On the signature line of the document's last page, he wrote his name with extreme force. The pen tip tore through the paper, even leaving deep marks on the cardboard underneath.

"Even if it's real hell, I'll go."

The suited man looked at Lin Yi's bloodshot yet exceptionally determined eyes, surprise flashing in his gaze, then nodded with satisfaction.

"Very good. Ark needs 'consumables' like you who have attachments yet are crazy enough."

The suited man collected the document, took out a walkie-talkie and spoke a few words quietly.

"The money has been transferred. In ten minutes, my team will take you to the underground laboratory. Your body will be injected with hibernation fluid and frozen until the trial ends."

"Go say goodbye to your sister."

Lin Yi ignored the suited man. He turned and rushed to the ICU's glass window, looking at his still-sleeping sister inside. His lips trembled violently, trying to squeeze out a smile, but tears burst forth instead.

"Xiao Ya, when brother clears the game, I'll take you home..."

He silently swore in his heart. Lin Yi at that time had no idea this goodbye would be exactly fifty years; he had no idea he was signing not a game beta agreement, but a hibernation pod ticket before the apocalypse.

Just as Lin Yi took a deep breath, preparing to wipe away his tears and leave with the suited man.

From the other end of the corridor, an extremely noisy commotion suddenly erupted.

"Help! What are you doing!"

"Debts must be repaid—that's natural law! Your old man jumped off a building and died, so this rotten debt falls to you, his daughter! If you don't produce the money today, I'll take you as payment!"

Accompanied by a woman's screams and men's brutal cursing, several figures came pulling and shoving from the stairwell into the corridor.

Lin Yi instinctively turned his head.

Under the dim corridor lighting, he saw several vicious debt collectors cornering a young girl in a white coat who looked like a medical school intern against the wall.

Though driven to desperation, that girl's eyes were exceptionally cold and fierce.

That was nineteen-year-old Su Liang.
