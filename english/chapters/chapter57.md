# Chapter 57: Those Who Go Against the Tide

## Section 1: The Madman's Chips

"Detonate the armory?"

The Ranger boss's mechanical left arm hung in midair, single eye staring intently at Lin Yi, as if looking at an absolute madman who just escaped from a mental hospital.

"Kid, do you know how much of our Rangers' assets are stored down there? That's the life savings we've scraped together over more than ten years on this wasteland!"

"This pile of scrap metal can't save your life."

Lin Yi met the boss's gaze without backing down. The concrete ceiling overhead was violently trembling, more and more rubble crashing onto the ammunition boxes beside them. The scraping sounds of mechanical hounds above and the rumbling of Harvester treads were already so clear they seemed right beside their ears.

"The defense line is breached, the outpost location completely exposed. If those iron dogs rush in, not only will these weapons become a pile of scrap metal, but all of us will become spare parts in Ark's wetware processing factory."

Lin Yi raised the AK assault rifle in his hand, pointing toward the piles of homemade explosives and high-explosive mines deep in the armory.

"Trade an abandoned bomb shelter we can't hold anyway for dozens of Ark's elite combat units, plus a ticket straight to the mainframe's heart." Lin Yi's lips curved into an extremely cold arc. "This deal is guaranteed profit no matter how you calculate it."

"Damn it..."

The boss gritted his teeth, the burn scars on his face twisting from extreme struggle. He was a battle-hardened wasteland veteran who naturally understood the principle that compassion doesn't command troops.

"Falcon!" The boss suddenly turned his head, letting out a roar. "Activate the 'Scorched Earth' contingency! Get those thermobaric bomb detonators out!"

"Roger!" Falcon wasted no words. She was even more decisive than the boss. In this damned world, hesitating even one second was disrespecting life. She quickly rushed to the control panel in the armory corner, pulling open a red physical circuit box.

"Take the emergency drainage pipe! It connects to the backup garage on the canyon's back side!" The boss grabbed two heavy electromagnetic guns, roaring at Lin Yi and Su Liang.

## Section 2: Scorched Earth and Tomb

The four ran like moles frantically shuttling underground through the narrow, damp emergency drainage pipe.

Just thirty seconds after they entered the pipe.

BOOM!

The armory's ceiling finally completely collapsed. Dozens of mechanical hounds poured down from the breach like a black tide, their red glowing eyes instantly illuminating the entire underground space. Immediately after, an extremely massive Harvester used its terrifying mechanical legs to forcibly crush the remaining rock layer, its huge body squeezing into the bomb shelter.

"Large weapons cache detected. Targets not found. Rescanning heat sources..."

The Harvester's electronic voice echoed in the empty underground.

But all they scanned was a detonator with a frantically flashing red light.

At the end of the drainage pipe, the boss suddenly pressed the remote control in his hand.

"Go to hell, iron turtles."

BOOM—!!!

Not the sharp explosion of ordinary explosives, but an extremely dull rumble as if the earth's crust itself was groaning.

Four old-era thermobaric bombs positioned at the armory's base detonated simultaneously. Terrifying high temperatures instantly sucked all oxygen from the underground space, forming an extremely violent metal storm and vacuum negative pressure.

The blast wave surged wildly through the drainage pipe, directly ejecting all four like cannonballs from the pipe opening.

The four crashed heavily onto a hidden sandy area on the canyon's back side, looking back.

The entire rock mountain where Ash Outpost was located suddenly collapsed over ten meters inward from the violent internal explosion. A huge mushroom cloud mixed with dust shot skyward. Those mechanical hounds that had just rushed into the armory and that high-and-mighty Harvester, under several thousand degrees of heat and tens of thousands of tons of rock burial, left not even slag, completely buried in this tomb custom-made for them.

## Section 3: Against the Sand Sea

"Cough cough cough..."

Lin Yi climbed up from the sand, brushing off the dirt, looking at that collapsed rock mountain. On his left palm, the black metal brand was slightly heating up, as if cheering for this pure physical destruction.

"Satisfying! Damn satisfying!"

The Ranger boss spat out bloody saliva mixed with sand, looking at the destroyed outpost. His eyes held no regret whatsoever, but instead burned with unprecedented fervor. "After being chased like rats by Ark for so many years, this is the first time I've dug them a grave!"

"Don't celebrate too early." Su Liang stood up, closing her eyes to listen. Her hearing, freed from underground echo interference, became even more acute on this open wasteland.

"The explosion solved the pursuers, but also attracted more attention." Su Liang's brow furrowed tightly. "Three o'clock and nine o'clock directions, about ten kilometers away, two more Harvesters' tread sounds are rapidly approaching."

"Get in the vehicle!"

Falcon had already pulled off the backup garage's camouflage net. Inside was a heavily modified all-terrain assault vehicle with an extremely high chassis. "Before the encirclement closes, we break through!"

The four quickly jumped into the assault vehicle. Falcon floored the accelerator. The engine roared violently, kicking up clouds of yellow sand, charging into the sandstorm with extremely poor visibility.

But at the canyon exit fork, Falcon suddenly slammed the brakes.

Two roads.

One led to the wasteland's deep uninhabited zone—the Rangers' usual escape route.

The other, facing the overwhelming yellow sand and patrolling mechanical legions, pointed straight toward that massive, heavily fortified, inviolable Ark One dome.

In all previous engagements, the Rangers always fled outward.

Falcon's palms gripping the steering wheel were covered in sweat. She turned her head, looking at Lin Yi in the back seat, extremely calmly checking his AK rifle magazine.

"Kid, give directions." The Ranger boss sat in the passenger seat, tapping the door with his mechanical arm, voice rough yet resolute. "Today, we're not deserters anymore."

Lin Yi raised his head.

His gaze penetrated the murky sandstorm, locking onto that huge metal hemisphere at the edge of his retina.

"Drive straight there."

Lin Yi's voice was cold as ice, without the slightest hesitation. "Su Liang, be the radar. Avoid all patrol teams. We're going to stick the knife directly into its exhaust pipe."

The assault vehicle let out a roar. Tires violently scraped the sandy ground, then suddenly turned, like a mad dog swimming upstream, racing wildly toward the wasteland's most dangerous center.

## Section 4: Physical Firewall

The sandstorm grew stronger, visibility less than ten meters.

But this was the best natural cover. Su Liang sat with eyes closed in the bouncing vehicle, threading the needle through this death minefield purely by hearing.

"Hard left! Heavy mech patrol eight hundred meters ahead!"

"Slow down! Servo motor sounds from thermal imaging scanner behind the right dune, drive along the leeward slope!"

Under Su Liang's quantum-radar-level precise callouts, the assault vehicle moved like a ghost, extremely dangerously shuttling through Ark's outer defense network.

Forty minutes later.

"Stop." Lin Yi suddenly spoke.

Simultaneously, Su Liang opened her eyes. "No road ahead. It's a wall."

Falcon hit the brakes. The assault vehicle stopped before a seemingly ordinary huge sand dune. Sand blew past—ahead, besides rolling yellow sand, there was nothing.

"Where's the exhaust port you mentioned? There's nothing here." The boss raised his gun, warily surveying the surroundings.

Lin Yi jumped out of the vehicle.

He didn't use his eyes to look. He closed his eyes, raising that left hand bearing the black brand, slowly extending it forward.

Thump. Thump.

In Prison Zero, he had relied on this brand to sense the physical exhaust port's vibrations amid the overwhelming formatting storm, jamming it and causing mainframe overload.

At this moment, in the real physical world, that extremely massive heat discharge vibration maintaining Ark's millions of brains and super AI operation was clear as thunder in Lin Yi's palm!

"Eyes can deceive."

Lin Yi suddenly opened his eyes, drawing the P-19 kinetic pistol from his outer thigh, and without hesitation pulled the trigger at the seemingly empty sand dune before him.

BANG!

The electromagnetically accelerated armor-piercing round shot into midair. It didn't penetrate air, but instead struck like hitting a water surface, rippling outward with dazzling blue circles.

Crackle—

The optical camouflage disguise briefly glitched after being hit.

The originally unremarkable sand dune disappeared.

Revealed before everyone was a massive black metal structure thirty meters high, embedded in the rock layer. Twelve giant industrial fans over five meters in diameter were frantically rotating, expelling Ark's internal waste heat skyward.

This was Ark One's physical firewall, and also its most vulnerable respiratory tract.

And before that massive exhaust valve.

Stood four rows—a full twenty-four Ark elite guards in white exoskeleton armor, plus two defense mechs mounted with heavy Gatling guns.

"Optical camouflage damaged! Intruders detected!"

Alarm sounds instantly echoed across the dune.

"Twenty-four cyborgs, two heavy mechs." Lin Yi looked at that death defense line composed of steel and gun barrels ahead, lips curving into a cruel wild smile.

He holstered the P-19 pistol, both hands leveling that scratch-covered old-era AK rifle from the Ranger armory. The [Nirvana] gene in his body erupted with tsunami-like battle intent the instant it saw enemies.

"Su Liang, old rules."

Lin Yi pulled the bolt with a click, striding forward.

"You listen, I kill."
