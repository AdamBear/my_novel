# Chapter 53: White Coat and Payment Demands

## Section 1: Logic of Loan Sharks

202X, summer night, Jiangcheng First People's Hospital.

The fluorescent tube at the corridor's end emitted a "buzz" of electric current due to poor contact, flickering on and off.

Nineteen-year-old Su Liang wore a slightly oversized white coat—her uniform as a medical school intern. But at this moment, this garment symbolizing healing was stained with mud spots. She was cornered in a dead-end safety passage by three debt collectors reeking of alcohol and covered in dragon and phoenix tattoos.

"Doctor Su, running? Why aren't you running anymore?"

The leader was a bald man with a face full of flesh, playing with a switchblade. The blade reflected cold light in the dim illumination.

Su Liang didn't cry and beg for mercy like an ordinary nineteen-year-old girl, nor did she tremble. Her back pressed tightly against the cold tiles, those cool eyes showing a fierceness and extreme rationality of being driven to desperation.

"Although my father's company went bankrupt, before he jumped off the building he had already mortgaged all property under his name to the bank. As for your so-called private loan..."

Su Liang looked coldly at the bald man, speaking extremely fast, as if stating a medical record. "Principal of three hundred thousand, only three months have passed, and now you want me to repay two million. This is loan sharking. The law doesn't support this compound interest calculation method. Even if you kill me, I can't produce this money."

"Law?"

The bald man acted as if he'd heard the world's biggest joke, suddenly leaning close to Su Liang, a mouthful of suffocating alcohol breath spraying on her face. "I am the law! Your old man, that deadbeat, died quick and easy, kicked his legs and flew down from the rooftop. This rotten debt falls to you, his daughter!"

He patted Su Liang's pale cheek with the knife's back, eyes brazenly looking her up and down.

"Can't produce money? Easy. You're so pretty, and a top student from medical school. Our boss said, sell you to a Southeast Asian establishment, or dismantle your fresh organs and sell them on the black market—two million can be gathered quickly."

"Especially these eyes—so beautiful, they'll definitely fetch a good price."

The bald man's eyes instantly became vicious. He suddenly reached out, grabbing Su Liang's collar, about to drag her out.

"Let go!"

Su Liang, like an enraged small leopard, lowered her head and bit hard into the bald man's wrist.

"Hiss—you bitch, you're asking for death!"

The bald man cried out in pain, eyes flashing with murderous intent. The switchblade in his hand slashed toward Su Liang's face without hesitation!

## Section 2: Madman in the Storm

At the other end of the corridor, Lin Yi, who had just signed that "Infinite Trial" life-selling contract, witnessed everything.

If it were normal times, as a rationality-first college student, he absolutely wouldn't provoke this kind of social scum. He would choose to call the police, or silently walk away.

But tonight was different.

Ten minutes ago, to gather medical expenses for his sister in ICU, he had just sold his life to a mysterious company called "Ark" for two million yuan. Now he was no longer a normal person with a bright future, but a "living dead" about to enter a hibernation pod, not even afraid of death.

Watching that girl at the corridor's end also driven to desperation by "money," watching that knife about to fall, an uncontrollable violence and madness suddenly surged in Lin Yi's mind.

No shouting, no warning.

Just as that knife was about to slash Su Liang's cheek, at that critical moment, a rain-soaked figure charged over like a cheetah.

Thud!

Lin Yi suddenly extended his left arm, forcibly blocking it in front of Su Liang.

The sharp switchblade directly cut through Lin Yi's forearm, carving a deep bloody groove reaching bone. The blade's momentum continued, sliding down Lin Yi's arm. The knife tip grazed Su Liang's right shoulder, tearing through the white coat, leaving a seven-to-eight-centimeter bloody mark below her collarbone.

"Ah!" Su Liang cried out in pain, covering her shoulder.

But Lin Yi acted as if nothing happened, not even furrowing his brow.

Blood dripped from his fingertips onto the clean tiles. He didn't even look at his wound, but raised those bloodshot, abyss-like dead eyes, staring intently at that bald man.

## Section 3: More Vicious Than Evil Spirits

The bald man froze. He'd mixed in the streets for so many years, seen people begging for mercy, seen people fighting desperately, but never seen a college student who, after being slashed, had eyes even more vicious than his own.

"Who the fuck are you? Want to play hero?"

The bald man tried to pull out the knife with false bravado.

But he couldn't pull it out.

Lin Yi's slashed arm, muscles clamped tightly onto the blade. His right hand suddenly shot out, grabbing the bald man's collar, directly slamming this man a size larger than him hard against the wall.

Bang!

"You just said, debts must be repaid?"

Lin Yi's voice was terribly hoarse. His face almost pressed against the bald man's face, blood dripping onto the bald man's clothes. "How much does she owe you? Two million?"

"None... none of your damn business!" The bald man was intimidated by Lin Yi's genuine desperado aura.

Lin Yi suddenly laughed.

He released his grip, pulled from his rain-soaked pocket a freshly printed payment receipt bearing Jiangcheng First People's Hospital's public account stamp, and slapped it on the bald man's face.

"Open your dog eyes and look clearly. I just sold my life for two million."

Lin Yi pointed at the veins on his neck, smile crazed. "I'm now a dead man who might not even live to tomorrow! You garbage debt collectors, want to clash with this rotten life of mine?!"

Lin Yi suddenly yanked out the switchblade stuck in his arm, gripping it reverse-handed, knife tip aimed at the bald man's eyeball.

"Come on!!"

That was a hysterical roar, a howl venting all despair and unwillingness.

The three debt collectors were completely terrified by this reckless madness. The tough fear the reckless, the reckless fear those who don't care about their lives. This blood-covered teenager before them was clearly a complete madman.

"You're ruthless... kid, you wait for me!"

The bald man didn't even want his knife. He pushed Lin Yi aside and fled toward the stairwell with his two subordinates, stumbling and crawling.

## Section 4: Origin of the Debt

The corridor returned to deathly silence.

Only the storm outside continued washing against the glass.

Lin Yi leaned against the wall as if drained of strength. The switchblade in his hand clattered to the ground. Only then did he feel the piercing pain from his forearm. He sucked in a cold breath, covering the wound, slowly sliding to sit on the ground.

Across from him, Su Liang still leaned in the corner.

She covered the still-bleeding knife wound below her right collarbone with one hand, examining this suddenly appearing lifesaver with extremely complex eyes.

No tearful gratitude, no concerned inquiries.

Su Liang pulled a roll of bandages and a bottle of iodine from her white coat pocket—standard supplies for an intern doctor. She walked before Lin Yi and crouched down.

"Extend your hand." Her voice remained cool, even carrying a businesslike tone.

Lin Yi didn't refuse, extending his blood-covered left arm.

"You didn't do it to save me." Su Liang said while brutally disinfecting him with iodine. "You were just venting."

"Hiss—lighter." Lin Yi gritted his teeth in pain. "Is this how you treat your lifesaver?"

"I didn't ask you to save me." Su Liang efficiently wrapped his wound tightly with bandages, then simply bandaged the knife wound on her own shoulder. "But I'll remember this favor."

Their gazes met in the dim corridor.

Like two lone wolves frozen nearly to death in a blizzard, able only to warm themselves by licking each other's wounds.

Su Liang's gaze fell on that payment receipt on the ground, and the "Infinite Trial" promotional flyer with Ark Corporation's logo peeking from Lin Yi's pocket.

"You just said you sold your life for two million?" Su Liang picked up that flyer, looking at the slogan "High settlement fees, 100% real pain sensation testing," pupils slightly contracting.

"What, you want to go?" Lin Yi leaned against the wall, laughing self-mockingly. "Don't say I didn't warn you—that person said testing might cause brain death. That's real hell."

Su Liang held the flyer, slowly standing up.

She looked at the pitch-black night sky outside the window, touching the scar just left on her shoulder.

"For people carrying compound interest on loan shark debts, reality is hell."

Su Liang turned her head, looking at Lin Yi on the ground, those cool eyes flashing with a madness and determination ordinary people couldn't understand.

"Where is the person who gave you the contract?" she asked.

Lin Yi paused, pointing toward the corridor's end elevator. "First floor lobby, a black business van."

"Thank you."

Su Liang clutched that flyer tightly in her hand, turning toward the elevator.

Lin Yi watched her back, suddenly speaking. "What's your name?"

Su Liang stopped, turning her head sideways.

"Su Liang."

"I'm Lin Yi."

Nineteen-year-old Lin Yi supported himself against the wall and stood up, looking at that girl as desperate as himself. "If we meet in hell, remember to repay my favor."

Su Liang didn't turn back, only coldly dropping one sentence:

"I don't owe favors. I only calculate interest."

The elevator doors closed, taking that nineteen-year-old girl, and their fateful bond spanning half a century, slowly sinking into the abyss.
