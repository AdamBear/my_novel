# Chapter 51: Painkillers and Old Scars

## Section 1: Crude Reality

The medical area deep in the bomb shelter was filled with an even more pungent smell than the communications room. It was a nauseating mixture of inferior medical alcohol, moldy gauze, and old blood.

There were no sterile wards here, no cardiac monitors—only a few makeshift beds cobbled together from ammunition boxes and wooden planks.

Lin Yi strode into the medical area and immediately saw Su Liang lying on the innermost bed.

The situation was indeed very bad.

Su Liang's body was like a drawn bow, violently resisting and convulsing on the bed. Her eyes were tightly closed, face showing a sickly flush, forehead covered with fine beads of sweat. Her hands were desperately clawing at the bed board, nails even scratching deep bloody marks into the wood.

"Hold her down! Quick, hold her down!"

Old Ghost, the medical officer with the mechanical prosthetic eye, was sweating profusely trying to pin down Su Liang's shoulders. In his hand was a thick glass syringe that looked like it was meant for livestock, containing half a tube of murky yellow liquid.

But even in deep comatose fever, Su Liang's instinctive explosive strength was astonishing. She suddenly swung her hand, directly pushing Old Ghost into a stumble, nearly falling.

"Don't touch me... get away..."

Su Liang's throat emitted unconscious low growls, voice hoarse as if scraping on sandpaper.

"She's running a high fever." Lin Yi stepped forward, steadying Old Ghost with one hand, brow furrowed. "What's wrong? Is the processing factory's nutrient solution poisonous?"

"It's not the nutrient solution—it's her brain!"

Old Ghost gasped, raising the syringe and pointing at Su Liang's head. "Her auditory nerves endured loads far beyond human limits in what you call that 'virtual network.' Although she survived in the nutrient tank, now that she's free from the hibernation fluid's suppression, the nerve damage has triggered extremely severe physical high fever!"

"If we don't get this painkiller and sedative into her quickly, her brain will be cooked into mush by her own body temperature!"

## Section 2: 450 Points Delirium

Lin Yi wasted no more words.

He strode to the bedside, hands reaching out like iron clamps, firmly pinning down Su Liang's shoulders.

After the [Nirvana] gene awakened in his physical body, Lin Yi's strength had long surpassed ordinary human limits. Su Liang's violent struggles were forcibly nailed to the bed board under his restraint.

"Let go... too loud... it hurts..."

Su Liang twisted her neck in pain, physiological tears seeping from the corners of her tightly closed eyes. Her mind was clearly still trapped in that soul-tearing formatting storm of Prison Zero.

"Lin Yi..."

In extreme pain, her lips trembled, suddenly murmuring Lin Yi's name.

Lin Yi's hands pressing her paused slightly.

"Principal... three hundred... compound interest..." Su Liang's brow twisted painfully together, as if having the world's most terrible nightmare, grinding her teeth as she muttered, "You owe me... 450 points... don't you dare die..."

Hearing this, Old Ghost standing to the side froze, looking bewildered. "What three hundred, four hundred? Is she delirious from fever?"

But Lin Yi understood.

In this filthy, desperate wasteland camp, hearing this "last words" full of money talk, Lin Yi's eyes—which had become cold and hard as iron after learning he was "fifty-year-old frozen meat"—suddenly rippled with extremely complex emotion.

In that absolute death trap where even the system wanted to format them, this woman forcibly withdrew her auditory defenses, exposing her brain nerves to white noise, just to give him coordinates.

"I'm not dead."

Lin Yi lowered his head, leaning close to Su Liang's ear, voice low and steady. "And you won't die either. I remember that debt."

Miraculously, hearing Lin Yi's voice, Su Liang's tense body showed a brief moment of relaxation.

"Now!" Lin Yi barked at Old Ghost.

Old Ghost moved quickly. The thick needle brutally pierced Su Liang's arm vein, pushing in that half tube of murky sedative.

## Section 3: Secret of the Right Shoulder

The medication took effect quickly.

This industrial-grade purified sedative from the wasteland had severe side effects, but immediate results. In less than half a minute, Su Liang's violent struggles gradually subsided, rapid breathing becoming long and steady.

"Phew... finally got it done." Old Ghost wiped the sweat from his forehead, scanning Su Liang's vital signs with his mechanical eye. "The fever hasn't broken, but this drug can forcibly block her neural pain receptors, letting her brain rest. Whether she can make it through tonight depends on her own fortune."

Old Ghost shook his head, packed up his medical kit, and limped out of the partition to treat other wounded.

By the narrow bed, only Lin Yi and the unconscious Su Liang remained.

The room was stuffy and hot. Su Liang's white hospital gown from the processing factory was completely soaked with sweat, clinging tightly to her body. Due to the violent struggles earlier, the gown's collar had been torn open halfway, slipping askew below her shoulders.

Lin Yi instinctively reached out, wanting to help her pull the clothes back up to prevent catching cold again in this damp bomb shelter.

However, just as his fingers touched the fabric on Su Liang's right shoulder, his movement suddenly froze like someone had pressed pause, stuck rigidly in midair.

His gaze, as if magnetized, locked onto the exposed area below Su Liang's right collarbone.

There was a scar.

This wasn't a wound left by monsters in virtual trials. Injuries received in the system, no matter how real the pain, would leave no trace on the physical body once disconnected.

This was an old wound from the physical world.

The scar was about seven or eight centimeters long, showing an old pale pink color. The edges were very irregular, as if something extremely sharp had viciously slashed here, even injuring the muscle layers beneath.

Lin Yi stared intently at this scar.

The [Fragment of Reality] on his left palm gave no warning, meaning this wasn't an illusion.

A strong sense of déjà vu, like an electric current piercing through his brain, instantly swept through Lin Yi's entire body.

## Section 4: Rainy Night Half a Century Ago

"This scar..."

Lin Yi's fingers trembled slightly.

His computer-precise memory bank began frantically searching through his mind.

He suddenly remembered the second instance "Lightless Hospital."

In that absolutely dark hall, when he first heard the voice of Su Liang, codename "99," he had felt an extremely absurd sense of familiarity.

He had thought at the time that in that darkness filled with formalin smell, that cool female voice seemed like he had heard it long ago on some rainy night, in some equally desperate hospital corridor.

He had originally thought it was just some déjà vu given by the system, or an illusion from his subconscious.

But now, looking at this old scar across Su Liang's shoulder, the floodgates of memory collapsed with a roar.

This scar... he had seen it before.

Not only seen it—he even clearly remembered how this scar came to be!

The images in his mind began rapidly rewinding.

Out of Ash Camp, out of Ark One, out of that false Perfect City and Lightless Hospital...

All the way back fifty years. Back to that summer of 202X.

Back to that night of pouring rain and thunderous roars.

It was outside the ICU intensive care unit of Jiangcheng First People's Hospital.

Nineteen-year-old Lin Yi clutched his sister's thick stack of payment demands—enough to crush a family—desperately leaning against the cold wall.

And at the end of the corridor, several vicious debt collectors were cornering a medical school intern in a white coat, also driven to desperation.

A switchblade flashed cold light in the dim illumination, slashing the intern's shoulder.

That intern's face, that deep wound reaching bone, and the scar on Su Liang's shoulder on the bed before him—across exactly half a century of time—perfectly overlapped.

"Su Liang..."

Lin Yi slumped into the chair beside the bed, looking at this "desperado" who, like him, had been awakened from a fifty-year-old freezer.

His voice in the silent ward carried a tremendous shock that had traversed life, death, and time.

"So fifty years ago... the person who signed that life-selling contract with me in the hospital corridor..."

"Was you."
