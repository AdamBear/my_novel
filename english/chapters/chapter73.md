# Chapter 73: Tyrant's Iron Law

## Section 1: Blood and Bread

When Falcon led those three hundred ragged "frozen meat" remnant forces into the blood-washed Black Water Bar, everyone held their breath, shocked by the scene before them.

The air was filled with pungent blood smell and gunpowder's acrid scent. The originally noisy bar hall now resembled a slaughterhouse. Over a dozen incomplete mercenary corpses lay scattered on the ground—some riddled with shotgun holes, some with sternums forcibly shattered. The walls were covered with dense bullet holes.

At the bar's only intact counter in the center, Lin Yi sat calmly. He held a clean rag, carefully wiping blood off that P-19 kinetic pistol.

That once-arrogant Iron Blood Mercenary Corps deputy commander "Mad Dog" now hung like a dog with broken legs, hands bound behind with wire, suspended from a load-bearing pillar behind the bar counter, mouth stuffed with a rag, emitting desperate, weak whimpers.

"Boss... Boss Lin..."

Refugee leader Old Zhao swallowed hard, walking on weak legs across the sticky blood-colored floor. The three hundred survivors behind him were even more terrified, pale-faced, not daring to look at the corpses on the ground.

Lin Yi didn't look up, just casually holstered the cleaned pistol.

Bang!

The Ranger boss shouldered two heavy military wooden crates from the VIP booth, heavily slamming them on the floor. He used a tactical knife to pry open the locks, kicking open the lids.

The entire scene instantly fell silent.

The left crate was filled with neatly arranged old-era high-energy field rations, canned goods, and dozens of barrels of pure water without any radiation contamination. The right crate contained brand new assault rifles gleaming with cold hard metallic luster, large-caliber shotguns, and piles of brass bullets.

"You ate liquid food your whole lives in Ark, gnawed radioactive raw meat in the snow."

Lin Yi finally raised his head, those pitch-black eyes sweeping over the crowd, voice not loud yet clearly reaching everyone's ears.

"Now, look at these corpses on the ground, then look at the bread and water in the crates." Lin Yi pointed at Mad Dog on the ground. "This is wasteland logic. The only way to live decently is to step on others' heads, seize their supplies."

## Section 2: Granting Armor

Three hundred hungry eyes stared fixedly at that food and water, but due to fear of Lin Yi, not one person dared rush forward to loot.

"Old Zhao, step forward." Lin Yi coldly called out.

Old Zhao trembled all over, quickly jogging to Lin Yi, waist bent almost to the ground.

Lin Yi grabbed an old-era assault rifle with tactical rails from the right munitions crate, slapped in a magazine full of bullets, then directly tossed it to Old Zhao.

Old Zhao fumbled to catch this heavy killing instrument, the heavy steel touch making his hands tremble violently. In the real world fifty years ago, he was just an ordinary programmer who had never touched a real gun.

"Pick out thirty of the strongest, most daring to fight. From today, you're my personal guard."

Lin Yi stood up, looking down at him from above. "Guns are given to you. But without courage to back it up, this thing is just a fire poker. Anyone who dares retreat, I'll use this gun to blow their head off."

"Yes... yes! Boss Lin!" Old Zhao clutched the gun tightly, eyes flashing with light interweaving fanaticism and fear.

He immediately turned around, like selecting livestock, picking out thirty men from the three hundred refugees who had been most vicious when fighting over lizard meat earlier.

The Ranger boss and Falcon watched coldly from the side, assisting Lin Yi in distributing weapons from the crates one by one to these thirty people. Though their gun-holding postures were comical and movements clumsy, when these thirty black gun barrels were raised, this group originally only fit to be "meat pigs" finally had a trace of wasteland legion prototype.

## Section 3: Decimation Law

"Those who got guns, each person can receive two rations and one canteen of purified water. Everyone else gets half a ration."

Lin Yi issued the order.

The crowd began showing slight unrest. Though not daring to protest loudly, those not selected, looking at the personal guard's abundant food, showed extreme desire and unwillingness in their eyes.

"Think it's unfair?"

Lin Yi keenly captured these emotions. He walked to the front of the crowd. The substantialized oppressive aura catalyzed by the [Nirvana] gene made everyone instinctively lower their heads.

"On my turf, there are only three rules."

Lin Yi raised three fingers, voice like polar wind.

"First, absolute obedience. My words are system commands. Those who disobey orders, die."

"Second, food, water, antibiotics—all distributed by military merit. I don't want waste, I want wolves that can bite. Want to eat your fill, take those rocks and metal shards in your hands, go cut ears off enemy corpses to exchange for points. Kill more, eat better."

Lin Yi's gaze suddenly became extremely cruel. He slowly raised his third finger.

"Third. If in upcoming battles, any squad dares to flee or retreat without my order, or causes defensive line collapse due to cowardice..."

"I won't kill all of you."

Lin Yi paused, the corner of his mouth curving like a demon. "I'll have you execute 'decimation law.' Out of ten people, pick out the most cowardly one. The remaining nine teammates will use gun butts and rocks to beat him to death."

"If you can't bring yourselves to do it, I'll kill all ten of you."

Silence.

Absolute silence.

This most brutal collective punishment military law from ancient Rome, like a heavy hammer, viciously smashed into these former civilized people's souls. It not only locked off retreat with extreme fear, but also bound each person's life to their teammates'.

Retreat meant not only your own death, but being beaten to death by companions.

"Understood?" Lin Yi suddenly drew his kinetic pistol, firing a shot at the sky.

"Understood!!!"

Over three hundred people, awakened by the gunshot, erupted in heart-wrenching roars. That sound no longer contained the initial confusion and cowardice upon awakening, but was filled with ferocity and madness after being cornered.

## Section 4: Sharpening the Blade

"Too ruthless."

Falcon leaned against the bar edge, watching that group frantically using rags to wipe firearms and rocks to sharpen metal shards, saying quietly to the Ranger boss. "He's using fear to force a flock of sheep into wolves."

"On this wasteland, mercy doesn't last long." The boss lit a cigarette with his mechanical arm. "Besides, we're about to face situations a hundred times more brutal than this. Ark's purge forces and Black Rock City's other two major gangs won't give us breathing room."

Lin Yi finished handling the legion's organization, leaving remaining defense work to Old Zhao and the Rangers, then turned and walked into that VIP booth deep in the bar.

The gambling table in the booth had been cleared, replaced with a stainless steel long table wiped with disinfectant.

Medic Old Ghost was sweating profusely, opening that silver sterile medical case seized from Iron Blood Mercenary Corps' vault. Inside, rows of precise micron-level probes, bionic nerve interface lines, and high-magnification microscopes emitted cold luster.

Su Liang sat beside that stainless steel table.

She had removed her heavy tactical vest, wearing only a thin black tank top. Her face was pale as paper from continuous neural fever, right ear still occasionally seeping traces of blood.

But seeing Lin Yi walk in, those cool eyes showed not a trace of fear for the upcoming surgery without anesthesia.

"Outside rules established?" Su Liang looked at Lin Yi, asking flatly.

"Established."

Lin Yi walked to the table, looking at that old-era military bionic cochlear module covered in dried blood, the black brand on his left hand slightly contracting.

"Old Ghost, equipment's all here." Lin Yi turned to look at the medic adjusting the microscope. "I don't want to hear extra nonsense. I want her ears not only to hear, but to be more sensitive than before."

Old Ghost wiped sweat, glancing at that equipment, then at the medicine box without even anesthetics.

"Boss Lin, the equipment is top-grade. But this surgery requires cutting open her skull's lateral periosteum, inserting probes directly hard into brain nerves. Without high-strength anesthetics..." Old Ghost swallowed, voice trembling. "This level of severe pain will drive a person's sanity completely insane."

Su Liang didn't speak. She just silently picked up a clean towel nearby, folded it twice, and bit down on it.

Then she lay on that cold stainless steel table, exposing her right ear side under the light.

"Begin." Su Liang bit the towel, mumbling unclearly yet extremely firmly issuing the command.

Lin Yi strode forward, hands like iron clamps pressing on her shoulders.

"I'm here." Lin Yi looked into her eyes. "You haven't repaid the loan shark debt you owe me. I don't allow you to go insane."
