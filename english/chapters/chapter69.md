# Chapter 69: Blood-Colored Entry Token

## Section 1: Return from Wind and Snow

Deep in the old-era highway tunnel, the bonfire flickered unsteadily in the backdraft of cold wind, emitting crackling bursts.

Over three hundred survivors huddled tightly together, relying on each other's body heat and that pile of faint firelight from burning discarded tires to desperately resist the minus thirty-degree extreme cold. Their brains had frozen somewhat numb, stomach acid frantically corroding stomach walls, hunger like a dull knife sawing back and forth through their intestines.

The Ranger boss sat on the off-road vehicle's hood, his intact right hand gripping tightly the heavy machine gun's handle, single eye staring fixedly at the pitch-black blizzard outside the tunnel.

"Almost an hour now." Falcon paced anxiously by the fire. "Fifteen kilometers away are Ark regulars. Even with stealth assassination, this blizzard's intensity is enough to freeze them to death halfway."

"Shut up." The boss exhaled white breath. "If that Lin guy only had this much ability, he wouldn't deserve to kill his way out from under the mainframe's nose."

Just as he finished speaking.

From the wind-and-snow-filled tunnel entrance suddenly came heavy footsteps.

Crunch, crunch.

That was the sound of military boots stepping on accumulated snow.

Everyone simultaneously held their breath. Falcon even directly raised his electromagnetic rifle, finger on the trigger.

Two figures walked out from the sky full of white whirlwinds.

Lin Yi walking ahead was covered head to toe in a thick layer of ice shell mixed with mud and radioactive dust, the whole person like a statue dug from a glacier. But the moment he stepped into the tunnel and felt the bonfire's heat, the ice shell on his body surface began rapidly melting and cracking, emitting waves of white steam.

Behind him, Su Liang dragged two heavy black military supply crates, each step leaving a deep drag mark in the snow.

Bang!

Lin Yi casually tossed the two ice-covered supply crates beside the fire with a dull thud.

"Open them." Lin Yi's voice was terribly hoarse—the aftermath of vocal cords damaged in extreme cold then forcibly repaired under [Nirvana] gene action.

Falcon froze for a moment, stepped forward, and used his knife to pry open the crate locks.

The instant the lid popped open, those survivors around with hunger-green eyes uniformly swallowed extremely loud gulps.

Two hundred tubes of high-concentration synthetic nutrient paste neatly arranged, emitting an enticing energy aura. On the wasteland, this crate's value was enough to buy dozens of lives.

"You... you really wiped out Ark's full squad?!" The Ranger boss's single eye instantly widened. Looking at Lin Yi's face showing no emotional fluctuation, he felt a chill down his back.

Without heavy fire support, without armor cover, two people, one gun, one knife. Completely annihilating a well-equipped Ark patrol in a blizzard?

Was this still human?!

## Section 2: Tyrant's Grace

Lin Yi ignored the Rangers' shock. He removed that soaked cold-weather clothing, exposing his muscular upper body. High-temperature steam rose from his pores. Those fine wounds grazed by stray bullets were scabbing at a visible rate.

He walked to the supply crate, taking out a tube of nutrient paste.

Hundreds of hungry, greedy eyes stared fixedly at his hand movements. Someone even crawled forward two steps regardless of everything due to extreme hunger.

Lin Yi suddenly drew the P-19 kinetic pistol from his outer thigh, barrel directly pressed against the forehead of the man crawling at the front.

"Back off." Lin Yi's voice wasn't loud, but carried a killing intent more bone-chilling than the blizzard outside.

The crowd instantly shrank back like electrocuted.

Lin Yi holstered his gun, gaze sweeping over this ragged, trembling remnant force. Having experienced the radioactive meat selection, though these three hundred plus were weak, their eyes had shed the greenhouse naivety, gaining a trace of beast-like ferocity.

"In Ark, you were batteries, parts for the system to drain. Just lying there dreaming, the system would inject nutrient solution directly into your veins."

Lin Yi raised the nutrient paste in his hand high. Firelight cast his shadow on the tunnel's curved wall, like a demon preaching.

"But on the wasteland, nature doesn't feed waste! Here, food is seized, lives are killed for!"

Lin Yi tossed the nutrient paste to that center-parted man (the temporary leader among these refugees).

"Old Zhao, right?" Lin Yi looked at him. "You're in charge of distribution. Half a tube per person. Eat it, then stand up for me, grip those metal shards and rocks in your hands like real warriors!"

Old Zhao caught the nutrient paste, hands trembling violently. Looking at this asura-like youth before him, his knees went soft, directly kneeling on the ground.

"Boss Lin... from now on, the brothers' lives are yours!" Old Zhao bit open the nutrient paste seal, tears mixing with mud flowing into his mouth. "Whoever you tell us to bite, we'll bite!"

"I don't need your oaths."

Lin Yi turned, looking toward the deep darkness. "I only need your fangs sharp enough."

This very night, by this bone-chilling cold wind and faint bonfire. This group of former "civilized people" completely completed their transformation into "wasteland thugs." The first-generation tyrant's personal guard, in this tunnel full of radiation and ice and snow, finally completed their flesh-and-blood reforging.

## Section 3: City of the Abyss

The wind and snow finally ceased the next morning.

When that dark red sun struggled to climb above the horizon, the Rangers' convoy with three hundred walking thugs arrived at the edge of an extremely spectacular landform.

"We're here." The Ranger boss stepped on the brakes, pointing ahead.

Lin Yi stood in the truck bed, looking forward, pupils slightly contracting.

That wasn't a city built on the surface at all. What appeared before them was a super open-pit mine over ten kilometers in diameter, bottomlessly deep!

This was like a massive scar gouged into Earth's surface by a meteorite.

At the pit's edge, rows of rusty but still menacing old-era anti-aircraft artillery were mounted. Countless thick steel cables and elevators hung from the pit edge, leading straight to the depths. On the pit's inner walls, densely packed like a hornet's nest, countless shantytowns pieced together from scrap metal, shipping containers, and rock were embedded.

The pungent sulfur smell, black smoke from burning coal, and clamor mixed in the wind rose from that massive abyss.

"This is Black Rock City."

Falcon lit a cigarette, eyes complex. "The wasteland's largest lawless zone, a carnival ground for a hundred thousand thugs, smugglers, and mutants."

The convoy drove down the extremely steep mountain dirt road without guardrails along the pit edge.

Just as they were about to reach the massive iron gate entrance to the first residential level, two armored vehicles mounted with heavy Gatling guns cut across, blocking the way.

Over a dozen mercenaries wearing mismatched armor and reeking of blood jumped from the vehicles, gun barrels uniformly aimed at Lin Yi's convoy.

"Iron Blood Mercenary Corps people." The Ranger boss lowered his voice. "One of Black Rock City's three major local snakes, responsible for guarding gates and collecting protection fees."

## Section 4: Blood-Stained Door Knocker

A mercenary leader with a face full of muscle, wearing a mechanical prosthetic eye, walked over chomping a cigar. He glanced at the Rangers' off-road vehicle, then focused on those three hundred ragged survivors behind who looked like refugees.

"Well, isn't this the Rangers' Ash Squad?" The mercenary leader mockingly exhaled thick smoke. "What, couldn't make it outside, came to Black Rock City as refugees? Brought so many 'meat pigs' too?"

He walked to Old Zhao, reaching out to squeeze Old Zhao's arm, eyes showing greedy light. "Tender skin and flesh, not yet completely contaminated by radiation sickness. This batch of goods is good. Send them to lower mine shafts to dig coal, or sell to the 'Corruption Cathedral' for experiments—can exchange for plenty of bullets."

Old Zhao gripped the sharp metal shard in his hand tightly, but he didn't move, because he was waiting for Lin Yi's order.

"Get lost."

A cold voice came from the off-road vehicle.

Lin Yi leaped down from the truck bed, those eyes made terrifyingly deep after a night of killing coldly locked onto the mercenary leader.

"Who the fuck are you?" The mercenary leader raged, reaching out to push Lin Yi's shoulder. "On Iron Blood's turf, it's not your turn to..."

Crack!

His hand hadn't touched Lin Yi's clothes when Lin Yi's left hand shot out like lightning, gripping the leader's wrist. The [Nirvana] second stage's terrifying bone density and explosive power instantly activated. With a teeth-grinding crisp sound, the mercenary leader's wrist was forcibly crushed into a grotesque comminuted fracture!

"Ahhhhh!!!" The leader let out a pig-slaughtering scream, directly collapsing to his knees.

"Click click click!" The surrounding dozen mercenaries instantly pulled bolts, atmosphere tense.

"Nobody move!" Falcon and the Ranger boss simultaneously raised heavy firepower.

Lin Yi didn't even glance at those gun barrels pointed at him. He released the leader's wrist, kicking him aside like trash. Then from his blood-stained pocket, he pulled out something, casually tossing it onto a nearby tin oil drum.

Clang.

It was a square metal block stained with black machine oil, still flickering with faint blue light.

The mercenary leader clutched his broken hand, about to order fire, but the instant he saw that metal block, his voice stopped abruptly as if stuck in his throat. That mechanical prosthetic eye frantically zoomed, scanning the data patterns on it.

"Ark... Ark guardian-class spider mech core CPU chip?!"

The leader's voice was full of incredulous trembling. This thing was priceless top-tier hard currency in Black Rock City! Ark's mech self-destruct programs were extremely tight. To obtain an intact chip, one must physically destroy the core the instant before mech detonation.

"Not just the chip, the underlying code inside is complete."

Lin Yi looked down at him from above, eyes carrying pure jungle overlord aura.

"My name is Lin Yi. These three hundred are my soldiers, not meat pigs."

Lin Yi extended his finger, tapping that priceless chip. "This chip is the entry ticket. Arrange an absolutely safe garrison for me. Also, I need your best antibiotics here, and precision neural surgery equipment."

He bent down slightly, looking at the cold-sweating mercenary leader, corner of his mouth curling into a cruel smile.

"Take my money, do your job properly. If your Iron Blood Mercenary Corps wants to double-cross me..." Lin Yi's tone was flat as casual conversation. "I don't mind letting this pit change owners."
