# Chapter 48: Ash Camp

## Section 1: Underground Remnants

The off-road vehicle jolted through sky-filling yellow sand for nearly three hours. Only after Ark One's massive dome completely disappeared beyond the horizon did the convoy enter a complex dark red canyon terrain. The bald driver skillfully navigated through maze-like rock pillars, finally stopping before a massive camouflage net that almost merged with the rock wall.

Following two long and one short car horn signals, the rock wall below slowly split open, revealing a heavy, bullet-scarred gear explosion-proof gate.

"Welcome to the sewer." Falcon jumped from the vehicle, patting her dust-covered armor. "Not so glamorous here, but at least you don't have to worry about being crushed by Ark's iron turtles."

Lin Yi carried Su Liang down from the vehicle, following the Rangers through the gate interior. The gate rumbled shut behind them, completely isolating the wasteland's violent wind and deadly sand.

Before Lin Yi's eyes appeared a massive old-era underground bomb shelter converted into a refuge. There were no blinding shadowless lights like inside Ark, no false neon like in Perfect City—only flickering dim yellow incandescent bulbs hanging in midair on messy wires.

The air was filled with an extremely complex smell: pungent inferior tobacco, engine oil, fermented sweat stench, and an undissolvable bloody smell. Along both sides of the bomb shelter passage, ragged tents and tin shacks were haphazardly erected.

Some ragged, emaciated humans huddled in shadows, watching Lin Yi—this "outsider" in a white hospital gown—with wary yet numb eyes. Some were missing limbs, using crude steel pipes as prosthetics; some had dark red radiation spots on their bodies.

Here were no virtual citizens with semi-permanent smiles from Infinite Trial, only the most real human remnants struggling desperately in dire straits.

## Section 2: Real Temperature

"Medic! Get out here and work!" The bald driver roared toward the bomb shelter's depths with his rough voice.

An old man wearing a stained white coat and a crude mechanical prosthetic eye over his left eye limped over. Lin Yi immediately placed the burning-hot Su Liang from his back onto a blood-stained army cot.

"Severe brain nerve damage causing physical high fever, plus severe dehydration and mild radiation infection." The old man scanned Su Liang with his mechanical eye, shook his head, and rummaged through a worn medical kit to pull out a murky injection with a faded label. "I only have expired broad-spectrum antibiotics and adrenaline. Whether she survives depends on her own fate."

In virtual trials, even near-death severe injuries could be instantly healed with a few hundred points purchasing "high-precision cell repair fluid"—one green light and complete recovery.

But here, there was no system panel, no points shop. Lin Yi watched the old man brutally push that murky liquid into Su Liang's vein, listening to Su Liang's painful muffled groans in unconsciousness. For the first time in his life, he felt a heavy sense of powerlessness.

This was reality's cruelty—life here wasn't data, but extremely fragile carbon-based protein.

"Eat something." Falcon walked to Lin Yi's side, handing him a military canteen and a black, stone-hard object.

Lin Yi took it and bit down. The texture was extremely rough, with intense earthy smell and a trace of undisguisable sour rot.

"Mutant sand rat jerky. Tastes terrible, but provides calories." Falcon leaned against the wall, lighting a rolled cigarette. "On the wasteland, picky eaters all died out."

"Tastes great." Lin Yi chewed expressionlessly, swallowing along with the sand in the jerky. This rough throat-cutting pain made him truly feel the weight of being alive.

## Section 3: Delayed Echo

"Come with me. Our boss wants to see you." Falcon exhaled a smoke ring, stubbing out the cigarette.

Lin Yi settled Su Liang and followed Falcon toward the bomb shelter's depths. Along the way, they passed a compartment resembling a communications room. Inside were piles of disassembled electronic parts. An old, rust-antennae radio was emitting "crackle" static noise.

In this noisy static, Lin Yi's footsteps suddenly stopped dead. His pupils contracted sharply, gaze locked onto that old radio. The [Fragment of Reality] brand on his left palm transmitted a warm throb.

"What's wrong?" Falcon turned her head, hand instinctively pressing her waist holster.

"Is this place... called Ash Outpost?" Lin Yi's voice was hoarse, but his tone carried a bone-chilling certainty.

Falcon's eyes instantly became extremely dangerous, gun already half-drawn: "Who exactly are you? How do you know our base's code name? Ark's wanted notice didn't mention this."

Lin Yi ignored her gun muzzle. His mind clearly recalled that after passing the second instance "Lightless Hospital," in that pure white transit station, Su Liang kneeling on the ground, bleeding from seven orifices, repeating that passage.

"Seventh call to Ark, please respond." Lin Yi looked into Falcon's eyes, reciting word by word that dialogue buried deep in memory. "Our defense line was breached by Titans, requesting fire coverage. This is the last frequency. You bastards in the greenhouse, how long will you keep hiding?"

*Click.*

Falcon's gun pressed directly against Lin Yi's forehead. Her resolute face now showed complete disbelief and horror: "You... how do you know this encrypted message?! This was our desperate open-code distress signal sent to Ark three days ago when our defense line was about to collapse!"

## Section 4: Survivors' Gaze

"Because we heard it in the system."

Lin Yi didn't retreat, facing the dark gun muzzle, voice cold as ice. "In that Ark virtual network you call the 'greenhouse.' My companion is a radar. She intercepted this noise that penetrated the physical server into the data abyss."

The air froze in this moment. Falcon's gun-holding hand trembled slightly. She couldn't imagine that two "wetware" soaked in nutrient tanks with tubes plugged into their heads for virtual trials could cross the dimension between physical and data to hear their dying cry for help on this wasteland.

"Lower your gun, Falcon. He's not lying."

A low, hoarse voice like two rusty sandpapers rubbing came from the shadows.

The iron curtain behind the communications room was lifted. A burly middle-aged man with his left arm completely replaced by a crude mechanical prosthetic walked out. His face was covered with terrible burn scars, a knife scar cutting across his entire face, yet his eyes were as sharp as a lone wolf on the wilderness.

"Boss." Falcon immediately holstered her gun, stepping aside.

The man walked before Lin Yi, looking down at him scrutinizingly. His gaze swept over Lin Yi's white Ark hospital gown, over the barcode at the collar somewhat whitened from nutrient solution soaking, finally settling on the pitch-black metal brand fused with flesh and blood on Lin Yi's left palm.

"To intercept reality frequencies in deep sleep brain-in-vat state, and still live to tear off physical tubes and kill your way out of the processing factory..." The boss's mechanical left arm emitted slight hydraulic sounds. He stared at Lin Yi, eyes flashing with extremely complex emotion—seemingly mocking, yet seemingly sorrowful. "Looks like those mad scientists' theory is true. Only [Nirvana] genes pushed to desperation can break the boundary between illusion and reality."

"What exactly are you trying to say?" Lin Yi frowned, keenly detecting the dissonance in the other's words.

"I want to ask you a question, awakened one." The boss leaned close to Lin Yi, those bloodshot eyes staring at Lin Yi's young face. "In your memory, how long ago did you put on that VR helmet and enter the game?"

"A few days ago." Lin Yi answered without hesitation. "At most no more than a week."

Hearing this answer, the boss suddenly split his cracked lips, letting out an extremely desolate, bone-chilling miserable laugh.

He turned around, rummaged through the messy document pile on the communications desk, pulled out a dust-covered physical calendar with edges weathered and curled, and slammed it against Lin Yi's chest.

"Look at this, kid." The boss's voice echoed in the bomb shelter, carrying a death-sentence-like cruelty. "Look at what year and month it actually is now."
