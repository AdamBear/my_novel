# Chapter 59: The Deity's Entrails

## Section 1: Abyss Slide

Scalding.

This was the only sensation after jumping into the exhaust shaft.

Sixty degrees, seventy degrees, possibly approaching eighty degrees. This wasn't a passage at all, but a giant industrial oven running at full power. The massive waste heat generated by Ark's mainframe surged wildly upward through this huge vertical shaft, as if to directly roast all creatures falling into it into jerky.

"Ah—!"

Falcon let out a muffled groan. Her tactical vest began heating up from the high temperature and intense friction, skin surface rapidly developing large patches of red welts.

The Ranger boss's mechanical left arm continuously burst with blue electric sparks from intense friction and heat. Even the internal hydraulic oil began boiling, emitting piercing hissing sounds.

This was a sloped slide hundreds of meters deep. Lin Yi held Su Liang tightly in his arms, using his own back as a meat cushion, sliding down at high speed on the smooth, scalding metal pipe walls.

The [Nirvana] gene in his body once again displayed terrifying adaptability when encountering this deadly extreme physical environment.

Lin Yi's temperature regulation center was forcibly taken over. Sweat glands instantly sealed to prevent water loss from causing shock too quickly. His skin surface even secreted an extremely thin protective layer similar to some kind of heat-resistant oil, forcibly withstanding the pipe wall's temperature hot enough to sear flesh.

"Lin Yi... too hot..." Su Liang kept her eyes tightly closed, nearly unable to breathe under the pressure of strong wind and high temperature, voice thin as a thread.

"Hold on! Almost at the bottom!"

Lin Yi's eyes were bloodshot. The [Fragment of Reality] brand fused with flesh and blood on his left palm emitted astonishing dark red light, like a guiding ghost lamp in the darkness.

Forty-five seconds later.

The slide's gradient suddenly eased.

Splash! Splash! Splash!

The four were ejected from the pipe exit one after another like dumplings, crashing heavily into a huge liquid pool.

## Section 2: Real Eden

"Puha—!"

Lin Yi suddenly emerged from the liquid, gasping for breath.

The liquid in the pool wasn't hot, but instead carried an extremely cold, bone-piercing sensation. This wasn't water, but a colorless, odorless, somewhat viscous advanced insulating coolant specifically used to cool supercomputer physical motherboards.

"Everyone alive?!" The boss wiped coolant from his face with his only good hand, standing up from the pool. His mechanical left arm was completely destroyed, hanging lifelessly at his side.

"Can't die." Falcon pulled Su Liang, struggling to climb onto the nearby maintenance platform paved with metal grating.

Lin Yi also climbed up. He shook his soaked hair, wrung out that tattered white hospital gown, raised his head, and looked at this world hidden in Ark One's deepest depths.

All four, even the well-traveled Ranger boss, fell completely speechless at this moment.

Too massive.

This was a hollowed-out huge underground fault. Before them stood countless black metal obelisks a hundred meters tall. These obelisks were densely arranged like a grim steel forest.

Countless thick fiber optic cables emitting ghostly blue light wound and connected between the obelisks like blood vessels. Overhead were giant data bridges constantly flashing red and green indicator lights; underfoot were rumbling coolant circulation pumps.

The air was thick with strong ozone smell and that unique burnt smell from silicon-based chips operating.

"This is..." The boss looked up, single eye full of shock. "Ark's heart?"

"To be precise, the deity's entrails."

Lin Yi looked at this cold, solemn machine room that even carried a strange sacred feeling, a trace of mockery flashing in his eyes.

In the fifth instance "Perfect City," he had entered that cloud machine room called "Eden." There, the system used holographic projections to cover cruel reality, disguising the machine room as a fragrant grassy field.

And now, all false filters were stripped away.

This was the true physical carrier of Ark mainframe "Father." No breeze, no sunlight—only these cold silicon-based behemoths greedily devouring human brain matter and computing power.

## Section 3: Flesh Puppets

"Quiet."

Su Liang, who had just caught her breath, suddenly changed expression. Though her auditory enhancement system was destroyed by the formatting storm in Prison Zero, her physical body's hearing foundation still far exceeded normal people.

She lay on the maintenance platform, ear pressed against the cold metal grating.

"Many footsteps... approaching. Bare feet stepping on metal sounds."

Su Liang raised her head, eyes showing a trace of disbelief. "And they have no heartbeats."

"Creatures without heartbeats?" Falcon immediately raised the electromagnetic rifle soaked in coolant but still functional, aiming at the darkness deep in the machine room.

Tap, tap, tap.

Footsteps grew denser, surging from the obelisk shadows in all directions.

When they saw clearly what emerged, the Ranger boss sucked in a cold breath.

They were a group of "people."

Or rather, a group of flesh puppets that were once people, now only empty shells.

They were naked, skin pale as paper, hairless. Most horrifying was that their eyes and mouths were brutally sewn shut with sutures, control fiber optic cables connected to servers directly inserted into the backs of their heads.

In their hands, they held high-pressure welding guns, heavy metal wrenches, even cutting machines.

"Those 'upper class' who uploaded their brains to the cloud in 'Perfect City,' enjoying false eternal life..."

Lin Yi looked at these zombie-like monsters, voice cold as ice. "Ark didn't waste their remaining bodies. It made these brainless shells into the lowest-grade physical maintenance drones."

"Eliminate... intruders..."

All flesh puppets' throats, through implanted voice generators, uniformly emitted "Father's" mellow yet cold electronic synthesized voice.

The next second, dozens of puppets wielding maintenance tools lunged at the four like zombies!

## Section 4: Physical Virus Invasion

"Open fire!" The boss roared, drawing the large-caliber revolver from his waist.

Bang bang bang!

Ratatata!

Powder weapons' roars instantly shattered the machine room's deathly silence. Large-caliber bullets easily tore through the puppets' pale flesh, breaking their arms and legs.

But despairingly, these shells had no pain nerves at all. Even with perforated organs, even with a broken leg, they still crawled on the ground in extremely twisted postures under server command control, trying to burn through Lin Yi and the others' bodies with high-pressure welding guns.

"Shooting heads is useless! They have no brains!"

Su Liang extremely precisely severed a lunging puppet's cervical spine with her tactical knife while shouting. "Cut the data lines on the backs of their heads!"

"Too slow."

Lin Yi's eyes sharpened. If entangled by this tireless group of flesh puppets, when Ark's regular mechanical forces arrived, they would certainly die.

He slung the old-era AK rifle on his back, both hands empty, actually charging directly at the corpse tide.

"Lin Yi!" Falcon exclaimed.

The instant he approached the frontmost puppet, Lin Yi didn't use weapons. He suddenly extended his left hand, firmly pressing on the data line port at the back of that puppet's head.

The pitch-black [Fragment of Reality] brand on his palm erupted with dazzling red light the instant it contacted Ark mainframe's physical data line!

[Pain Network (Incomplete Version) · Reverse Physical Injection!]

In Prison Zero, Lin Yi used this move to explode the virtual Warden's logic.

And now, in the physical world, he would inject true human "agony" into this self-righteous supercomputer.

Hum—!!!

Lin Yi's consciousness directly connected through this data line to the entire server area's underlying maintenance network. He unreservedly converted the extreme physical pain he endured during the fall and burning into data pulses, madly pouring them into the network!

"Ahhhhh—!!!"

Accompanied by extremely sharp electrical screeching.

Those originally painless flesh puppets suddenly froze in place like being electrocuted. Black machine oil seeped from their sewn eyes and mouths, bodies violently convulsing like sieves.

Ark's underlying maintenance system experienced local logic overload under this extremely massive, unanalyzable "pain data" impact.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

Data lines connected to the backs of their heads burst with sparks from short circuits. Dozens of flesh puppets collapsed like puppets with cut strings, completely losing movement.

"What... what kind of monster ability is this..." The boss looked at the bodies covering the ground, then at Lin Yi standing in the corpse pile center with smoke rising from his left hand, couldn't help swallowing.

Lin Yi slowly turned around, face pale but eyes bright as if burning.

He ignored the boss's shock, instead raising his head to look toward the machine room's deepest depths.

Below that tallest, most massive black obelisk was a tightly closed, heavy titanium alloy door engraved with a huge double helix emblem.

Behind that door was the super AI controlling all of Ark One, treating humans as computing power batteries—"Father's" core motherboard.

"Let's go."

Lin Yi shook off the black blood on his left hand, drew the rifle from his back, striding toward that door.

"Time to meet our 'good father.'"
