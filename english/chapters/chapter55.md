# Chapter 55: Wasteland Dawn and Rust

## Section 1: Physical Dimension Evolution

The dim yellow bulb in Ash Camp's medical area flickered twice, emitting an extremely faint electric "buzz."

Su Liang sat on the bed cobbled together from ammunition boxes, reaching out to decisively pull out the IV needle from her arm. A drop of real blood seeped out. She had no points to exchange for "instant hemostatic gel," so she could only press hard on the needle mark with rough gauze nearby.

"Hiss..." Su Liang frowned slightly.

This subtle, persistent stinging sensation in the real world was completely different from the system-quantified "health deduction" in virtual trials. Here, pain wasn't just a warning—it came with real weakness and muscle fatigue.

"Your body is still very weak." Lin Yi leaned against the doorframe, watching her movements. "Fifty years of freezing, plus post-tube-removal rejection reactions—this isn't something that can be immediately recovered with inferior antibiotics."

"But I can hear."

Su Liang released her pressing fingers and closed her eyes. Her ears twitched slightly in the air. "I can hear that medical officer called Old Ghost in the adjacent passage cutting open bandages with rusty surgical scissors; I can hear mutant rats thirty meters away in the ventilation shaft gnawing on pipe insulation."

She opened her eyes, a trace of shock in her gaze. "Lin Yi, the abilities we were enhanced with in the system... have been brought into reality."

"To be precise, Ark has conducted targeted genetic induction on our physical bodies over the past fifty years."

Lin Yi raised his left hand, looking at the pitch-black [Fragment of Reality] brand on his palm. He slowly clenched his fist, and the air emitted an extremely dull bone-cracking sound.

He could feel that even without that stamina bar marked "100/100," the explosive power contained in his muscle fibers still far exceeded normal humans. That gene called [Nirvana] wasn't a virtual code at all, but was truly inscribed in the double helix structure of their carbon-based flesh.

"Ark used that hellish virtual trial to stimulate our subconscious, making the subconscious change the neural synapses and muscle structure of our physical bodies." Lin Yi coldly summarized. "They forged us as the highest-grade biological weapons."

"Then use the blade they forged to cut their throats." Su Liang threw off the tattered hospital gown, picked up a somewhat oversized gray-black wasteland combat uniform left by the Rangers from beside the bed, and changed without any hesitation in front of Lin Yi.

After dressing, she tied her hair neatly behind her head, her eyes regaining that veteran assassin's coldness.

"Let's go, creditor. We should inventory the assets on this wasteland."

## Section 2: Equivalent Exchange

When Lin Yi and Su Liang walked out of the medical area one after another, Old Ghost, who was changing dressings for the wounded, was so shocked his jaw nearly dropped.

"You... how are you up?!" Old Ghost looked Su Liang up and down with his mechanical prosthetic eye. "High fever caused by nerve damage—normal people would be unconscious for at least three days! Are your cellular metabolism rates monstrous?"

Lin Yi ignored Old Ghost's exclamation. He walked straight through the chaotic bomb shelter passage to the communications room where the Ranger leader and Falcon were.

Hearing footsteps, the camp boss with a face full of burn scars looked up, his mechanical left arm emitting extremely faint hydraulic sounds. When he saw Su Liang, pale but walking steadily, a trace of undisguised surprise flashed in his single eye.

"You're tougher than I imagined, frozen meat." The boss threw a severely worn atlas on the table, his tone carrying some probing.

"Lin Yi, right?" Falcon beside him held an unlit cigarette. "Since you've pulled through, you must follow Ash Outpost's rules. We don't feed idlers—everyone who gets rations must work. Considering you just woke up..."

"We need weapons."

Lin Yi directly interrupted Falcon's words. He walked to the table, hands pressed on the surface, staring at the boss's single eye with overwhelming pressure.

"Firearms, ammunition, tactical gear. Preferably high-frequency vibration knives or armor-piercing explosive rounds."

The air in the communications room instantly froze.

Falcon's eyes instantly sharpened, hand instinctively reaching for the holster at her waist. "Kid, do you know what you're saying? On the wasteland, weapons are life. Why should we share our lives with two deserters we just met?"

"Because I know where Ark One's exhaust port is."

Lin Yi's voice wasn't loud, but it struck like a heavy hammer on the boss and Falcon's hearts.

## Section 3: Wasteland Armament

The boss's mechanical arm slammed on the table, staring intently at Lin Yi. "What did you say?"

"Ark One is a closed ecological circulation system. Its underground section is the wetware processing factory. To maintain cooling for those millions of brains and the super AI's calculations, it must have physical exhaust channels to the outside."

Lin Yi dipped his finger in some tea water on the table, drawing several intersecting lines on the atlas with extreme precision.

"They're hidden beneath sand dunes three kilometers from the main dome, using optical camouflage and radar-jamming coating. But I personally smashed through there. I remember the coordinates and the weak points in the defenses."

Lin Yi raised his head, eyes showing absolute confidence and cold calculation. "You Rangers have been fighting guerrilla warfare on this wasteland for so many years without even touching Ark's shell, right? Give me weapons, I'll give you a blood vessel leading straight to the heart."

This was an open conspiracy, and the fairest equivalent exchange.

The boss stared at the water marks on the map, silent for a full half minute.

Finally, he split his cracked lips into a rough laugh.

"Falcon, take them to the armory." The boss's single eye flickered with ambition and madness. "Whatever they want, let them pick."

Ten minutes later, the bottom-level armory of Ash Outpost.

There were no sci-fi equipment glowing with blue light like in the system shop—only mountains of old-era firearms, homemade explosives, and scratched bulletproof vests. The air was thick with gun oil and rust.

Lin Yi didn't reach for those imposing-looking heavy machine guns. He selected an old-era AK-series assault rifle with the simplest, most durable structure, skillfully disassembling it, checking the rifling, oiling it, and reassembling it. Then he picked a large-caliber kinetic pistol and inserted it into the holster on his outer thigh.

Su Liang rummaged in the corner. She finally found two military tactical knives. Though they lacked the high-frequency vibration cutting ability of those in "Lightless Hospital," the pure steel blade and perfect balance could still easily cut an enemy's throat in the real physical world.

The two stuffed ballistic plates into tactical vests.

When they stood together again, that aura of top-tier hunters honed through countless life-and-death trials finally perfectly merged in these real bodies.

No system panel enhancements—only the most primitive steel and flesh.

## Section 4: Hound's Growl

"Good equipment choices, very practical."

Falcon leaned against the armory doorframe, watching their skilled movements, the wariness in her eyes gradually transforming into a trace of respect. "Looks like you killed plenty in that virtual hell."

"Just to survive." Lin Yi pulled the bolt, the crisp metallic collision echoing in the empty armory.

Just as Lin Yi was about to insert the magazine.

Su Liang's movements suddenly froze.

The tactical knife in her hand suspended in midair, her entire body taut like a drawn bow. She suddenly turned her head, staring intently at the thick concrete ceiling above the bomb shelter.

"What's wrong?" Lin Yi immediately noticed her abnormality, the brand on his left palm also beginning to transmit faint stinging sensations.

"Quiet."

Su Liang's face turned deathly pale in an instant. Her pupils slightly contracted, auditory nerves stretched to the limit in this narrow enclosed underground space.

"Above... on the surface..." Su Liang's voice trembled from extreme tension. "There's sound. Very faint, but rapidly approaching."

Falcon frowned. "The surface? Impossible. Our camouflage net is lead-grade, can block thermal imaging. And outside right now is a sandstorm—even mutant beasts won't come out."

"Not mutant beasts."

Su Liang gritted her teeth, speaking word by word. "It's the sound of treads grinding gravel, and... the hum of high-frequency servo motors."

She looked at Lin Yi, eyes showing the gravity of encountering a deadly Boss in trials.

"Lin Yi, it's those things we encountered in the processing factory... but more numerous. At least thirty."

As soon as she finished speaking.

The bulb in the armory suddenly emitted a series of rapid "buzzing" sounds, then the entire bomb shelter's power, as if interfered with by some strong electromagnetic pulse, instantly went out!

Immediately after, from the solid rock layer over ten meters thick overhead came an extremely dull explosion that seemed to penetrate the earth's crust!

BOOM—!!!

Large amounts of dust and rubble fell from the ceiling.

"Enemy attack!!!" Came the Rangers' heart-wrenching roars from outside. "It's Ark's purge forces! The defense line is breached!"

In the darkness, Lin Yi slammed the magazine into the rifle with a crisp "click."

"Looks like this fifty years of interest." Lin Yi said coldly in the darkness, eyes red as a demon's. "They're already eager to deliver it to our doorstep."
