# Chapter 54: Hidden Accomplices

## Section 1: Receding Rain Sounds

The patter of torrential rain gradually faded in his ears.

Along with the memory's retreat, that pungent disinfectant smell and formalin scent also dissipated, replaced by the moldy gauze smell of Ash Camp's medical area, the bitter taste of inferior antibiotics, and the omnipresent dust breath of the wasteland.

Lin Yi still slumped before that bed cobbled together from ammunition boxes.

The wind outside the bomb shelter, like a beast's whimper, completely pulled him back to reality.

He looked at Su Liang on the bed with eyes tightly closed, looked at that pale pink old scar below her right collarbone. An indescribable sense of absurdity and fate intertwined in his chest, blocking until he could barely breathe.

Fifty years ago. Half a century of time.

The system matched them together in "Lightless Hospital's" darkness, forced them to kill back-to-back under "Perfect City's" neon, made them complete soul resonance in "Prison Zero's" desperate situation.

Lin Yi had always thought his tacit understanding with Su Liang was forcibly forged by the system in life-and-death trials.

Only now did he understand—their bond had been deeply carved into bone and blood long ago, on that desperate rainy night, the moment that life-selling contract was signed.

"Mm..."

On the bed, Su Liang emitted an extremely weak muffled groan.

That crude sedative for livestock and expired broad-spectrum antibiotics finally took effect. The burning fever on her body was slowly receding, a trace of color returning to her pale cheeks.

Long eyelashes trembled a few times. Su Liang slowly opened her eyes.

## Section 2: Scars as Proof

Newly awakened Su Liang's eyes were still somewhat confused.

Her brain had just experienced Prison Zero's formatting storm, then endured the severe pain of tube removal and physical high fever in reality. At this moment she was like an old machine just rebooted, memory still somewhat fragmented.

She rotated her dry eyeballs and saw Lin Yi sitting by the bed.

"We... made it out alive?" Her voice was hoarse as if scraping on sandpaper.

"Yes. We're out. This is the wasteland Rangers' camp." Lin Yi didn't move, still maintaining that forward-leaning posture, gaze profoundly watching her.

Su Liang noticed where Lin Yi's gaze fell.

She lowered her head, discovering her tattered hospital gown was soaked with sweat, collar open, exposing her right shoulder.

On this wasteland without any sense of security, exposing vulnerable neck and shoulders was extremely dangerous. Su Liang's eyes instantly recovered their coolness and vigilance. She instinctively reached to pull up her collar, covering that old scar.

"What are you looking at?" Su Liang's voice carried a trace of warning coldness.

Lin Yi didn't answer.

He only slowly raised his left arm, and before Su Liang's eyes, rolled up that equally tattered hospital gown sleeve, bit by bit.

On Lin Yi's forearm covered with blue veins and recent new wounds, a deep old knife scar reaching bone lay prominently.

That scar's position, length, and the rough healing marks at its edges all pointed to a specific weapon—a switchblade.

"I'm looking at..." Lin Yi gazed into Su Liang's eyes, voice low and hoarse. "The 'handiwork' I left in this world half a century ago."

Su Liang froze.

Her gaze locked onto that scar on Lin Yi's forearm, pupils violently contracting.

## Section 3: Recognition Across Half a Century

"You..."

Su Liang's breathing suddenly became rapid.

Because of the hibernation pod's deep freeze, physical body time had been halted for fifty years. For them, what happened that rainy night was, in body sensation, merely "a few hours before entering the game"!

That switchblade flashing with cold light.

That madman college student who sold his life to Ark for two million, covered in blood yet more vicious than debt collectors.

"Lin Yi..."

Su Liang murmured this name.

In virtual trials, they only used codenames, or even if they knew names, no one would connect a ruthless character in a game with that teenager briefly encountered in a hospital corridor in reality.

Moreover, though their appearances in trials were their own, under that extreme life-and-death pressure, who had time to carefully identify whether the other was a passerby they'd once seen?

Until this moment, physical body scars became the only iron evidence connecting illusion and reality, past and present.

"You're actually that... idiot who blocked the knife for me in the corridor?"

Su Liang leaned against the headboard, looking at Lin Yi. That vigilance and defensiveness in her eyes collapsed with a roar, replaced by extreme shock, and a trace of... an impulse to cry that even she hadn't noticed.

"I'm an idiot?" Lin Yi laughed self-mockingly, eyes tinged with red. "I don't know who, clearly owing loan shark debts and driven to desperation, after seeing my liability waiver, not only didn't run but actively went to find Ark's people to sign a life-selling contract."

"We're two of a kind." Su Liang bit her pale lips.

The two looked at each other in the crude ward.

No tearful embrace, no sobbing after surviving disaster.

But they could both hear each other's violent heartbeats.

In this devastated fifty years later where all relatives and acquaintances had turned to dust, on this wasteland with only radiation and monsters remaining, they had miraculously found a "fellow" from the same era, who even signed contracts on the same rainy night.

This feeling was like two lone wolves frozen in the Ice Age who, upon awakening, suddenly caught the scent of their own kind.

## Section 4: Hidden Accomplices

"Su Liang, you said then you don't owe favors, you only calculate interest."

Lin Yi rolled down his sleeve, body leaning back against the chair, gaze burning as he stared at her. "So in 'Lightless Hospital's' transit station, I took advantage of the situation, forcing you to sign that IOU for 300 points with 50% compound interest, and you said 'even loan sharks aren't as black-hearted as me.'"

"So, creditor, how should we settle this account now?"

Su Liang was amused by Lin Yi's words into a light cough. It pulled at damaged nerves. She frowned slightly, but the corners of her mouth curved into a smile of genuine meaning.

Not that calculating fake smile in the Pseudo-Human Apartments, but a kind of relief after removing all disguises.

"The account must be settled, of course."

Su Liang looked at the dim flickering incandescent bulb overhead, voice weak yet showing a gritted-teeth fierceness. "However, the rotten loan shark debt from before, with those lenders all dead, has been written off."

"Now, we have a new, bigger bad debt."

Su Liang turned her head, looking at Lin Yi.

"Ark deceived us for fifty years. They not only didn't save your sister, didn't give me settlement fees, but also drained our blood like batteries for fifty years."

"Lin Yi, this breach of contract penalty—just calculating interest is enough to buy their entire tower."

Lin Yi smiled.

He stood up, extending that left hand bearing the black metal brand, stopping in midair.

In that virtual hell called "Infinite Trial," they had guarded against each other, used each other to survive, forming a fragile alliance of interests.

But now, on this cruel real wasteland, when the truth from fifty years ago was revealed, when they confirmed each other as the only remaining, truly "same kind" in this world...

The alliance completely sublimated into a life-and-death bond.

Su Liang raised that equally pale hand with an IV drip, forcefully grasping Lin Yi's hand.

Their hands met in the cold air, transmitting human, scalding body temperature.

"We're not just collecting debts." Lin Yi looked at Su Liang, word by word, voice carrying Asura-like killing intent.

"We're also going to drag that 'deity' that herds humans like livestock from its throne, completely into this wasteland's mud."

"This deal—are you in?"

"In." Su Liang answered coldly, eyes sharp as knives. "Principal and interest together."
