# Chapter 37: Spark of the Asylum

## Section 1: Pain Contagion

The eerie green storm in the garbage dump had just subsided when harsh mechanical sounds came from the depths of darkness.

It was a squad of "jailers" responsible for cleaning up bottom-layer data. They had no facial features, their bodies made of silver-gray liquid metal with cascading bottom-layer code flowing across their surfaces. Their arms had mutated directly into cutting blades crackling with high-voltage arcs, specifically designed to physically shred those "large chunks of garbage" that the storm hadn't torn apart for recycling.

Lin Yi leaned against a mountain of scrap metal, the [Fragment of Reality] in his left palm still faintly burning.

The monster mother who had shielded him from the storm had completely dissipated, but her desperate obsession to protect her child—transcending all logic—now surged through Lin Yi's neural center like boiling magma.

This was an extremely terrifying mental burden. If it were an ordinary person, when receiving this extreme pain, the brain's self-protection mechanism would instantly cause shock or even madness.

But Lin Yi didn't go mad.

After experiencing cold abandonment and extreme guilt, this pain instead became a quenching blade, shattering the fear deep in his heart.

"Uncleared logic remnants detected."

Three liquid jailers locked onto Lin Yi. They had no emotional fluctuations, their cold electronic voices echoing over the garbage field.

The middle jailer raised its high-voltage cutting blade, approaching Lin Yi with absolutely uniform steps. No system panel warnings, no skill cooldown timers—in this hell's bottom layer, Lin Yi was just a mortal prisoner.

But he didn't retreat.

When that cutting blade came slashing toward his face with a piercing buzz, Lin Yi suddenly dodged sideways. Though his body was sluggish without system enhancement, he still relied on muscle memory to narrowly avoid this fatal strike.

Then he made a move that the system logic completely couldn't understand.

He didn't evade the follow-up attack. Instead, he advanced rather than retreated, charging straight into the jailer's liquid metal body!

*Squelch.*

The jailer's other arm, transformed into a spike, easily pierced through Lin Yi's right abdomen. Blood instantly gushed out, staining his gray prison uniform red.

"Cough..." Lin Yi gritted his teeth hard, swallowing the blood surging up his throat. This zero-distance contact was exactly what he wanted.

He raised his left hand with the [Fragment of Reality] and grabbed the jailer's smooth, cold metal head tightly.

"You like cleaning up garbage?"

Lin Yi's eyes were blood red, his pupils reflecting the chaotic code flickering on the jailer's face. "Then taste... the filthiest garbage!"

【Pain Network (Lv.1) · Reverse Transmission!】

*Buzz—!!!*

Lin Yi channeled the intensely concentrated despair, fear, and irrational maternal obsession he had absorbed from the monster mother through the [Fragment of Reality] as a physical conduit, violently injecting it into the jailer's core code without reservation!

It was like forcibly stuffing a virus called "maternal love and despair" into a precision calculator that only understood 0s and 1s.

The jailer's body suddenly froze.

"Error... logic conflict... unable to parse variable..."

Its originally uniformly flowing silver code instantly turned glaring crimson. It couldn't understand why one program would die for another. Its pitiful antivirus processing power overloaded on the spot before this massive human obsession!

*Bang!*

The jailer's head exploded like a watermelon stuffed with firecrackers, bursting into a mass of green waste code.

Lin Yi pulled out the spike from his abdomen, gasping heavily. He glanced at the remaining two jailers, briefly frozen due to their companion's "logic self-destruction," and without hesitation turned and dove into a corroded ventilation duct crevice.

## Section 2: Data Crevice

The ventilation duct was filled with the pungent smell of rust and ozone.

This was Prison Zero's "data crevice," the sewers used to discharge redundant heat and waste code.

Lin Yi covered his bleeding abdomen, crawling with difficulty through the narrow duct. Blood loss made him dizzy, and the backlash from [Pain Network] made his head throb. Those sorrowful memories that didn't belong to him kept flashing back in his mind, torturing his sanity.

But he didn't make a sound.

*Tap... tap... tap.*

Just as he was about to lose consciousness, an extremely faint, rhythmic tapping sound came from the darkness ahead.

It was Morse code.

Lin Yi's spirits lifted. He used his good right hand to tap twice on the metal duct wall in response.

A few minutes later, at a duct junction, he saw that familiar figure.

Su Liang.

She looked equally disheveled, her gray prison uniform covered with engine oil and unknown sticky substances, her originally fair face smudged with black streaks. But when she saw Lin Yi, a barely perceptible relief flashed through those cool eyes.

"You're still alive." Su Liang lowered her voice, reaching out to pull Lin Yi into the relatively spacious junction chamber.

"Can't die yet." Lin Yi leaned against the chamber wall, tearing off a corner of his prison uniform to tightly bind his abdominal wound. "Your hearing wasn't stripped?"

"The system enhancement was stripped, but my hearing was better than normal to begin with." Su Liang frowned, her gaze sweeping over Lin Yi's blood-covered left hand and severe abdominal wound.

But what alarmed her wasn't Lin Yi's injuries—it was his eyes.

Before entering this prison, Lin Yi's eyes had been cold and calculating, like a precise survival machine. But now, though those eyes still held wolf-like ferocity, they carried an extremely heavy, even tragic suppression.

Like a gambler who only cared about his own survival suddenly burdened with the debt of a hundred lives.

"What did you encounter?" Su Liang couldn't help asking. "Your condition isn't right."

"I encountered a debt I can never repay." Lin Yi didn't explain further. He caught his breath and looked up toward the depths of the chamber. "Who are you contacting? You're not the only one in these ducts."

## Section 3: Whispers of Zero

*Crackle... crackle...*

Just as Lin Yi finished speaking, the junction chamber's long-defunct ventilation fan suddenly emitted static noise like an old radio tuning frequencies.

The voice was extremely hoarse and aged, with severe electronic distortion, speaking directly by their eardrums.

"He encountered... the most terrifying thing in this prison. He encountered 'humanity.'"

Lin Yi's muscles instantly tensed, the fragment in his left palm warming slightly.

"Who are you?"

"Who am I?" The voice let out a dry laugh like a broken bellows. "I've been locked here so long that even those self-proclaimed god Observers only call me... Sample 01."

Sample 01!

Lin Yi's heart jumped violently. This was the first awakened person when the Infinite Trial system started, mentioned in the hidden files!

"Where's your body?" Lin Yi looked around warily but saw no signs of any living being.

"Rotted long ago. Rotted in the Ark's nutrient tank decades ago." 01's voice carried deep numbness and exhaustion. "I'm now just a ghost code the system refuses to delete, parasitizing in these data crevices. Where you are now is Prison Zero. This isn't an instance—this is the system's garbage recycling station."

01 began revealing the truth about this hell.

"Those players and NPCs who can't be recycled due to mental collapse or logic conflicts all get thrown here. The system turns them into mindless monsters, letting them slaughter each other. And you, Sample 17..."

The static suddenly became sharp.

"In Perfect City, you injected the purest emotional virus into the mainframe. You became a super bug even the system can't parse. So they stripped you of everything, wanting you to rot here like a dog."

## Section 4: Shadow of Dimensional Reduction

Just as 01 was speaking, the ruins outside the junction chamber suddenly fell into absolute silence.

Not quiet—but the air flow and data noise were forcibly "muted."

"Shut up. Don't move. It's here." 01's voice instantly dropped to minimum, as if even this electromagnetic wave was filled with fear.

Lin Yi and Su Liang simultaneously held their breath, looking out through the duct's louvers.

Above the vast garbage dump below, an object impossible to describe in human language descended.

It was a massive silver-white geometric luminous body constantly shifting seamlessly between a regular tetrahedron and octahedron. It emitted extremely cold light, its surface flowing with waterfall-dense bottom-layer order code.

"That's the Warden." 01 transmitted information through weak static. "This prison's highest antivirus program. It has no physical form—it's the embodiment of the system's absolute rationality."

Below, a massive stitched monster was roaring madly.

But the Warden didn't even pause. The silver-white geometric body just slowly rotated in midair, projecting a light curtain.

The moment the curtain touched the stitched monster, the three-meter-tall creature didn't even scream. Its massive three-dimensional body was like a dimensional reduction strike, instantly becoming a two-dimensional texture with no thickness, then turning into green garbled code and completely "Deleted."

Clean, efficient, not a single wasted movement.

Su Liang broke out in cold sweat. This wasn't some Boss that could be defeated with positioning and skill—this was erasure at the physical law level.

"See that?" 01's voice carried despair. "You can't defeat an antivirus program. Before long, it'll scan this crevice. We'll all be cleaned up."

After the Warden left, that suffocating oppression gradually faded.

Lin Yi leaned against the chamber wall, looking at his blood-covered left hand. He remembered the mechanical jailer that had crashed on the spot because it couldn't parse "maternal love."

"Can't defeat an antivirus program?"

Lin Yi's lips slowly curved into a cruel, mad smile, carrying the desperate aura of someone pushed to the brink.

"01, if the tens of thousands of monsters imprisoned in this prison are all bugs suppressed by the system..."

"Then tell me, if I return all their memories and pain from when they were 'human,' letting tens of thousands of logic viruses go mad simultaneously..."

Lin Yi raised his head, his eyes burning bright.

"Can this antivirus program kill them all?"
