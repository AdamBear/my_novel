# Chapter 43: Flesh Forging

## Section 1: Breaking the Wall

The silver-white regular octahedron exploded in midair, transforming into a violent data stream sweeping through Prison Zero.

Those white pixel storms representing absolute rationality and erasure laws, under the impact of the extremely chaotic virus called "humanity," shattered inch by inch like a massive broken mirror.

"Lin Yi..."

In the sky-filling firelight and disintegrating code, that hoarse, old-radio-like static voice sounded again. It was the ghost code—Sample 01.

Its broken data that had been parasitizing in the ventilation ducts was now burning. It transformed its last traces of existence into a ghostly green flame, crashing viciously into the thick black firewall at the top of Prison Zero.

"Go to the surface... go to reality..." 01's voice gradually dissipated in the firelight, carrying a sense of final liberation. "Let them see what real humans are."

*Boom—!*

The firewall was completely burned through with a large hole.

Outside the door was no longer the void data abyss, but a deep, cold physical darkness.

Lin Yi's virtual body, formatted to only half remaining, finally stopped disintegrating the moment the firewall broke open. He used his last bit of strength to hold the severely comatose Su Liang tightly in his arms.

"Hold on tight."

Lin Yi silently called out in his mind. Then, the two were violently thrown out of the virtual network's boundary by that massive data turbulence, falling like two meteors toward that endless darkness.

No white light.

No cold system settlement sound.

Only a suffocating sensation as if their souls were forcibly stuffed into an extremely cramped, heavy container.

## Section 2: Amniotic Fluid and Engine Oil

*Gurgle... gurgle...*

Suffocation.

Lungs filled with some viscous, sweet-smelling liquid, unable to extract even a trace of oxygen.

Lin Yi's eyes suddenly opened.

Vision was blurry, everything before him was pink liquid bubbling with air. This liquid was too familiar—in the fourth instance "Eternal Night Train," the "Navigator" brain in the jar was soaked in this kind of nutrient solution used to maintain biological activity.

"Cough...!"

Lin Yi instinctively wanted to struggle, but found his hands and feet were full of small IV tubes, and at his neck spine, a black metal tube as thick as a child's arm was connected.

That was a real physical tube, the hardware interface for the Ark mainframe to read their consciousness.

"Get... away from me!"

Lin Yi roared in the liquid, though no sound came out. But the [Nirvana] gene in his body, upon contact with his real flesh, erupted with an extremely terrifying beast-like strength.

He suddenly raised his hand, forcibly tearing off the IV tubes on his arm. Real blood instantly spread through the pink nutrient solution.

Then, he reached back with both hands, gripping the black tube connected to his spine tightly, kicked against the glass chamber wall, and pulled outward with all his strength!

*Squelch—!*

With a string of flesh and neural mucus, that thick metal tube was brutally pulled out.

This was no longer pain synchronization, but pure, physiological agony. Lin Yi's vision went black, but he gritted his teeth, swung his fist, and smashed it viciously against the thick bulletproof glass chamber wall.

*Bang!*

First punch—spider-web cracks appeared in the glass.

*Bang!*

Second punch—the glass shattered.

Hundreds of liters of nutrient solution mixed with glass shards poured out, washing Lin Yi heavily onto the cold metal floor.

"Cough, cough, cough... urgh..."

Lin Yi lay on the ground, vomiting mouthfuls of nutrient solution from his lungs. The air was filled with pungent engine oil smell, strong disinfectant smell, and lingering bloody smell.

Smells without any system modification, real enough to be nauseating.

He struggled to raise his head, looking at another still-operating cultivation chamber nearby.

Inside was Su Liang.

Her ears in her physical body were also bleeding, staining the nutrient solution red. She was completely unconscious.

Without any hesitation, Lin Yi picked up a sharp glass shard from the floor, smashed Su Liang's cultivation chamber, dragged her out of that viscous "amniotic fluid," and manually pulled out the tube from the back of her head.

"Wake up... Su Liang! Breathe!"

Lin Yi pressed hard on Su Liang's chest. After a few compressions, Su Liang suddenly coughed up a large mouthful of pink liquid. Her chest began heaving violently, but she still showed no signs of waking. Her brain nerves were too severely damaged resisting the formatting storm. Her physical body had also fallen into deep protective coma.

## Section 3: Real Wasteland

Lin Yi placed Su Liang outside the broken glass pile and stood up supporting himself on the cold steel operating table.

He finally saw clearly where he was.

This was a circular factory so large you couldn't see the end at a glance. From the towering metal dome, pale searchlights cast cold light.

This was inside Ark One—the wetware processing factory.

Around him, thousands upon thousands of identical cultivation chambers were densely arranged. Inside were countless pale human bodies. Some were still sleeping, some were being brutally cut for organs by mechanical arms, some had already had their brains removed, leaving only empty shells being transported by conveyor belts to the incinerator.

Those defective monsters he had awakened in Prison Zero were, in reality, these "waste materials" being cut up like spare parts.

Lin Yi habitually raised his right hand, swinging it sharply downward in the air, trying to summon the system panel.

The air remained calm.

No light blue holographic projection, no [Life Points], no [Mental Points], even that cold mechanical prompt sound had disappeared without a trace.

All the game's data coating had been stripped away.

Getting shot would kill, bleeding would cause shock, broken bones couldn't be instantly repaired with points. This was the cruel physical reality world with no margin for error.

"The system's gone."

Lin Yi murmured in a low voice, looking at his pale hands covered with real scars and calluses.

## Section 4: Black Brand

Just when he thought he had lost all power, his left palm suddenly transmitted a scalding burning sensation.

Lin Yi suddenly opened his left hand.

In the virtual world, that [Fragment of Reality] he had gripped tightly and ultimately used to detonate the Warden's core hadn't disappeared with the system's collapse.

It was originally a metal fragment from the Ark's gate in reality.

And now, this fragment had completely fused with his physical body in reality.

In his left palm appeared a pitch-black metal brand. The brand's shape was like a broken sharp blade, with several black metal filaments at the edges spreading like blood vessels to his wrist.

*Thump. Thump.*

Lin Yi could feel this metal brand pulsating along with his heartbeat.

Although the system panel had disappeared, the [Nirvana] gene in his body hadn't been stripped away. On the contrary, after breaking free from data suppression and returning to this tortured real flesh, that power was like a beast breaking free from its cage, frantically roaring through his limbs and bones.

His muscle fibers became extremely tough, his hearing and vision far exceeded normal humans, and even the bloody hole in his back where the tube was pulled out was stopping bleeding and scabbing at an incredible speed.

*Clack, clack, clack...*

Heavy, orderly metal footsteps came from the end of the factory corridor.

Red alarm lights began spinning frantically above the factory.

"Illegal de-chambering event detected in D-14 zone. Two cultivation bodies awakened. Execute physical elimination."

A squad of Ark guards in fully enclosed white exoskeleton armor, carrying real electromagnetic pulse rifles, was rapidly advancing toward them.

These weren't low-level AI from the game, but Ark's most elite semi-mechanical cyborgs.

Lin Yi looked down at the unconscious Su Liang, then at the sharp, thick bulletproof glass shard he had knocked off the cultivation chamber.

He had no gun, no equipment, wearing only a soaked white hospital gown.

But he felt no fear whatsoever.

Lin Yi gripped the glass shard tightly. The black brand on his left palm faintly emitted dark red light. A materialized killing intent erupted from his pitch-black eyes.

"The real world, huh..."

Lin Yi's lips curved into a bloodthirsty cold smile, like a lone wolf driven to desperation yet revealing its sharpest fangs.

"Let's see if your guns are faster, or my life is harder."
