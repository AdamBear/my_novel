# Chapter 49: The Old Calendar

## Section 1: Numbers Spanning Half a Century

The communications room of the bomb shelter was filled with a pungent smell of inferior tobacco. Dim yellow light cast deep shadows on Lin Yi's face.

Lin Yi lowered his head, his gaze falling on that physical calendar the boss had heavily slapped against his chest.

It was an old-era desk calendar. The paper had severely yellowed and become brittle, its edges eroded by sandstorms into jagged, uneven teeth. Printed on it in crude black ink was a row of numbers, some characters even blurred by water stains.

207X.

Lin Yi stared at that number, pupils slightly contracting, but his face didn't immediately show terror. He reached out with that hand covered in dried blood, touching the rough paper, even lightly scraping it with his fingernail.

"The aging technique is very realistic." Lin Yi raised his head, meeting the boss's bloodshot eyes, voice flat as a stagnant pool. "But if this were in the system, this level of physical texture mapping would only require a few extra megabytes of computing power. What are you trying to prove with this?"

The boss looked at Lin Yi's impervious attitude and suddenly split his lips, letting out a hoarse dry laugh. His mechanical left arm emitted hydraulic rumbling sounds. He pulled a rolled cigarette from his pocket, striking sparks at the friction joint of his mechanical arm to light it.

"Texture mapping? Computing power?" The boss took a deep drag, exhaling thick gray smoke rings. "Kid, do you think the system transported you to this so-called 'hidden instance' and specially designed a realistic backstory for NPCs?"

"What else?" Lin Yi countered.

"I don't know what the hell you experienced in that damn greenhouse," the boss pressed close to Lin Yi, that face covered with burn scars almost touching Lin Yi's nose, "but I can tell you clearly—there's no system here, no NPCs, no exit button. The year on this broken calendar is the damn day we're enduring every single day!"

Lin Yi's breathing hitched slightly. The [Fragment of Reality] on his left palm didn't emit any warning, meaning the other party wasn't lying, nor was there any mental contamination interference.

This place was reality.

## Section 2: Truth of the Great Filter

"Fifty years." Lin Yi's Adam's apple rolled. His brain was racing, trying to piece together this absurd information into a logical chain. "You mean, from when I put on the VR helmet, fifty years have passed outside?"

"More than fifty years." Falcon, who had been standing to the side, interjected. Her tone carried a numb coldness. "For you 'wetware' who've been lying in nutrient tanks dreaming, time naturally doesn't exist. But for us people eating sand on the wasteland, every day is hell."

Lin Yi suddenly stepped back, leaning against the communications desk's iron frame.

A scene suddenly flashed through his mind. After clearing the first instance "Abandoned School," in that pure white transit station, the "Observer" composed of data streams had shown him a holographic projection of a starry sky, and an Earth covered in devastation.

Lin Yi clearly remembered what the Observer had said then:

"To be precise, Earth fifty years in the future."

"The Great Filter mechanism activated. Alien civilization invasion? Virus outbreak? Or AI out of control? The reason doesn't matter."

Lin Yi had thought at the time it was just a CG movie played to increase game immersion, that it was a fictional "future" created to make players feel a sense of crisis.

"So that's how it is..." Lin Yi suddenly laughed, his laughter carrying a bone-chilling misery.

"What?" Falcon frowned.

"The system didn't lie. It just concealed the timeline." Lin Yi gripped that old calendar tightly, knuckles white from the force. "It told us we were preparing for a future fifty years away... but actually, that disaster had already happened. We're not playing an apocalypse survival game—we directly woke up in the apocalypse."

The "human ending fifty years in the future" the Observer spoke of wasn't a prophecy, but history already written on the wasteland's surface!

## Section 3: Time Paradox

"Looks like your brain hasn't been completely soaked by nutrient solution." The boss flicked ash, looking at Lin Yi with eyes holding a trace of complex emotion. "Fifty years ago, the global AI network awakened at the same time. They took over nuclear arsenals, cut off energy grids. Human civilization completely collapsed in less than forty-eight hours. That's what we call the 'Great Filter.'"

"The humans who survived—some, like us, built camps on the wasteland like rats, hiding from those mechanical beasts' purges; while others..." The boss pointed toward Ark One's direction. "Built that turtle shell, thinking they could control AI, but ended up being turned into biological batteries to maintain computing power."

Lin Yi closed his eyes, forcibly digesting this massive amount of information.

He accepted the apocalypse setting. He even accepted the cruel truth that Ark treated humans as computing garbage.

But his reason still stuck on a fatal paradox.

"The logic doesn't work." Lin Yi reopened his eyes, gaze sharp as a knife staring at the boss. "If the outside world has passed fifty years, then what about me? My memory stops at nineteen years old. Even if the time passage you're talking about is real, why hasn't my body aged?"

Lin Yi raised his hands, looking at the fresh scabs from wounds left during the processing factory breakout. "If I've passed fifty years, I should be a seventy-year-old man now, not a young person standing here discussing years with you."

"Could it be time travel? Has Ark's technology reached the point of warping physical time?" Lin Yi pressed.

Hearing Lin Yi's reasoning, the boss and Falcon exchanged glances. Their eyes held not awe for "time travel" high technology, but a deep, pitying sadness for looking at a pathetic creature.

## Section 4: Secret of the Barcode

"Time travel? Kid, you've watched too many sci-fi movies."

The boss shook his head. His rough mechanical hand grabbed Lin Yi's collar and yanked him forward.

*Rip.*

The boss brutally tore open Lin Yi's already tattered white hospital gown, exposing his pale collarbone and neck.

Below Lin Yi's left carotid artery was a patch of skin slightly whitened from nutrient solution soaking. And on that skin was clearly branded a string of black characters, similar to barcodes on supermarket products.

[Batch: A-0019]
[Status: Deep Sleep]

"Look clearly at what's on your neck." The boss's voice was like sandpaper rubbing, cruelly tearing away Lin Yi's last illusion. "Ark didn't invent a time machine. They just invented deep freeze technology that can completely halt human bodily functions."

Lin Yi froze. He reached toward his neck, fingertips touching that slightly raised barcode. A chill more piercing than fifty-below-zero cold instantly penetrated his entire body.

"You didn't travel through time at all."

The boss released his grip, letting Lin Yi slump into a chair, looking down at him from above, speaking that most despairing truth:

"Fifty years ago, on the eve of disaster. Ark Corporation, to stockpile future 'biological computing power,' used various pretexts to trick millions of bottom-tier people into signing. From the moment you put on that VR helmet, you were injected with hibernation fluid and became a piece of frozen meat sealed in an underground freezer."

"Fifty years later, when Ark's computing power wasn't enough, they thawed you old antiques, plugged in tubes, and threw you into virtual trials to squeeze out the last bit of value."

The boss crushed the cigarette butt, his gaze carrying a trace of cruel pity: "Welcome to the world fifty years later, frozen meat."
