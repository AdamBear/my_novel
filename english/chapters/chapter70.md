# Chapter 70: Black Rock City's Rules

## Section 1: Vertical Slums

The convoy slowly drove down the extremely steep mountain dirt road along the pit edge.

As depth increased, the howling wind and sand sounds from the wasteland surface were gradually replaced by a chaotic, disorderly industrial roar. Black Rock City, this shelter built in an old-era super open-pit mine, showed Lin Yi its suffocating cyber-wasteland ecology.

On the pit's inner walls, countless "shantytowns" welded together from shipping containers, abandoned train cars, and steel plates hung densely. These steel cages connected via thick steel cables and crisscrossing metal catwalks, forming a bottomless vertical labyrinth.

The air was filled with pungent sulfur smell, coal smoke, and the sour-rotten stench of hundreds of thousands of people's accumulated excrement.

But within this filth, a deformed prosperity was mixed in. Various signs pieced together from old-era neon tubes flickered red and blue light in the darkness: [Black Water Bar], [Prosthetic Black Market], [Pure Water Exchange].

"Really fucking spectacular." Falcon gripped the steering wheel, watching those thugs and mercenaries around who stuck their heads out, eyeing their convoy with greedy gazes.

The three hundred "frozen meat" remnant forces following behind the off-road vehicle huddled tightly together. They instinctively feared this chaotic environment, but Lin Yi standing on the vehicle ahead was their only spiritual pillar in this demon's den.

That mercenary leader whose wrist was crushed was now being held single-handedly by Lin Yi in the off-road vehicle's truck bed, pointing directions like a guide dog. His intact hand clutched his broken wrist tightly, face pale with pain, not daring to say another harsh word.

Before absolute physical crushing, wasteland tough guys understood submission better than anyone.

## Section 2: Iron Blood's Outpost

The convoy finally stopped at a massive platform in the pit's mid-upper level. This was originally a mine cart dispatch hub, now surrounded by a circle of heavy sandbags and barbed wire, with several heavy machine guns mounted on top.

"This... this is our Iron Blood Mercenary Corps' outer outpost." The mercenary leader looked at Lin Yi covered in cold sweat, voice trembling. "This area is very safe. Water Gang and Cathedral people don't dare come here. You... you can temporarily garrison here."

Lin Yi tossed him down like trash.

Soon, a group of fully armed mercenaries walked out from inside the barbed wire. Leading them was a scarred man wearing exoskeleton chest armor with one blind eye. He glanced at his subordinate kneeling on the ground wailing, then at the [Ark Spider Mech CPU] flickering with blue light in Lin Yi's hand, his single eye flashing with extremely intense greed.

"I'm the Iron Blood Mercenary Corps' deputy commander. You can call me 'Mad Dog.'" The scarred man saw Lin Yi wasn't easy to provoke, suppressing the impulse to directly double-cross him, pointing at that chip. "Using this thing to exchange for a garrison, you're very generous."

"I said, I also need the best antibiotics, and a set of sterile surgical equipment capable of micron-level nerve suturing."

Lin Yi stood on the vehicle, looking down at him from above. "I have more chips. As long as the goods arrive, this one is the deposit. But if you want to play dirty..."

Lin Yi's left hand suddenly clenched, veins bulging on the back of his hand, the alloy truck bed beneath his feet emitting an overburdened groan. "I don't mind skinning you so-called 'local snakes' one by one."

Mad Dog's eye corner twitched. He looked at Lin Yi, then at the Ranger boss and Falcon beside him holding heavy firepower.

"Antibiotics and purified water, delivered in an hour." Mad Dog snorted coldly. "As for neural surgery equipment, that stuff is scarce on the black market. Need time to requisition from the Water Gang's old hospital ruins. Wait here peacefully. As long as you don't cause trouble, Iron Blood guarantees your safety."

Finished speaking, Mad Dog led his subordinates out of the platform, yielding this massive hub area to Lin Yi.

## Section 3: Tyrant's Territory

The gate closed. This several-thousand-square-meter steel platform temporarily became a shelter for these three hundred plus refugees.

Lin Yi jumped down from the vehicle. He didn't immediately rest, but heavily slammed those two crates of [High-Concentration Synthetic Nutrient Paste] seized from the Ark patrol in the platform's center.

All the refugees, including that leader called Old Zhao, instantly swallowed hard, eyes green with hunger.

"Old Zhao." Lin Yi called out.

"Here! Boss Lin, your orders!" Old Zhao scrambled over, still gripping tightly that iron pipe in his hand.

"Divide people into three groups." Lin Yi's voice echoed across the empty platform, carrying undeniable dictatorial will. "First group, reinforce all surrounding barbed wire, push abandoned mine carts over as cover; second group, check all ventilation pipes and sewers on this platform level, weld shut all dead corners that people can crawl through; third group, prepare to light fires."

Old Zhao froze. "Then... then this food..."

"Those who finish work get one tube of nutrient paste each. Those who don't work, or slack off, don't expect a single drop of water."

Lin Yi drew the P-19 kinetic pistol from his outer thigh, pulling the bolt, coldly surveying this group of flowers raised in greenhouses.

"On my turf, there's only one rule: I give you food, you give me your lives. Understood?"

"Understood! We understand!"

Old Zhao immediately turned around, roaring at the crowd like a dutiful overseer. "What are you all standing around for?! Want to starve to death? Move!"

Survival desire overwhelmed exhaustion. Over three hundred ragged survivors frantically operated like worker ants, moving sandbags, pushing mine carts, welding shut ventilation openings.

In less than an hour, this originally empty platform was forcibly transformed by this group driven by survival instinct into a heavily defended iron barrel formation.

## Section 4: Noise in the Shadows

"You're a natural-born dictator."

The Ranger boss leaned against the car door, watching those refugees desperately working for half a tube of nutrient paste. "However, the Iron Blood Mercenary Corps definitely isn't that kind-hearted. Wealth moves hearts—that mech chip is enough to make them red-eyed. They're just feeling us out now. Once they figure out we only have these few guns..."

"I know."

Lin Yi wiped the rifle in his hands. "So I never expected them to protect us. These gangs, sooner or later need to kill a batch."

Just then, Su Liang, who had been sitting in the truck bed with a face pale as paper from nerve damage, suddenly painfully covered her right ear.

"What's wrong?" Lin Yi immediately stepped forward, pressing her shoulder.

"Too noisy..."

Su Liang's breathing became rapid, cold sweat sliding down her forehead. Having broken free from the system's shielding, her ears undergoing physical mutation were forced to receive this underground black market's massive and chaotic sound waves.

"Lin Yi, this city... is rotten through."

Su Liang closed her eyes, extremely difficultly stripping useful information from the massive noise, voice trembling. "I heard... below us, probably several hundred meters deep in the waterways. There are many people... no, those aren't people."

"They're chanting sutras... they're using knives to cut their own flesh, and there's a kind of... sound like mushrooms growing and peeling..."

"They're saying: 'Flesh is weak, corruption ascends.'"

Hearing this phrase, the Ranger boss's single eye suddenly widened, face instantly becoming extremely ugly.

"Damn it, it's the 'Corruption Cathedral.'" The boss cursed through gritted teeth. "Those cult lunatics who worship wasteland aberrations, treating nuclear radiation and spore viruses as divine miracles!"

Lin Yi's eyes instantly became ice-cold.

He remembered what that AI mainframe called "Father" said in the fifth instance [Perfect City]—the truth of the "Great Filter" was to evade some kind of high-dimensional spore in the universe that fed on human chaotic emotions.

Could it be that unspeakable horrific catastrophe had already taken root in Black Rock City's depths?

"Old Ghost!" Lin Yi turned to look at the medic. "Su Liang's ears can't hold out much longer. As soon as that neural surgery equipment arrives, immediately perform physical bionic modification on her!"

"No matter what demons and monsters are hiding in this city..."

Lin Yi gripped his rifle tightly, the black brand on his left hand faintly heating in the dim light.

"Anyone who dares block my path, I'll kill my way through this entire pit."
