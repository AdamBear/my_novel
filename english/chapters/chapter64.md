# Chapter 64: Wasteland's First Lesson

## Section 1: Deadly Sunlight

When the first ray of sunlight after fifty years penetrated the lead-gray clouds and struck these just-awakened "old humans" without any obstruction, it brought not warmth, but deadly scorching.

The wasteland's atmosphere had long lost its ozone layer protection. The sunlight carried high-intensity ultraviolet rays and lethal cosmic radiation, mixed with choking radioactive dust in the air, transforming into an invisible chemical storm.

"It hurts... my skin..."

Agonized screams began erupting from the crowd. Those bodies that had been soaked in constant-temperature amniotic fluid in nutrient pods for a full half-century were pale and tender as newborn infants. Now exposed to the real wasteland environment, after less than five minutes, many people's shoulders and backs rapidly developed large patches of red spots, even beginning to blister.

The icy gale wrapped coarse sand grains, scraping across their naked bodies like knives.

"Cough cough cough!"

Some people, having inhaled large amounts of dust carrying trace radiation, knelt on the ground violently vomiting, the gastric fluid they coughed up streaked with glaring blood. Due to extreme temperature differences and low immunity, the physically weaker elderly and women began collapsing in waves, falling into shock amid convulsions.

This was the wasteland's welcoming ceremony. No newbie protection period, no health point buffer. Nature used the most brutal physical laws to conduct the first round of cruel "great purge" among millions of survivors.

Su Liang stood beside Lin Yi. She wore that gray combat suit seized from Ark, though tattered, it at least blocked the wind and sand. Even so, she could still feel stinging pain from exposed skin.

"They won't make it." Su Liang looked at the hellish scene below, brow furrowed.

"That's their own business." Lin Yi stood on a massive piece of shattered armor plating fallen from the dome, eyes cold and hard as iron.

## Section 2: Refusing to Be Savior

"Please... save us!"

A middle-aged man who had dreamed of being a tycoon in "Perfect City," now naked and covered in mud, crawled to the bottom of the armor plate. Looking up at Lin Yi standing high above holding a kinetic pistol, he saw a lifeline.

"You have a gun... you must know where there's shelter, right? Take us there! I have money, I used to be CEO of a listed company, as long as you protect me..."

Bang!

Lin Yi didn't even look down. He casually fired a shot at the sand beside the man's feet. The electromagnetically accelerated bullet exploded a scorched crater, scaring the man into screaming and collapsing.

The gunshot instantly silenced the agitated, desperate crowd. Tens of thousands of eyes filled with fear and pleading uniformly looked toward Lin Yi.

"Shut up. Listen carefully."

Lin Yi's voice wasn't loud, but across the empty ruins, it carried a bone-chilling oppressive force.

"First, I'm not your savior. I fired because you're too noisy."

"Second, your old money, status, position—here they're not even worth toilet paper. In this world, the only hard currency is food, water, and the ability to kill."

Lin Yi pointed his gun barrel at the Ark One ruins behind him, continuously experiencing internal explosions and billowing smoke.

"The cage collapsed, but those dead guards have clothes and body armor, the collapsed supply lines have industrial purified water, the bottom warehouses might still have unburned synthetic starch."

Lin Yi looked down from his height at this trembling flock, the corner of his mouth curling into a bloodthirsty, cruel sneer.

"Either stand here and freeze to death in the wind and sand, get roasted by the sun."

"Or turn around like real beasts and charge back into the ruins. Go rob those dead people's clothes, scavenge those machine wreckages! Whoever can grab supplies gets to live through today!"

## Section 3: Foundation of a Tyrant

The crowd fell silent for three seconds.

Then, humanity's most primitive survival instinct finally overcame fear and civilized-era dignity.

"Clothes... I need clothes!"

Someone let out the first roar. Immediately after, thousands upon thousands of naked people rushed toward the dangerous Ark One ruins like crazed zombie hordes, eyes red. They fought viciously over a single guard corpse's exoskeleton armor, bit each other to lick a single drop of coolant leaking from a ruptured pipe.

Civilization's fig leaf was completely torn away, wasteland law formally established at this moment.

"You're a natural-born tyrant."

The roar of engines came from the side. The Ranger boss and Falcon drove their heavily modified all-terrain assault vehicle, stopping at the ruins' edge.

The boss dragged his somewhat rusty mechanical left arm, watching those "frozen meat" slaughtering each other in the ruins over half a piece of clothing, his single eye full of shock. He had thought Lin Yi would be helpless facing millions in misery.

"Boss, we can't take this many people." Falcon glanced at the survivors covering the mountains and plains. "Our Ash Camp can't even feed a thousand. Without antibiotics and sufficient purified water, 90% of these people will die within three days from radiation sickness and infection."

"I never planned to take everyone."

Lin Yi holstered his pistol, jumping down from the armor plate with movements as agile as a panther. "I only need those who can crawl out of the ruins alive, who dare to rob and kill for survival."

"The remaining 90% are wasteland fertilizer." Lin Yi's tone was flat without a ripple.

The Ranger boss looked deeply at Lin Yi. He'd fought on this wasteland for over a decade, seen countless cruel warlords and raider chieftains. But he admitted, this youth with a biological age of only nineteen, the cold rationality in his bones was more terrifying than any wasteland warlord.

## Section 4: Nirvana Second Stage

Suddenly, Lin Yi's body froze abruptly.

Without warning, he half-knelt on the sandy ground, hands desperately clawing at the cracked earth.

"Lin Yi? What's wrong?" Su Liang immediately sensed something amiss, quickly stepping forward to help him, but the instant she touched Lin Yi's skin she jerked her hand back from the heat.

Lin Yi's body surface temperature was frighteningly high, as if a fire was burning in his internal organs.

"Don't... touch me."

Lin Yi gritted his teeth, veins bulging on his forehead. He violently tore open his hospital gown, gasping heavily.

This wasn't illness, nor radiation infection.

In Prison Zero, he personally severed his last attachment to his sister, experiencing soul fragmentation and reconstruction in the absolute formatting storm; in reality, he manually unplugged the physical mainframe, personally smashing the idol that had imprisoned humanity for fifty years.

Those extreme pains, suppressions, and angers finally reached the critical point of quantitative change at this moment.

Moreover, the harsh air on the wasteland filled with trace radiation hadn't destroyed his cells, but instead acted like a powerful catalyst, madly surging into his lungs.

【Warning... (system notification invalid)】

In Lin Yi's self-perception depths, that gene sequence named [Nirvana], like a snake shedding its skin, broke through the first stage's human physiological limits amid waves of dizzying tearing sensations.

His bone density underwent extremely microscopic yet lethal changes in a short time, becoming harder than titanium alloy from the old era. His retinas were covered with an extremely thin gray film, allowing him to see clearly the outlines of mechanical behemoths kilometers away through blinding sunlight and sandstorms.

More terrifying was his left hand.

That [Fragment of Reality] brand not only didn't disappear, but spread outward in a circle of dark red patterns, rooting deeply into his forearm muscles like blood vessels.

Whoosh—

Lin Yi slowly stood up.

The air around him seemed to produce distorted ripples from the extreme body temperature. He took a deep breath—the original lung-stinging radioactive dust sensation disappeared, replaced by an extreme sense of power.

He was no longer a player who needed to rely on system panels to fight.

He was a true apex predator belonging to this wasteland.

"Let's go."

Lin Yi turned his head, those pitch-black eyes faintly flickering with dark red light. He looked at Su Liang, then at the Ranger boss.

"To your camp. We still have a hard battle to fight."
