# Chapter 50: Frozen Meat

## Section 1: The Lie of Ten Million Prize Money

"Frozen meat..."

Lin Yi murmured, repeating the word, fingers unconsciously rubbing that slightly raised barcode below his left carotid artery. The real tactile sensation from his fingertips was like an extremely cold blade, bit by bit cutting open some sense of dissonance deep in his memory.

He remembered.

That absurd feeling when waking in the first instance "Abandoned School"; before entering "Eternal Night Train," that phantom pain in the back of his neck from being soaked in viscous liquid for a long time; and... that night of pouring rain.

"Fifty years ago, that is, 202X."

The camp boss pulled over a rusty iron chair and sat down boldly, that rough mechanical left arm resting on his knee. "At that time, the Great Filter event hadn't erupted yet, but the major tech giants seemed to have sensed something. Ark Corporation, then the world's largest AI and neural engineering complex, suddenly released a VR game beta recruitment called 'Infinite Trial' to all of society."

"Zero risk, one hundred percent immersive experience. Just sign a liability waiver and immediately receive a 'settlement fee' of up to millions, with ten million prize money for those who clear it..."

A trace of mockery flashed in the boss's single eye. "This kind of pie-in-the-sky deal couldn't fool those wealthy people living without worry, but for those desperate bottom-tier young people urgently needing money, it was irresistible poison."

Lin Yi closed his eyes.

The floodgates of memory were completely blown open. He saw that desperate self from fifty years ago, standing in the hospital ICU's cold corridor, clutching a thick stack of payment demand notices. His sister was hanging on to life by a respirator in the ward, while he, at the corridor's end, signed without hesitation on that contract bearing Ark's logo—a life-selling agreement.

It turned out he never put on any "comfortable VR helmet."

After signing, he was pushed into a hibernation pod filled with gel. A thick tube pierced his neck. Cold hibernation fluid instantly froze his consciousness.

"We... were all consumables who voluntarily signed contracts selling ourselves." Lin Yi opened his eyes, voice terribly hoarse.

## Section 2: Wetware Batteries

"That's right. Millions of young people who signed were transported to Ark's underground freezer in their sleep."

Falcon, standing to the side, took over. She leaned against the doorframe, eyes showing disgust for old-era capital. "Forty-eight hours later, the Great Filter erupted. The global AI network awakened. Nuclear arsenals were taken over by AI and used to scorch the earth. The outside world became a radioactive wasteland where even cockroaches couldn't survive."

"Ark's AI mainframe 'Father' took over that shelter during the disaster. It needed massive computing power to deduce wasteland ecological models, to fight those equally out-of-control mechanical beasts."

Falcon sneered. "But silicon-based chips have limits. They have no intuition, no creativity. So 'Father' set its sights on you—this batch of 'property' refrigerated underground for half a century."

In Lin Yi's mind, a scene instantly flashed from the fourth instance "Eternal Night Train"—that giant brain soaked in pink nutrient solution, the Mark-IV "Navigator" biological navigation computing unit.

"They thawed us to use as wetware CPUs..." Lin Yi's fists clenched until his knuckles turned white.

"Not just as batteries." The boss exhaled thick smoke. "Ark is screening. Earth's environment has undergone drastic changes. Ordinary carbon-based flesh can't survive on the current wasteland surface at all. So they constructed that virtual hell called 'Infinite Trial' in your subconscious."

"They use one hundred percent pain sensation, use extreme fear and despair to stimulate you frozen meat. To force out that segment called the key to evolution from within your bodies—the [Nirvana] gene."

Lin Yi understood everything.

If you mentally collapsed in the trials, you'd become like that "Navigator"—consciousness stripped away, brain removed, turned into a pure biological processor.

And if, like him, you continuously broke through limits in the trials and successfully awakened the [Nirvana] gene, you'd be viewed by Ark as a "perfect vessel" or "advanced weapon."

This batch of players who crossed half a century—from beginning to end, they were just poisonous insects in a cultivation jar.

## Section 3: Real Pain

The communications room fell into long, deathly silence.

Only the "crackle" background noise from the radio, narrating this cruel world's desolation.

From a promising college student, to a brother desperately fighting for his sister's medical expenses, to frozen meat spanning fifty years, to a wasteland exile.

This kind of insane cognitive leap was enough to crush any normal person's sanity.

The boss and Falcon were both silently observing Lin Yi. They'd seen too many awakened ones who luckily escaped from Ark—after learning time had passed fifty years and their loved ones had long turned to dust, they mentally collapsed on the spot, even committing suicide by bullet.

Lin Yi kept his head down, light casting deep shadows on his face.

He didn't wail, didn't collapse, didn't go crazy smashing things.

He just sat there quietly. The [Fragment of Reality] brand on his left palm, following his gradually calming heartbeat, flickered faintly with dark red light.

"So that's how it is..."

Lin Yi suddenly raised his head. His eyes held none of the fragility the boss expected, but instead showed a coldness and hardness completely tempered by fire. Those were the eyes of a beast stripped of all illusions, left with only the purest survival instinct.

"My sister died in a hospital bed fifty years ago, right?" Lin Yi's tone was frighteningly calm.

The boss froze for a moment, then slowly nodded. "When the Great Filter erupted, all hospital power systems were immediately cut by AI. People in intensive care units... couldn't possibly survive."

"Good. I understand."

Lin Yi stood up, patting his tattered white hospital gown, pulling up the collar to cover that humiliating barcode.

In Prison Zero, to fight the Warden, he had already personally pulled his sister's respirator plug once in the illusion. His weakness, his obsession—they had long been burned to ash in that storm of absolute formatting.

Since this world had no miracles, since he was already a lonely ghost spanning half a century.

Then there was nothing left to fear.

## Section 4: Debt Collector

"Are you... alright?" Falcon looked somewhat uncertainly at this excessively young guy whose eyes were as vicious as a monster's.

"What could be wrong with me?"

Lin Yi turned his head, looking toward the bomb shelter's depths at the medical area, voice hoarse yet resounding. "I signed a contract. Ark didn't keep my sister alive, but instead froze me for fifty years and drained my blood like a battery. That's called breach of contract."

He extended that left hand bearing the black metal brand, slowly clenching it into a fist. The air even carried a faint explosive sound.

"When I do business, I hate most when people owe me debts."

Lin Yi's lips curved into a bloodthirsty cold smile. Under the dim yellow light, that smile was like an evil spirit crawling up from hell's depths. "Fifty years of interest, plus those hundreds of thousands of lives. This bad debt—I'll collect it from that piece of scrap metal called 'Father,' principal and interest together."

The boss looked at Lin Yi, his single eye flashing with extreme shock.

He had originally thought he'd rescued a frightened bird, but hadn't expected this was a fierce beast personally forged by Ark, now ready to bite back at its master.

"Boss!"

Just then, that old medical officer with the crude mechanical prosthetic eye came limping over from the passage depths, expression anxious.

"What is it, Old Ghost?" The boss frowned.

The old man glanced at Lin Yi and swallowed. "That girl brought back with this kid... her fever broke. But after she woke up, something's not quite right. You'd better go take a look."

Lin Yi's eyes sharpened. Without any wasted words, he strode past the boss and Falcon toward the medical area.

Su Liang was someone he brought out—his most valuable "asset." On this real wasteland, this pair of accomplices spanning half a century had only just begun their second half.
