# Chapter 67: Baptism of Radiation and Flesh

## Section 1: Eating Raw Meat and Drinking Blood

Wind and sand howled beneath the dark red sky.

The C-class aberrant giant lizard over five meters long now lay across the wilderness like a mountain of flesh. Its blood was dark green, emitting a strong earthy smell and faint fluorescence—a sign of massive radiation accumulation in its body.

Four hundred "frozen meat" who had just escaped from Ark's greenhouse stared fixedly at this monster's corpse.

Their stomach acid churned madly. Fifty years ago, they ate refined synthetic foods, takeout, and cuisine; in Ark's hibernation pods, they survived on pure intravenous nutrient solution.

Eating raw radioactive aberrant rotting meat? This exceeded the bottom line of human civilization.

"Urgh..." Someone merely smelling that odor knelt on the ground dry heaving.

"I... I won't eat..." A young girl in tattered pajamas cried and backed away. "This meat definitely has viruses, eating it will cause mutation! We'll die!"

"Don't eat, you'll freeze to death now."

Lin Yi looked at her coldly, without a trace of pity.

He walked to the lizard's corpse, using that high-frequency vibration knife covered in green blood to cleanly carve out a two-pound chunk of raw meat from the lizard's fattest hind leg.

In front of everyone, Lin Yi didn't light a fire, directly stuffing that blood-streaked raw meat into his mouth, chewing heavily.

The coarse meat fibers were extremely tough, like chewing oil-soaked tires. That blood carrying trace radiation slid down Lin Yi's throat, instantly transforming into a scalding fireball in his stomach.

But Lin Yi didn't even furrow his brow.

His [Nirvana] gene second stage was frantically operating. That dense membrane covering his bones and internal organs easily filtered out the radiation toxins in the meat. High-intensity stomach acid began breaking down these coarse proteins within seconds, converting them into extremely pure heat and kinetic energy, replenishing the stamina he consumed during the fight.

"He... he ate it..." The survivors looked at Lin Yi like a monster.

"I'll eat!"

That center-parted man whose gun barrel Lin Yi crushed with one hand (now the informal leader of this group) suddenly gritted his teeth, first to rush forward.

He used a sharp rock to cut off a piece of lizard meat, closing his eyes and stuffing it in his mouth.

"Gulp." The center-parted man forcibly swallowed, then his stomach spasmed, but he covered his mouth tightly, not vomiting it out.

With the first, came the second.

Survival instinct finally completely tore away civilization's fig leaf. Hundreds of ragged humans, like a pack of crazed hyenas, pounced on that lizard corpse. They tore with hands, smashed with rocks, even bit directly with mouths.

The scene was bloody, savage, filled with the wasteland's most primitive tension.

## Section 2: Natural Selection

The bald driver sitting in the off-road vehicle cabin, watching this scene, couldn't help swallowing.

"These flowers raised in the greenhouse... are really fucking ruthless."

The Ranger boss leaned against the car door, using his only remaining right hand to light a cigarette, his single eye flickering with complex light. "It's not that they're ruthless, it's that Lin kid who's ruthless. He's using this beast's meat for the final 'genetic selection.'"

The boss saw clearly.

Wasteland native aberrations all contained high concentrations of radiation and unknown viruses. Even when Rangers hunted, they only dared eat after repeated roasting and filtering.

Eating raw like this, for ordinary people's immune systems, was no different from drinking poison.

Sure enough, less than half an hour later.

"Ugh... it hurts... my stomach..."

Screams began appearing in the crowd.

That young girl who had cried saying she wouldn't eat, unable to bear the hunger, also took a bite. Now she lay on the sand convulsing violently. Dark red patches rapidly appeared on her skin surface, foaming at the mouth, eyes rolled back.

Not just her. Over fifty people successively collapsed, wailing in extreme pain. Their immune systems couldn't resist the radioactive meat's erosion, organs rapidly failing.

The remaining three hundred plus stopped chewing, terrified, looking at their companions on the ground, then at Lin Yi standing high above.

"Save them! Aren't you the boss! Save them!" Someone shouted in breakdown.

"I only guarantee you have meat to eat, not that you won't die from eating it."

Lin Yi wiped the green blood from his mouth with the back of his hand, eyes cold as an emotionless idol.

"In the greenhouse, when sick the system gave you medicine. But on the wasteland, nature is the system. If your flesh can't even digest this bite of meat, then you don't deserve to breathe the radioactive dust under this sky."

Lin Yi turned, looking at the Ranger boss.

"Time's about right. Take those who can stand up, continue the journey."

## Section 3: Shadow of Black Rock City

Nightfall.

Temperature dropped to minus twenty degrees. The convoy camped in an old-era highway tunnel half-buried by yellow sand.

Outside, gales raged. Inside the tunnel, a bonfire was lit using discarded synthetic tires and dead wood.

By the bonfire, Lin Yi, Su Liang, the Ranger boss, and Falcon sat in a circle.

Among those three hundred plus survivors, another twenty died tonight. The remaining three hundred thirty huddled tightly in the tunnel depths.

But surprisingly, those who survived, though tortured by radioactive meat with vomiting, diarrhea, and high fever, after enduring the initial rejection reaction, their body surfaces actually grew an extremely fine keratin layer similar to radiation resistance.

"This is human genetic resilience." Lin Yi looked at those remnant forces still gripping rocks and metal shards in their sleep. "As long as you don't die, you'll evolve."

"But they need antibiotics and clean water." Su Liang wrapped in her coat, face still somewhat pale. "My radar can hear their heartbeats are extremely chaotic. If we can't find supplies before tomorrow night, half of these three hundred will die."

"So we must go to 'Black Rock City.'" The boss tossed a piece of wood into the fire. "Only there has enough supplies to feed hundreds."

"What exactly is Black Rock City?" Lin Yi picked up a branch, poking the fire, flames reflecting on his sharply defined face.

The boss took a deep drag on his cigarette, tone becoming exceptionally grave.

"Black Rock City was originally a giant open-pit mine near Jiangcheng fifty years ago. After the Great Filter, surviving humans hid in mine shafts at the pit bottom. After fifty years of reproduction and expansion, it became an underground black market with over a hundred thousand population."

"It's the largest lawless zone in this sector. The reason Ark's mechanical legions never conquered it is because Black Rock City possesses heavy defensive artillery left from the old era, and... an extremely complex underground air defense network."

Falcon sneered, taking over. "Of course, it's no paradise either. Black Rock City has no laws, only pure jungle law. Currently the city is carved up by three major forces: the 'Water Gang' controlling the underground purification system, the 'Iron Blood Mercenary Corps' holding old-era munitions, and a group of lunatics worshipping mutation and evolution—the 'Corruption Cathedral.'"

"Though we Rangers have a reputation on the wasteland, in the eyes of those Black Rock City local snakes, we're just a bunch of wandering paupers." Falcon looked at the refugees behind Lin Yi. "You bring these three hundred combat-ineffective burdens into the city, not only won't you get supplies, but those gangs will capture them as 'meat pigs' for slaves."

## Section 4: Entry Fee

"Meat pigs?"

Lin Yi looked at the dancing flames, the corner of his mouth curling into a bloodthirsty arc.

He raised his left hand bearing the black brand. Under the dancing firelight, that [Fragment of Reality] brand seemed alive, faintly flowing with dark red light beneath the skin.

"Boss, in Black Rock City, what's most valuable?" Lin Yi asked.

"Three kinds of hard currency: clean water, old-era firearms and ammunition, and... Ark technology." A trace of greed flashed in the boss's single eye. "Though Ark is humanity's enemy, their exoskeleton armor, high-frequency vibration knives, especially those advanced mech core processing chips, can sell for astronomical prices on Black Rock City's black market."

"Understood."

Lin Yi stood up, patting sand off himself. He turned his head, looking toward the pitch-black blizzard night outside the tunnel.

"Since we have no money to buy passage, let's go stock up on goods."

"Stock up? Where?" The boss froze. "In this wilderness..."

Lin Yi didn't answer him.

He looked at Su Liang sitting on the ground.

"Radar." Lin Yi's voice echoed in the empty tunnel, carrying a chilling killing intent. "Earlier on the road, didn't you hear at our nine o'clock direction, an Ark sweeper team is camping?"

Su Liang opened her eyes. Those cool pupils instantly comprehended Lin Yi's insane intent.

"Distance fifteen kilometers. A fully equipped twelve-man exoskeleton squad, plus two guardian-class spider mechs." The corner of Su Liang's mouth also curved into a cold smile. "Their camp has two crates of high-concentration synthetic nutrient paste."

"Enough to feed these three hundred for three days."

Lin Yi drew the P-19 Enforcer kinetic pistol seized from the processing factory from his waist, pulling the bolt, chambering a round.

"Falcon, lend me your heavy electromagnetic rifle."

Watching Lin Yi and Su Liang, this "crazy duo" spanning half a century, the Ranger boss and Falcon were completely speechless.

Leading hundreds of nearly starving refugees, instead of fleeing quickly, they wanted to counter-raid fully armed Ark regulars in a blizzard night?

"What the fuck kind of monster are you..." Falcon cursed while unhesitatingly tossing that heavy electromagnetic rifle to Lin Yi.

"Me?"

Lin Yi caught the rifle, turning toward the wind and snow.

"I'm just a debt collector in urgent need of money."
