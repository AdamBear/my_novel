# Chapter 63: Glass Rain

## Section 1: Amniotic Fluid Ebb

Deep underground.

In Ark One's abyss-like massive cryogenic storage, ten million indicator lights that originally flickered with faint blue light turned dazzling crimson red at the same moment.

Accompanied by deafening exhaust valve roars, the constant-temperature system maintained for a full half-century completely collapsed. Three million hibernation pods densely arranged on honeycomb-like metal racks simultaneously initiated emergency revival procedures.

Hiss—!!!

Tens of thousands of tons of ice-cold gel and pink nutrient solution, like ebbing amniotic fluid, poured down from various chamber drainage ports, converging at the bottom of the cryogenic storage into a river emitting a pungent chemical smell.

Click. Click. Click.

Three million pod doors popped open simultaneously.

"Cough cough cough... urgh..."

Violent coughing and vomiting sounds, like detonating a bomb in an empty valley, instantly shattered this underground tomb's fifty years of deathly silence.

Countless pale, emaciated, naked bodies, along with life support tubes not yet fully removed, tumbled out from hibernation pods like dumplings, heavily crashing onto metal grating floors covered with gel.

They were college students, workers, bankrupt merchants, desperate gamblers from fifty years ago.

In their memories, one second ago, they had just signed their names on the "Infinite Trial" beta test agreement with its million-dollar settlement fee, hopefully donning that black VR helmet.

And now, bone-chilling cold and turbid air scraped like knives at their lungs that hadn't contacted the real world for fifty years.

"Nurse... nurse!"

A boy who looked like a high school student desperately groped through the gel, frozen purple all over. "This game is too cold... I want to quit! Did my two million get deposited into my mom's account?!"

"Where are my clothes? Which hospital basement is this?!"

"System? Customer service! Is this damn pain synchronization bugged?!"

Three million people's confusion, panic, and anger converged together, forming a sound wave sufficient to overturn the roof. In this massive "morgue," no one answered their questions.

They didn't know their families had long since turned to ash under nuclear explosions fifty years ago; they knew even less that the company promising them tens of millions in prizes had drained them like batteries for half a century.

## Section 2: Collapsing Greenhouse

Core mainframe room.

Lin Yi half-knelt in nutrient solution that didn't reach his ankles, gasping heavily. His left hand still gripped tightly that severed main control cable, the scorched black burns on his arm from high-voltage electricity shocking to behold.

"Lin Yi, let's go! This place is collapsing!"

Su Liang waded through the water and rushed over, supporting Lin Yi's arm. Though her hearing no longer had system enhancement, in the real physical dimension she could still clearly hear the scalp-numbing metallic twisting sounds from overhead.

"The mainframe's anti-gravity magnetic field failed!" The Ranger boss dragged his scrapped mechanical left arm, using only his remaining right hand to hold a flashlight, shining it toward the ceiling too high to see. "Ark's external defense dome lost energy support—millions of tons of armor and radiation-proof glass could crash down any moment!"

"Take the emergency passage, go to the upper cryogenic zone!"

Lin Yi gritted his teeth and stood up. The violent power of the [Nirvana] gene in his body entered a brief weakness period after unplugging, but his eyes were brighter than ever.

The four ran like racing death itself, frantically climbing upward through the maintenance shaft behind the mainframe room.

As altitude increased, the chaotic clamor grew louder.

When Lin Yi kicked open the ventilation louvers of cryogenic zone D and climbed up, the scene before him was a living depiction of hell.

Tens of thousands of just-awakened "frozen meat," naked, crashed around like headless flies in gel-filled corridors. Trampling, screaming, slipping. Some people went into shock on the spot from severe rejection reactions, then were trampled underfoot by the panicked crowd, literally crushed into pulp.

"Don't push! I'm Ark's senior programmer! Let me out first!"

"Someone stepped on my hand! Help—"

## Section 3: The Tyrant's Gunshots

"Too chaotic." Falcon looked at this flock of lambs penned in a greenhouse for fifty years. "This kind of trampling—they'll trample half themselves to death before the dome collapses."

"Then make them quiet down."

Lin Yi coldly pushed aside several people blocking the way. He didn't help the fallen weak, but instead strode to a high platform in the middle of the corridor, drawing the P-19 kinetic pistol from his waist.

In this underground world that had lost order, the only thing that could make people calm was absolute violence.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

Lin Yi fired three shots at the ceiling. The deafening gunshots echoed through the enclosed cryogenic zone, drowning out all screams.

The crowd instantly quieted. Tens of thousands of terrified eyes uniformly looked toward the man on the high platform.

His white hospital gown had long been stained dark red with blood and machine oil, the pitch-black brand on his left hand like a demon's eye, his entire body emanating a terrifying killing aura of someone who crawled out from mountains of corpses and seas of blood.

"All of you shut up."

Lin Yi's voice wasn't loud, but carried a chilling威压. "I know what you're thinking. You're looking for game customer service, looking for your two million settlement fee, looking for your families."

"I'll tell you the truth now."

Lin Yi extended his finger, pointing at the ceiling overhead that was continuously dropping dust and rubble.

"Ark Corporation went bankrupt fifty years ago. You weren't playing a game—you were frozen like pork for a full half-century! The outside world has been destroyed, your families are all dead! Not even ashes can be found!"

An incredulous collective gasp erupted from the crowd.

"Impossible! You're lying!" The high-school-aged boy broke down crying. "I just called my mom yesterday!"

"Shut up! Coward!" Lin Yi's gun muzzle suddenly pointed at the boy, scaring him into choking back his sobs.

Lin Yi surveyed the confused, fragile members of his species below. He knew that feeding them "hope" and "warmth" chicken soup now would be suicide. To keep this group alive, he had to become a tyrant more terrifying than demons.

"I'm not here to save you. I just happened to smash the cage holding you. Now, the cage is collapsing."

Lin Yi holstered his gun, drew the tactical knife from his outer thigh, pointing toward the end of the passage.

"Those who don't want to be crushed into pulp, shut your mouths, put away those cheap tears, stand up like human beings and run outside!"

"Anyone who blocks the way, anyone who pushes—I'll slaughter them first!"

## Section 4: Wasteland's First Dawn

Rumble—!!!

The moment Lin Yi finished speaking, Ark One experienced the most violent earthquake since its construction.

"The dome is breaking!" Su Liang shouted.

That layer covering directly above Ark, blocking wasteland sandstorms and lethal radiation for a full fifty years—the massive translucent dome finally reached its physical limit under the dual pressure of losing magnetic field support and external storms.

Crack—!

A massive fissure hundreds of meters long spread across the dome.

Then, like heaven and earth collapsing.

Millions of tons of special radiation-proof glass and alloy framework thunderously fell from ten thousand meters high.

"Run!!!" The Ranger boss roared, using his mechanical arm to shove the crowd.

Under death's threat, the crowd finally erupted with survival instinct, following Lin Yi and the Rangers toward the external emergency escape passage in a mad dash.

Crash—

Those massive glass shards fell like an extremely gorgeous yet deadly rainstorm, viciously smashing onto Ark's building shell. Some who ran slowly were instantly sliced into fragments by the falling glass rain.

Lin Yi fiercely protected Su Liang, weaving through the sky full of glass rain and collapsing metal supports. His [Nirvana] gene frantically warned in crisis, leading the human tide behind him, forcibly tearing open a path to survival through the ruins' gaps.

Bang!

Lin Yi kicked open a deformed external safety door.

Howling wind surged in.

No disinfectant smell, no nutrient solution's sweet cloy—only that rough, pungent wind carrying intense gunpowder and dust scent of the wasteland, viciously slamming into everyone's faces.

Millions stumbled out of that collapsing greenhouse, collapsing onto the desolate earth.

The false filter overhead finally completely shattered.

For fifty years, the first ray of real sunlight penetrated the thick lead-gray volcanic clouds above the wasteland, carrying despairing dim yellow and coarseness, mercilessly illuminating these naked, slime-covered "old humans."

Lin Yi stood atop the ruins.

He looked at the mechanical behemoths wandering on the distant horizon, looked at the endless scorched earth beneath his feet, then turned back to glance at the millions of eyes behind him filled with fear and helplessness.

The system was completely dead.

But the real survival trial, on this wasteland called "Earth," had only just begun.
