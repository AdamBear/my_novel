# Chapter 40: Absolute Formatting

## Section 1: Dimensional Reduction Crushing

"Now, let me teach you what truly illogical means!"

Lin Yi's roar still echoed over the containment area. The [Nirvana] gene in his body went into full rampage, crimson energy mixed with the desperate obsession of tens of thousands of defective monsters, transforming through the [Fragment of Reality] in his left hand into a high-heat black blade that seemed capable of cutting through space. His legs exerted force violently, shattering the metal grid beneath his feet. Like a black meteor flying in reverse, he charged straight at the silver-white geometric body frozen in midair from processing overload.

In his imagination, this would be the ultimate battle between flesh and machine.

But he was wrong. Completely wrong.

Facing Lin Yi's fatal strike capable of destroying any conventional Boss, the "Warden" representing the system's highest absolute rationality didn't even make any evasive or defensive gesture. The massive regular octahedron just paused slightly in midair, the flickering red-green error codes on its surface instantly unifying into pure, emotionless pale white.

【System Highest Command: Initiate bottom-layer physical formatting.】

No earth-shattering explosion, no deafening roar.

The entire Prison Zero, the instant this cold electronic voice fell, was directly stripped of "color" and "sound."

Lin Yi's charging body was like hitting an invisible, intangible air wall with absolute rule barriers, forcibly frozen in midair.

Then, destruction descended.

The tens of thousands of defective monsters below launching suicidal charges couldn't even let out a final roar. Their massive, deformed bodies full of unwillingness and anger, when swept by the pale white light, were instantly "flattened" from three-dimensional flesh and blood into two-dimensional textures without any thickness, then turned into white pixels dancing in the sky, completely disappearing into nothingness.

No bloodstains, no wreckage, no traces of ever having existed.

This was dimensional reduction strike—the creator's most merciless erasure of the created.

## Section 2: Pixel Disintegration

*Bang!*

Lin Yi fell heavily from midair, crashing onto the floor that had already begun to virtualize.

He tried to grip the fragment in his hand and stand up again, but discovered with horror that he had lost sensation in his right leg. He looked down to see his right leg from the knee down was no longer flesh and blood, but had become a pile of green square pixels constantly peeling off and drifting away.

"Ugh..." Lin Yi gritted his teeth, veins bulging on his forehead.

No pain. This formatting stripped away neural feedback. He could only watch helplessly as his body was disassembled bit by bit into the most basic data code.

Not just him—Su Liang nearby suffered the same catastrophe.

"Su Liang!" Lin Yi struggled to turn his head, pupils contracting sharply.

Su Liang curled up on the ground, both hands covering her ears tightly, blood frantically pouring from between her fingers, eye corners, and nostrils. In this world full of blinding white light, a "formatting white noise" inaudible to ordinary people filled the air.

For normal people, it was just dead silence; but for Su Liang whose hearing had been enhanced to the extreme by the system, this high-frequency white noise capable of pulverizing bottom-layer code was like tens of thousands of red-hot steel needles frantically stirring her brain matter.

Her proud hearing was completely destroyed in this instant. She couldn't hear Lin Yi's call, couldn't hear the system countdown. The entire world became a desperately chaotic blur in her perception.

The two were like dead fish on a chopping board waiting to be deboned, stripped even of the qualification to struggle in this erasure storm targeting the entire logic foundation.

## Section 3: Absolute Dead End

【Formatting Progress: 30%... Environmental redundant data cleared.】
【Formatting Progress: 50%... Logic conflict individuals being deconstructed.】

The Warden's emotionless voice was directly branded as text on their gradually disintegrating retinas.

Lin Yi's left arm also began pixelating. The [Nirvana] gene in his body was frantically roaring, boiling, trying to resist this erasure. But he was like a strongman with infinite strength locked in a gravity-less, leverage-less vacuum sphere—full of killing intent but unable to find any entity to attack.

The Warden had already dispersed its core into this sky-filling white storm. It was everywhere yet nowhere to be found.

"Fuck..."

Lin Yi spat out a mouthful of bloody saliva that turned into white garbled code before hitting the ground. He lay on the floor, looking at Su Liang nearby convulsing from severe pain, completely deafened. An unprecedented sense of powerlessness and despair completely submerged him.

Was he really going to die here?

After experiencing countless betrayals, stepping over countless corpses to reach today, even personally severing his obsession with his sister to rebel against the system, was the final result still just a one-click delete casually pressed by a deity?

## Section 4: Faint Pulsation

Just as Lin Yi's consciousness began to blur with his body's disintegration, an extremely subtle sensation suddenly came from his left palm.

*Thump.*

*Thump.*

It was the [Fragment of Reality] gripped tightly in his left hand.

In this white storm where everything was formatted into nothingness, this reality fragment forcibly invaded from the physical world into the virtual system hadn't been pixelated. On the contrary, it was now pulsating in Lin Yi's palm with an extremely weak and irregular frequency.

Lin Yi's pupils on the verge of dispersal suddenly refocused sharply.

His survival instinct honed through trials, like lightning, split through the fog of despair in this moment.

Why was it pulsating?

To completely format Prison Zero's massive data in just a few seconds required extremely terrifying computational load. This level of processing overload would inevitably cause the Ark mainframe's physical core to generate enormous heat and data redundancy.

To prevent the mainframe from burning out, the Warden must open the exhaust port connecting to "surface reality (physical server)" during the formatting process!

The pulsation the fragment sensed was the "spatial vibration" produced when that physical exhaust port continuously opened and closed in the data storm!

If he could thrust the [Fragment of Reality] into that exhaust port in this instant, it would trigger a dual logic explosion of virtual data and physical reality, completely destroying the Warden!

This was the only, fleeting chance for survival!

Lin Yi gripped the fragment tightly, trying to find the exhaust port's coordinates in the sky-filling white storm.

But it was useless.

Everything was blinding white light and dancing pixels. That exhaust port was continuously making high-speed irregular movements in the storm, impossible to capture with the naked eye.

His body's disintegration had reached seventy percent. Lin Yi's vision was already darkening. The chance for survival was right before his eyes, yet he was as powerless as a blind man.
