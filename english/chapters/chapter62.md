# Chapter 62: Unplugging

## Section 1: Amniotic Fluid Rupture

Crack—!

Accompanied by teeth-grinding shattering sounds, the military tactical knife in Lin Yi's hand, borrowing the high-dimensional destructive power remaining in the [Fragment of Reality], pierced through that ten-meter-diameter giant cultivation tank without any obstruction.

The originally impenetrable bulletproof glass instantly spread dense spiderweb-like cracks radiating from the knife tip as center.

"No! Stop!!"

Inside the glass tank, William Arkad's twisted human face attached beneath the massive brain tissue let out an extremely shrill scream.

Bang!

The high pressure differential caused those cracks to completely collapse within zero point one seconds. Tens of tons of pink nutrient solution poured out like a bursting dam, the massive impact directly blasting Lin Yi away, heavily smashing him into the ruins behind.

The entire core machine room instantly became a vast ocean. The sweet, cloying nutrient solution smell in the air, mixed with black viscous liquid seeping from the bottom of William's brain, emitted a nauseating rotten stench.

The massive brain tissue, having lost the buoyancy of the nutrient solution, crashed heavily onto the metal base at the bottom of the cultivation tank like a lump of unsupported rotten meat.

"Ahhhh... it hurts... what is this feeling..."

William's pair of white blind eyes violently bulged out. For fifty years, he had been soaking in constant-temperature, sterile nutrient solution with all physical pain nerves severed, fancying himself a lofty deity.

But now, when the cold air contacted his overdeveloped, completely undefended cerebral cortex, even just the slight friction of air currents was no different from ten thousand arrows piercing his heart.

The god finally experienced mortal suffering.

## Section 2: Torch in the Dark Forest

"You fool! You have no idea what you've done!"

William's face rubbed painfully against the base, his voice echoing through the machine room via the remaining broadcast system, carrying a despair like the apocalypse descending. "The shield has disappeared... the emotion blocking network is broken! The spores will see us!"

The massive holographic screen on the machine room wall began flashing frantically. The blue stealth network that originally represented Earth's coordinates was rapidly disintegrating, replaced by countless red dots representing "awakening" and "pain," like igniting a blazing beacon in the dark universe.

"Millions of souls suppressed for fifty years... they're waking up! Their fear will attract that high-dimensional monster! It feeds on emotions!" William shrieked madly, trying to use the last data streams to control several engineering mechanical arms to repair the glass tank.

"Then let it come and feed!"

Lin Yi stood up from the nutrient solution that didn't even reach his ankles. His body was covered with pink slime and his own blood, those pitch-black eyes burning with wildness more violent than any cosmic catastrophe.

"Antelopes burying themselves in the dirt to suffocate while hiding from lions—is that what you call salvation?"

Lin Yi strode through the water, facing those mechanical arms trying to block him, the black brand on his left palm erupting with dazzling red light. He smashed through a mechanical arm's hydraulic joint with one punch, his voice deafening.

"If that whatever-the-hell spore really dares to come, then we'll forge guns fiercer than it, cannons more ruthless than it! Drag it down from its high dimension and beat it to death alive on this wasteland!"

"This is what evolution really means!"

"Madman... you humans are all illogical madmen..." William muttered in despair.

Standing in the rear, the Ranger boss suddenly grinned upon hearing this exchange, letting out a rough, wild laugh. He raised the large-caliber revolver with his only remaining right hand and pulled the trigger at the ceiling.

"Well said! I've been eating sand on this wasteland for decades, never feared any alien mold!" The boss spat out bloody saliva. "Falcon, cover him!"

"Roger!" Falcon raised her electromagnetic rifle, picking off the defense robots trying to approach Lin Yi one by one.

## Section 3: Physical Unplugging

Lin Yi stepped on the glass shards covering the ground, rushing to the metal base of the cultivation tank.

William's brain was desperately writhing, trying to transfer his consciousness data to backup servers through the thick fiber optic cables connected to the base.

"Su Liang! Where's the main control nerve?!" Lin Yi roared.

Su Liang closed her eyes, letting the nutrient solution submerge her calves. Her hearing at this moment penetrated the chaotic water sounds and alarm sounds, precisely locking onto the target.

"Directly below! The thickest black data line! The highest frequency data stream is escaping inside!"

Lin Yi didn't hesitate for a moment. He leaped up, jumping onto the slippery metal base.

"Stop! Lin Yi!"

William's twisted face was right under Lin Yi's feet. He finally set aside his divine arrogance, beginning to plead like a dying old man. "Without my computational power scheduling, Ark's life support system will collapse! Once those millions of frozen meat wake up, without food, without antibiotics, they'll slaughter each other on the wasteland and all starve to death!"

"You're not saving them, you're starting a massacre!"

Lin Yi's movements paused for half a second.

He knew William was telling the truth. Suddenly awakening millions of humans who had been pampered in a greenhouse, whose memories still remained in the "peaceful era" of fifty years ago, and throwing them into this wasteland full of radiation and monsters—what awaited them would absolutely be a hell more cruel than the trials.

But Lin Yi's eyes remained cold and hard as iron.

"Freedom has always required bloodshed."

Lin Yi crouched down, both hands gripping tightly that black main control fiber optic cable connected to William's brainstem, as thick as an adult's arm.

The [Nirvana] gene in his body was squeezed to its limit at this moment. The muscles in both arms bulged high, even audibly tearing muscle fibers.

"Their lives—let them fight for on the wasteland themselves!"

"Break... for me!!!"

Accompanied by a wild roar that echoed throughout the machine room, Lin Yi's feet stomped hard on the metal base, his waist and abdomen suddenly exerting force.

## Section 4: Twilight of the Gods

Riiip—!!!

Amid extremely harsh tearing sounds, that main control neural cable gathering Ark's highest computational power was forcibly ripped out from William's brainstem by Lin Yi using pure physical violence!

Black high-voltage arcs and green data corruption instantly erupted like a fountain, splashing all over Lin Yi.

"Ahh—"

William emitted a final meaningless noise. His massive brain tissue, the instant it lost data connection, rapidly blackened and withered at a visible rate like an oxidizing apple.

On that twisted human face, the white blind eyes completely lost their luster, becoming a pool of lifeless rotten flesh.

The "deity" who had ruled this wasteland for fifty years, who had penned millions of humans, was thus unplugged at the physical level by the most primitive muscular force.

Hummm—!!!

With the mainframe's complete death, the lights throughout the entire core machine room, and even all of Ark One, began violently flickering on and off.

The originally cold mechanical synthesized voice now became red text on white background filled with corruption, frantically scrolling across all screens:

【CRITICAL ERROR: Main brain "Father" offline.】
【Underlying logic collapse... life support system protocol invalid.】
【Highest directive changed: Initiating "Great Awakening" program.】
【Cryogenic storage permissions unlocked... hibernation pods opening...】

The metal earth beneath their feet began trembling violently, more intensely than any previous time. This trembling didn't come from external attacks, but from Ark One's massive underground cryogenic storage.

Millions of hibernation pods spanning half a century were simultaneously draining their cold gel. Millions of faint heartbeats were reviving beneath this wasteland.

Lin Yi slid down from the base as if drained of strength, gasping heavily. His left hand was burned pitch black by the high voltage, but he still gripped tightly that severed data cable.

"Lin Yi!"

Su Liang waded through the water and ran over, catching him as he swayed unsteadily.

"Is it over?" The Ranger boss warily watched the servers around them continuously bursting with sparks.

"No."

Lin Yi raised his head, looking at the heavy metal ceiling overhead that was emitting harsh twisting sounds from losing magnetic field support. That was Ark One's internal protective dome.

He knew the real survival battle had only just begun.

"The system is dead." Lin Yi pulled at the corner of his mouth, revealing a smile tinged with the taste of blood. "Now, it's the real world's turn to go mad."
