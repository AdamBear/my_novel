# Chapter 68: Hunt in the Blizzard

## Section 1: Ghosts on the Ice Field

The wasteland blizzard was sharper than knives.

Temperature had dropped to minus thirty degrees. The gale swept up coarse sand and ice crystals, forming white whirlwinds in the pitch-black night sky capable of tearing ordinary people's skin.

Fifteen kilometers distance, without vehicles, was a death march with ten-to-one odds for ordinary people.

But in the vast wind and snow, two black figures advanced at a terrifyingly steady pace.

Lin Yi walked ahead, using his body to break through the oncoming gale. His [Nirvana] gene second stage frantically operated in this extreme physical environment, metabolism squeezed to an incredible efficiency. The radioactive raw meat in his stomach had long been digested by strong acid into purest heat, continuously supplying his limbs and body.

He didn't even feel cold. That dense bone layer and mutated subcutaneous tissue, like a perfect constant-temperature wetsuit, locked his body temperature tightly inside.

"How much farther?" Lin Yi didn't turn back, asking in a low voice through the tactical earpiece seized from Ark guards.

Su Liang followed closely behind him, stepping in the deep footprints Lin Yi left in the snow. She was wrapped in that tattered military coat, though frozen with a bluish face, her eyes were exceptionally bright.

After breaking free from the system's "white noise" suppression, on this wilderness with only wind sounds, her auditory radar became unprecedentedly clear.

"Three kilometers." Su Liang's voice trembled slightly in the howling wind and snow. "They haven't moved. Two guardian-class spider mechs are doing counterclockwise cross-patrol around the camp perimeter. Twelve infantry split into two groups, one in windproof tents, the other outside by a fire."

"Can you hear what kind of fire they're using?" Lin Yi asked a seemingly irrelevant question.

"The burning sound is very stable, no crackling noise from firewood... it's high-energy solid fuel rods." Su Liang immediately gave the answer.

The corner of Lin Yi's mouth curved into a cruel arc in the darkness.

High-energy fuel rods meant an extremely large heat source. Under the blizzard's cover, these Ark regulars thought staying by a warm heat source was safe, not knowing this massive temperature differential was the fatal flaw for breaking their high-tech defenses.

## Section 2: Physical Stealth

Twenty minutes later, the two reached a leeward slope less than five hundred meters from the Ark camp.

Lin Yi lay prone in the snow, observing the camp ahead through his scope.

Twelve Ark soldiers wearing white cold-weather exoskeleton armor gathered around a portable heater emitting ghostly blue light. On both sides of the camp, two massive spider mechs with six mechanical legs continuously rotated their head-mounted infrared thermal imaging scanners.

"The spider mechs' thermal imaging can scan mice within a kilometer." Su Liang lay beside Lin Yi, pressed tightly against the cold ground. "As soon as we show ourselves, those two mechs' heavy machine guns will tear us to shreds."

"Thermal imaging works by capturing temperature differentials."

Lin Yi put down his gun and suddenly began removing his clothes. He took off his warm tactical jacket, leaving only a thin undershirt, then grabbed the frozen mud mixed with ice shards and radioactive dust from the ground, unhesitatingly smearing it on his body and face.

The icy mud water contacted his scalding skin, instantly forming an ice shell.

"Are you insane?!" Su Liang's eyes widened. "Even if your genes can resist radiation, this hypothermia rate will cause muscle death within a minute!"

"I want hypothermia."

Lin Yi smeared the last handful of ice and snow on his neck. His body surface temperature rapidly dropped to below freezing under physical intervention, nearly matching the surrounding environment. Deep in his retinas, the [Nirvana] gene frantically warned, trying to reheat his body, but he forcibly suppressed it with willpower.

"Now, in their thermal imaging, I'm just a rock."

Lin Yi grabbed the heavy electromagnetic rifle borrowed from Ranger Falcon, voice cold as a real corpse. "You stay here and call out positions. If I don't kill them all within three minutes after my first shot, run back to Black Rock City yourself."

Finished speaking, Lin Yi slithered like a cold viper, silently sliding into the wind and snow ahead.

## Section 3: Blind Sniping and Hand-Tearing

The wind and snow perfectly concealed Lin Yi's tracks.

He crawled along the ground, his ice-mud-covered body passing under the spider mechs' red scanning beams without triggering any alarms.

Fifty meters. Thirty meters. Ten meters.

Lin Yi stopped in a snow pit extremely close to the right spider mech. His fingers were somewhat stiff from cold, but he still steadily raised that heavy electromagnetic rifle.

"Su Liang, where's the left mech's core weak point?" Lin Yi asked in a breath voice through the comm channel.

"Abdomen three inches left, there's gear meshing noise there, thinnest armor." Su Liang's answer came without hesitation.

"Good."

Lin Yi didn't use his eyes to aim at that left mech blurred in the wind and snow, but completely trusted Su Liang's callout, raising the barrel without even activating the electromagnetic rifle's laser sight.

On the real wasteland, there were no system lock-on red frames, only instinct and absolute trust in companions.

Click. Lin Yi pulled the trigger.

Hum—Bang!!!

Blinding blue electromagnetic pulses instantly illuminated the blizzard night sky. A high-explosive armor-piercing round screamed out at five times the speed of sound, extremely precisely hitting the left spider mech's abdomen a hundred meters away!

Boom!

The violent explosion directly flipped that several-ton mech, burning parts scattering everywhere.

"Enemy attack! Thermal imaging failed! Switch to sonar lock!"

The camp instantly exploded. Twelve Ark soldiers rapidly entered combat status. The intact right spider mech suddenly turned its head, gun barrel aimed at Lin Yi's firing direction.

But Lin Yi was no longer there.

The instant he fired, Lin Yi directly discarded that cumbersome electromagnetic rifle, drew the high-frequency vibration knife from his waist, legs suddenly kicking off!

The [Nirvana] second stage's terrifying explosive power manifested fully at this moment. Like an ancient tyrannosaur bursting forth, he instantly crossed ten meters distance, charging directly beneath the right spider mech!

"Die!"

Lin Yi gripped the knife in reverse with both hands, aiming at the mech's support leg's most fragile hydraulic joint, viciously stabbing in! The high-frequency vibrating blade cut through tungsten steel alloy like tofu. Then Lin Yi used arm strength comparable to a hydraulic press, violently yanking outward!

Riiip—!

High-pressure coolant sprayed all over Lin Yi. The massive mech lost balance, collapsing thunderously.

## Section 4: Blood-Colored Entry Token

"He's under the mech! Fire!"

The guards finally discovered this ghost-like attacker. Dense pulse barrages wove into a death net.

"Three o'clock direction, three! Nine o'clock direction, four!" Su Liang's voice urgently rang in the earpiece.

"Roger."

Lin Yi's body frantically weaved through the collapsed mech wreckage. He had no gun, but at this distance, he himself was the most terrifying weapon.

He charged into the three o'clock formation. Facing gun barrels sweeping toward him, he didn't dodge, left hand suddenly reaching out, grabbing the scalding barrel, forcefully bending it!

Crack!

The high-strength alloy barrel was forcibly bent before Lin Yi's abnormal bone density. Immediately after, Lin Yi delivered a powerful knee strike, viciously smashing into that guard's chest.

Bang! The guard's chest armor instantly caved in, rib-cracking sounds clearly audible in the wind and snow, the whole person flying out like a broken sack.

This wasn't virtual combat with health point deductions—this was the most primitive, bloodiest physical crushing!

"Monster... he's a monster!"

The remaining guards, watching this man covered in ice mud tearing through their exoskeleton armor like cardboard, finally felt fear. Their tactical training became meaningless before this absolute physical violence.

Less than two minutes.

Only the last guard remained in the wind and snow. He retreated in terror, trying to press his communicator to call Ark headquarters. "Requesting support! This is Seventh Patrol, encountering..."

Squelch!

Lin Yi appeared behind him like a ghost, knife extremely precisely piercing through his rear neck armor gap, severing the cervical spine.

Thud. The last corpse fell in the snow, blood rapidly freezing into ice.

The entire camp was deathly silent, only the central heater still emitting ghostly blue light.

"Finished."

Lin Yi pulled out the knife, casually wiping blood off on the corpse. The ice shell on his body had shattered from intense movement, body temperature rising again, wounds emitting faint white smoke.

He walked to the supply crate in the camp center, kicking open the lock. Inside were neatly stacked two hundred tubes of high-concentration synthetic nutrient paste.

Then he walked to those two destroyed spider mechs, bare-handedly tearing open their core compartments, pulling out two oil-stained, blue-glowing core processing chips.

This was Black Rock City's hard currency. This was his entry token to exchange antibiotics for those three hundred refugees.

Minutes later, Su Liang ran over from the leeward slope. She looked at the ground covered with dismembered corpses and mech wreckage, then at Lin Yi standing by the firelight, eyes cold as a true tyrant.

"Three minutes, with ten seconds to spare." Su Liang said softly.

"Take the food." Lin Yi tossed those two chips into his pocket, picked up the cold-weather clothing from the ground and put it back on, looking up toward the wind and snow depths. "We're going back to the tunnel. Then, to take over Black Rock City."
