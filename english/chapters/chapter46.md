# Chapter 46: Throwing Eggs at Stones

## Section 1: Giant Shadow of Steel

The ground tremors grew increasingly violent, causing the damaged building's load-bearing pillars to shed gray cement debris.

Lin Yi tightly covered Su Liang's mouth, pressing both their bodies completely low in the tilted ruin's shadows. His breathing was forcibly slowed to minimum. His gaze pierced through the building's shattered glass curtain wall, looking at that dim yellow sandstorm outside.

The sandstorm was forcibly parted by an extremely massive physical force.

A steel silhouette dozens of meters tall finally revealed its despair-inducing true form in the wasteland's dusk.

It was a land warfare weapon shaped like a giant multi-legged spider. Its main body was assembled from heavy dark composite armor, its surface covered with rough scratches eroded by sandstorms. Eight mechanical legs as thick as crane booms—each rise and fall smashed massive deep craters into the hard wasteland surface.

This wasn't the kind of zombie you could maneuver around with positioning in "Abandoned School," nor the human-sized Arbiter from "Perfect City."

This was a mobile steel mountain range—a "Harvester" dispatched by Ark to the surface specifically to search for organic matter and hunt survivors.

Under its massive head armor, densely packed red electronic eyes alternately flickered like a flock of hungry blood-eyed crows.

The black metal brand on Lin Yi's left palm was now erupting with unprecedented scalding temperature.

[Danger Perception] hadn't failed. On this real wasteland, it was frantically sending the highest-level death warnings to Lin Yi's brain with nerve-tearing pain.

Flee.

This was the most instinctive reaction when biological beings faced absolute power crushing.

But Lin Yi couldn't flee. In this sea of sand without any cover, running while carrying an unconscious burden would only result in being instantly blasted to pulp by the Harvester's ranged weapons.

## Section 2: Red Scanning Net

*Buzz—*

A low electromagnetic surge sound rang out above the ruins.

The Harvester stopped its steps. It seemed to have detected extremely weak heat source reactions in these ruins.

Then, those red electronic eyes on its head suddenly brightened, weaving into a massive, 360-degree red laser scanning net with no blind spots, beginning a carpet scan of this building wreckage.

Red beams penetrated through broken windows like invisible combs, combing through every inch of space.

Lin Yi watched that red light gradually approach the corner where they hid.

No system panel to show his current "stealth value," no countdown telling him how long to hold out. Here, all games returned to the most primitive physical level.

"Cough..."

Just as the red light was about to sweep over their heads, Su Liang, whose mouth Lin Yi tightly covered, let out an extremely weak muffled cough from deep in her throat due to too much dust in her lungs.

Though the sound was as small as a mosquito's buzz, to Lin Yi's ears it was like thunder.

*Whoosh!*

The sky-filling red scanning beams instantly converged in that instant, transforming into a blinding red light column that locked dead onto the load-bearing wall corner where Lin Yi and Su Liang hid!

"Unregistered carbon-based lifeforms detected. Water content meets standards. Execute recovery procedure."

An emotionless electronic synthesized voice, through the Harvester's massive broadcast speaker, exploded over the wasteland, shattering the surrounding remaining glass curtain walls.

## Section 3: Collapse of the Optimal Solution

It didn't fire.

Because for Ark's "wetware processing factory," intact human bodies and brains were extremely precious resources. Blasting them with artillery would be too wasteful.

One of the Harvester's mechanical forelimbs suddenly lifted, mechanical joints emitting deafening friction sounds. Its end was like a blooming steel man-eating flower, ejecting a massive metal net the size of half a basketball court, crackling with blue high-voltage arcs!

That electric net, with air-tearing howls, came crashing down on them!

Caught by this net, high voltage would induce deep paralysis in 0.1 seconds, then they'd be dragged back like pigs to that nutrient solution hell.

Facing this certain-death desperate situation, Lin Yi's brain still frantically operated.

According to the "profit maximization" principle he used to follow in Infinite Trial, the current optimal solution was very simple:

Kick Su Liang out.

Using the few seconds of rigidity while the Harvester captured Su Liang and retracted the net, he could completely rely on [Nirvana] gene burst speed to jump from the building's other side and escape into the sandstorm.

Trade a teammate who lost combat ability for his own life. This deal—he had calculated it clearly in the "Lightless Hospital" elevator.

Lin Yi turned his head, looking at Su Liang in his arms.

Su Liang had also barely opened her eyes. In those originally cool eyes, the sky-falling blue electric net reflected. She looked at Lin Yi. Her gaze held no pleading, only a calm acceptance of death. She even struggled to push away Lin Yi's hand, seemingly hinting he should escape alone.

"Damn it..."

Lin Yi suddenly cursed, voice hoarse as if dripping blood.

If this were two days ago, he would have let go without hesitation.

But in "Prison Zero," he personally endured that monster mother's death grip. He personally pulled the plug on his sister's phantom respirator. He used endless pain to prove to that lofty AI deity—humans aren't machines that only calculate gains and losses.

If he now pushed Su Liang out to survive, everything he'd insisted on through this journey of rebellion would become a joke.

He wouldn't just lose to the system—he'd lose to that once cold, selfish self.

## Section 4: Arrogance of Flesh and Blood

"You haven't repaid the 450-point bad debt yet. Where do you think you're running?"

Lin Yi suddenly bit through his tongue tip. With an extremely rough motion, he grabbed Su Liang's collar and violently threw her entire body into the extremely narrow load-bearing pillar crevice behind them that the electric net absolutely couldn't reach!

"Lin Yi!" Su Liang let out an incredulous cry from the crevice.

Having lost one person's weight, Lin Yi suddenly stood straight.

Violent wind disheveled his hair. That thin white hospital gown on his body flapped loudly in the sandstorm.

He had no gun, no bulletproof vest, no mental barrier that could resist everything.

His only weapon was that thick, sharp bulletproof glass shard knocked off Ark's cultivation chamber.

Watching that high-voltage net crackling with deadly blue light rapidly enlarging on his retinas, the black brand on Lin Yi's left palm erupted with unprecedented dark red light.

The [Nirvana] gene in his body was squeezed to its limit in physical flesh. His entire skeleton emitted overburdened explosive sounds. Every muscle fiber tensed like steel cables about to snap.

He didn't retreat, didn't evade, didn't curl up like a rat waiting for death.

Lin Yi's legs were nailed dead to the cracked concrete floor. His right hand reverse-gripped that sharp glass shard, suddenly swinging upward.

Even if this was truly throwing eggs at stones.

Even if the next second he'd be burned to charcoal by high voltage.

He would still use this mortal flesh to show this steel giant beast representing Ark's absolute power humanity's most arrogant fangs!

"Come on!!"

Lin Yi threw his head back, facing the sky-filling electric light, letting out a lone wolf's defiant roar.

Just as the electric net was less than half a meter from his face, when the violent blue arcs had already singed his forehead hair—

*Whoosh—!!*

An extremely sharp sound completely unlike this mechanical beast suddenly howled from the extreme heights of the building ruins!
