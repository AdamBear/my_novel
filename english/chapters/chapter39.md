# Chapter 39: Heart-Destroying Trap

## Section 1: Tranquility of Dimensional Reduction

The physical collapse lasted only an instant. When tens of thousands of awakened monsters crashed into that silver-white erasure curtain with their flesh and blood, the entire containment area erupted with supernova-like intense light. Lin Yi's retinas were instantly deprived of vision. Then, the deafening roars, mechanical shattering sounds, and howling winds all stopped abruptly, as if the air had been drained away. The world fell into absolute silence. Lin Yi felt the metal grid beneath his feet disappear, and the agonizing pain of ten thousand ghosts devouring his heart from the [Pain Network] backlash receded like a tide.

He slowly opened his eyes.

No dark Prison Zero, no mountains of garbage and corpses. He was standing in a corridor paved with white tiles. Bright fluorescent lights overhead, the air filled with a pungent smell of formalin mixed with Lysol.

"Lin Yi's family member, please come to the nurse's station."

From the speaker at the end of the corridor came a nurse's announcement, gentle and without inflection.

Lin Yi's muscles instantly stiffened. His breathing froze. Those eyes bloodshot from excessive killing stared fixedly at a half-open wooden door on the right side of the corridor. The door plate read: ICU-03. This was the place where, at nineteen in the real world, he had paced countless times and collapsed in despair countless times.

He slowly walked over and pushed open the door.

On the pale hospital bed lay an emaciated girl. She wore a respirator, her body covered with transparent IV tubes. The vital signs monitor beside her emitted a monotonous "beep... beep..." sound.

"Brother..."

The girl seemed to sense someone approaching. She struggled to open her eyes and, through the respirator mask, let out an extremely weak call. Lin Yi's hands suddenly trembled. The [Fragment of Reality] gripped tightly in his palm actually lost its cold physical sensation at this moment.

## Section 2: Devil's Deal

"Your heart rate is soaring, cortisol secretion exceeds lethal levels, but your dopamine is compensating in an illogical way. The human body is truly a container full of contradictions."

In the shadows of the hospital room corner stood a doctor in a white coat, appearing from nowhere. He had no face. His facial area was a silver-white geometric luminous body constantly shifting between a regular tetrahedron and octahedron—the Warden.

It didn't suppress Lin Yi with force. When physical processing power overloaded due to the riot of tens of thousands of monsters, this antivirus program representing absolute rationality directly pulled Lin Yi into this high-dimensional logic space constructed by reading his bottom-layer memories.

"An illusion..." Lin Yi gritted his teeth, staring at the white coat. "You think this cheap trick can stop me?"

"This is not an illusion."

The Warden's voice rang directly in Lin Yi's mind, carrying a cold sense of truth. "This is an 'independent calculation zone' specially created for you based on the Ark mainframe's processing power. Here, her consciousness data has been perfectly reconstructed. She will hurt, laugh, and have real memories."

The Warden walked to the bedside, looking at the fragile girl.

"Lin Yi, I cannot understand your behavior. You abandoned your kind on the train, you killed with borrowed knives in the hospital. Your bottom-layer logic is the same as ours—seeking optimal solutions for survival. You are clearly the most perfect 'wetware,' so why oppose the entire system for those worthless garbage data?"

"Because you not only devour people, you destroy their hearts!" Lin Yi roared.

"No, we are optimizing."

The Warden turned its head, that geometric glow gazing at Lin Yi. "Now, I give you an optimal solution. Hand over that virus fragment in your hand that causes logic overflow. Give up resistance. In exchange, I will seal this Room 03. The system will forever provide processing power support for her. In this world, she will recover, grow up, and stay with you forever. Isn't this the miracle you most desired at nineteen?"

## Section 3: Peeling Apples

The hospital room was so quiet only the respirator's rhythm could be heard.

"Brother... I hurt so much..." A real tear slid from the corner of the girl's eye on the bed. "Stay with me, okay?"

That voice, those eyes, the temperature of that tear—every detail frantically tore at Lin Yi's soul.

He was so tired. Since entering the trials, he had been like a taut string, bearing the burden of killing, bearing the sin of abandoning his kind, bearing the pain of ten thousand ghosts devouring his heart. Just nod, just let go. Hell would become heaven.

Lin Yi lowered his head, his bangs covering his eyes. He slowly walked to the bedside and pulled over a chair to sit down. He placed that [Fragment of Reality] representing rebellion on the bedside table.

Seeing this, the Warden's data-composed body flickered slightly, as if confirming the success of the logic loop.

Lin Yi didn't look at the Warden. He picked up a bright red apple from the fruit bowl on the bedside table, then picked up a fruit knife. He lowered his head and began peeling the apple with extremely skilled movements. The peel curled under the blade, spiraling down in one continuous strip.

"Do you remember, when you were sick as a child, I always peeled apples for you at your bedside." Lin Yi's voice was light and gentle, like casual conversation. "I always thought that if the apple peel didn't break, your illness would get better."

The girl on the bed weakly curved her eyes and smiled through the oxygen mask.

"I remember... Brother's apples were the sweetest."

"Yes. The sweetest."

Lin Yi finished the last cut. The long peel hung down without breaking. He placed the peeled apple by the girl's pillow, then extended his hand covered with blue veins and scars, gently holding the girl's cold, thin fingers.

"But you forgot one thing."

## Section 4: The Second Time Pulling the Plug

Lin Yi slowly raised his head. His eye sockets were blood red, two streams of tears silently sliding down his cheeks, but the look in his eyes carried a tragic madness that even AI couldn't look directly at.

"If my sister were really here, she would never let me surrender."

"She would say: 'Brother, pull it out for me. I don't want you to become a monster for my sake.'"

The geometric glow on the Warden's body suddenly flickered violently.

【Warning: Extreme logic paradox detected! Target emotional index breakthrough threshold!】

"I already killed her once in reality with my own hands, because of poverty and powerlessness."

Lin Yi stood up, both hands gripping the respirator plug tightly. His voice became hoarse and distorted from extreme pain and hatred. "I carry the lives of those I got killed, I endure the pain of tens of thousands of monsters, I've come this far... How could I possibly allow you scrap metal to use her face to deceive me a second time?!"

"Stop! You're destroying the only variable!" The Warden emitted a piercing electronic screech.

"Fuck your variable!"

Lin Yi threw his head back, letting out a hysterical roar, both hands exerting force violently!

*Click.*

The respirator plug was forcibly pulled out.

"Beep————"

On the vital signs monitor, the jumping curve instantly flattened into a glaring, long straight line. The girl on the bed turned into scattered green garbled code.

In that instant, Lin Yi personally tore to shreds the softest, most desired dream in his heart. That soul agony transcending physical limits, that rebellious will desperate to the extreme, finally transformed into a blazing fire, completely igniting that ancient gene sleeping deep in his spine.

*Boom—!!!*

A roar like a great bell erupted from within Lin Yi's body.

【Warning! Warning! Warning!】
【Host mental endurance detected breaking through physical limits!】
【Gene [Nirvana]... entering rampage state!】

The entire white ICU room shattered like a smashed glass box, instantly collapsing and fragmenting. The blinding white light receded, darkness and violent winds poured back in.

Lin Yi returned to the void of Prison Zero. His eyes burned with materialized crimson light. His left hand grabbed the [Fragment of Reality]. The originally pitch-black fragment now, under his boiling genetic resonance, seemed to become a high-heat light sword cutting through dimensions. He looked up at the silver-white Warden in the sky, frozen from processing overload.

"Now, let me teach you what truly illogical means!"
